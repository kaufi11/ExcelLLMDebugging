High-level summary
- Workbook models DRAM/LEM semiconductor supply, demand, pricing and project ROI (new fabs / upgrades) across years 2001–2006. Columns are years; rows are technology vintages / die sizes / cost / output lines. Key outputs: price per chip/functionality, economic capacity, delta revenues/costs and IRRs for different build/upgrade scenarios.

Sheets (purpose + key contents / formulas)

1) DRAM cost side
- Purpose: a compact DRAM-specific cost/profit and cash‑flow model for entrant vs incumbent analysis.
- Inputs: industry wafer starts (C4:H4 = 1000 K/month), discount factor for announced vs effective capacity (F7 = 0.6), overhead SG&A (C15 = 0.36), var fab cost/wafer (E15 ≈ 964.706), A&T cost/yielded die (H15 = 1).
- Calculations:
  - Convert monthly→annual starts (row 6 = 12*C4), effective starts = F7*C6 (row 8).
  - Baseline output (mil units) = effective starts * avg yielded die * yield / 1000 (row 11).
  - Baseline price P from fisher sheet: P = (Q - a)/-b references fisher!D49.. etc (rows 13, 31 use fisher! cells).
  - Variable costs per wafer/die include overhead multiplication (D16/D17) and cap cost per start (D19→D18).
  - Profit per wafer, per start amortized over 5 years, total cash flows for a 20K fab and IRR via IRR(range,0.15) (rows 21–41).
- Scenarios: entrant (competitive) and Cournot incumbent cases; net cash flows combine new‑fab flows with price impact on preexisting fabs.

2) LEM cost side
- Purpose: detailed cost model across nodes and die sizes for LEM (logic/epitaxy/memory?) used by Capacity&Price and RoI calculations.
- Structure: sections for capacity (D5:D15: wafer start equivalents by vintage), utilization (D16=0.925), effective wafer starts/year (D18..), yields, ultimate yields and throughput learning (rows 30–73).
- Key computed series:
  - Unyielded dice/wafer (rows 52–61) via formula (pi*r^2 / die_area).
  - Effective yielded die/wafer = throughput learning * unyielded dice * node factor (rows 64, 75–85).
  - Variable cost per wafer and per yielded die (rows 88–97 and 100–109): variable wafer costs (D88..D97), then per‑die = wafer cost / effective yielded die.
  - New equipment and upgrade capital per fab and per K‑starts (rows 110–185 etc), plus building cap and upgrade cap (rows 229–249, 240..247).
- Many parameters are filled for different vintages/node sizes; node factors and learning curves are given numerically.

3) LEM Demand Calibration
- Purpose: calibrates demand (functionality and price indices) using Semico forecast and fisher indexes.
- Inputs: semico revenue & units time series (C3:C4 etc).
- Uses fisher!P index outputs: DRAM qual‑adjusted P index pulled from fisher!C40.. (row 6).
- Computes functionality (dollars per functionality unit) and functionality per chip (rows 9–10), assumed price elasticity (A13 = -1.5), sets up linear demand curves Q = a - bP (rows 14–16) where slope b = -elasticity * (P/Q).

4) Capacity&Price Calculation
- Purpose: take LEM cost side capacity/yield outputs + demand calibration to compute baseline and scenario capacities, prices and “economic” capacity.
- Pulls LEM cost side totals (C3:C12 = D5..D14) then computes baseline units produced (rows 15–24) using LEM cost side effective yielded die (e.g., 'LEM cost side'!D76).
- Functionality produced = baseline units * functionality per chip (from LEM Demand Calibration!C10) (rows 26–36).
- Builds inverse demand P = (Q - a)/-b using LEM Demand Calibration parameters (rows 38–40).
- Computes var cost/chip = LEM cost side variable cost per die + assumed A&T cost (C41 = 0.7) (rows 42–52).
- Economic capacity: IF(var cost/chip > price/chip → set capacity 0) per vintage/node (rows 54–65). Retirement‑eligible capacity = total - economic (rows 67–78).
- Computes additional output and price impacts for adding a 20K fab and for upgrades; produces price/chip with extra or upgraded fab (rows 80–170).

5) Static RoI calc '02
- Purpose: consolidates costs, delta revenues and variable costs into cash flows and computes IRR for new builds and upgrades under competitive (entrant) and Cournot incumbent assumptions.
- Inputs from LEM cost side (cap, equip, building caps), Capacity&Price (prices/volumes) and LEM Demand Calibration.
- Competitive entrant case: computes K costs (bldg & equip), variable costs (E26..I27 etc), revenues (E37..I38), net cash flows per year (rows 47–71) and IRR = IRR(C71:H71,0.15) (B73).
- Cournot/incumbent: includes price effects on existing capacity (assumed market share N2 = 0.15). Net cash flows combine new fab returns with lost/gained cash flows at incumbents; IRRs computed via IRR(C83:H83,0.15) (B84).
- Upgrade1/2 sections compute alternative cost structures where upgrade parameters differ and produce delta revenues/var costs and net cash flows with IRR outputs (rows 70–131 etc).

6) fisher (price index and demand decomposition)
- Purpose: builds Laspeyres/Paasche/Fisher price indices from unit mix and ASPs, derives chained Fisher index and functionality measures.
- Inputs: unit forecasts by chip density (64M..1G) and ASPs by density.
- Calculates total 256M‑equivalent units, ASP per 256M equiv, P indices (Laspeyres, Paasche, fisher) and chained fisher series with base C40 = 1.
- Provides functionality ($ per function) and demand curve constants (rows 48–50). Many references from other sheets use fisher!C40..H40 as price index anchors.

7) $ per bit (duplicate / alternate price index)
- Similar to fisher sheet but uses a direct $/bit approach to compute indices and functionality. Mirrors the same structure and formulas (SUMPRODUCT, indices, ASP/256M equiv).

Key constants & numbers to note
- Years covered: 2001–2006 in columns C:H (consistent across sheets).
- Base/fab sizing: 20K wafer starts per month per fab (constant used in many calculations).
- Discount factor for announced vs effective capacity: 0.6 (DRAM cost side & other places).
- Overhead SG&A: 36% (C15 / C47).
- Var fab cost/wafer (example): 964.7058823529411 (used in DRAM and fisher copies).
- Assumed A&T cost/yielded chip for Capacity&Price: 0.7 (C41).
- Cap cost per start/year excl ovhd (D51 in multiple places): 5367 (used to compute per-start cap costs).
- IRR calls use initial guess 0.15 across scenarios: IRR(range,0.15).

Overall flow / linkage
- fisher computes price indices and functionality per chip.
- LEM Demand Calibration uses fisher indices and Semico forecasts to create demand curves (a and b).
- LEM cost side provides detailed cost, yields, equipment and building cap numbers by vintage/node; also effective yielded dice via throughput learning.
- Capacity&Price pulls costs + demand to compute baseline production, economic capacity, retirement eligible capacity, and prices under extra/new/upgrade fab scenarios.
- Static RoI calc uses Capacity&Price and LEM cost side numbers to produce cash flows and IRR for new builds (entrant) and incumbent (Cournot) cases and for upgrades.

Main formula types used
- Aggregations: SUM, SUMPRODUCT, SUM(Dx:Hx).
- Price/demand: linear inverse demand P=(Q - a)/-b.
- Indexing: chained Fisher (SQRT(Laspeyres*Paasche)), SUMPRODUCT for Laspeyres.
- Cost per die: wafer cost / effective yielded die.
- Conditional economic capacity: IF(var cost/chip > price/chip,0,capacity).
- IRR: IRR(range,0.15) to evaluate projects.
