2) Variance column (column E) uses inconsistent subtraction order
- Problem: The Variance column is intended to be Actual minus Forecast (A - C), but many E formulas are =C - A (the wrong sign). Example cells shown in your sheet that are reversed: E7, E9, E11:E41, E43:E49 (many of the E# formulas you listed are C - A). A few E cells are correct (=A - C) but most are reversed. This produces upside-down variances and will misstate negative/positive variances.
- Fix: Standardize all variance formulas in column E to be =A(row) - C(row).  
  - Quick way: In E6 (or the top variance cell), enter =A6 - C6 and then fill/download (drag) that formula down through the variance range (E6:E53 or wherever your variance rows stop).  
- Why: Variance = Actual − Forecast (so positive means Actual > Forecast). Using C - A flips the sign and makes the variance meaning incorrect.
