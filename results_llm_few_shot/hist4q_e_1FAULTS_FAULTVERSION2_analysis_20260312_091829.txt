Summary of the error
- Faulty cell: Sheet1!H12
- Problem: Formula uses COUNT(H9:H11) instead of SUM(H9:H11).
- Result: H12 returns 3 (the number of numeric cells) rather than the sum of the three quarter amounts. That wrong value propagates to every H-column total/ratio that depends on H12 (H15, H17, H19, H22, H23, H28, H29, H30, H33, H35, H36, H38, H39, etc.), producing incorrect totals and percentages for the 2001 2nd-quarter column.

Fix
- Replace the formula in H12 with:
  =SUM(H9:H11)

Why this fixes it (short)
- COUNT counts numeric cells (returns 3). SUM adds their values. Changing COUNT → SUM produces the correct business-machines total for that column and corrects all dependent calculations.

Quick check
- After fixing H12, verify H15 (Total of net sales), H19 (Gross profit) and H22 (Operating profit) values in column H — they should change from the small integer-based outputs to numbers in the same magnitude as other quarters.
