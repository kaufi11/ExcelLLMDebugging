Summary of the error
- Row 25 (BEACON FEDERAL, a Thrift) has F25 = 1.21674489270828 * $E25. That is wrong. Thrift institutions are being weighted at 0.5 * deposits in this sheet (see other thrifts). The incorrect multiplier inflates F25, which makes F36 (weighted total), all market shares (G10:G33) and the HHI (H10:H36) incorrect.

Why it matters
- F36 (the denominator used to compute market shares) includes the bad F25. That shifts every G% and every H value, so the whole market-share and HHI results are wrong.

Quick fix (one-step)
- Replace F25 with: =0.5*$E25
  - After that Excel will recalc F36, all G% cells (=F#/ $F$36*100) and HHI values correctly.

Better, safer solution (prevent future errors)
- Replace the hard-coded F formulas for the whole range with a rule that uses the Type column, e.g. put this in F10 and copy down to F33:
  =IF($C10="Thrift",0.5*$E10,1*$E10)
  or shorter:
  =IF($C10="Thrift",$E10*0.5,$E10)
- Benefits: no manual multiplier mistakes; if Type changes the weighting updates automatically.

Check after fix
- Verify F36 changed (downwards), then confirm each G cell =F#/$F$36*100 and H =G#^2. H36 should be the correct HHI total.
