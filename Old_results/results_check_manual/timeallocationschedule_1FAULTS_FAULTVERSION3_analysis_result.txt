1) NETWORKDAYS vs textual timestamps (J4)
- Formula: J4 =NETWORKDAYS(C4,F4)
- Cell values: C4 = "2004-08-01T00:00:00", F4 = "2004-09-30T00:00:00" (strings in that form)
- Problem: Excel does not always recognise ISO strings with the "T" as date serials. If C4/F4 are text, NETWORKDAYS may coerce them (locale-dependent) or return #VALUE / wrong result in other environments.
- Fix: Put real Excel dates (date serials) into C4 and F4 (e.g. 2004-08-01 formatted as a date) or convert the strings to dates before using NETWORKDAYS (e.g. remove the "T00:00:00" or use DATEVALUE/ SUBSTITUTE to convert).
