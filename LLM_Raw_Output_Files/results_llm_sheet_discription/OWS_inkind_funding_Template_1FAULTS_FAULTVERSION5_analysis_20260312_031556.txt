Findings (short)
- Bug: In the "In-kind contribution template" sheet cell F5 contains =E5*E5 instead of =D5*E5. That multiplies hours by hours (100*100 = 10,000) instead of rate × hours (50*100 = 5,000).
- Impact: Labor total and every downstream total on that sheet are inflated by $5,000.

How to fix
1. Edit F5 and change the formula to:
   =D5*E5
2. (Optional) Fill/copy that correct formula down through the labor rows (F5:F15) to ensure consistency.

What changes after the fix (with the example data)
- Before fix (wrong F5): Labor = $14,500; Travel = $9,000; Hardware/Software/Data = $65,100; Grand total = $88,600.
- After fix (correct F5): Labor = $9,500; Travel = $9,000; Hardware/Software/Data = $65,100; Grand total = $83,600.
- The sheet was over-reporting by $5,000 (100*100 − 50*100 = 5,000).

Quick recommendations
- Audit similar per-row formulas to ensure they are all =D#*E# (or consistent D×E).
- Consider converting the ranges to an Excel Table or protecting formula cells so accidental edits (like E*E) are less likely.
- If you expand rows, update SUM ranges (F5:F15, F20:F28, F33:F40) or use dynamic ranges/tables so totals auto-extend.
