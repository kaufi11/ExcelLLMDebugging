Findings (short)
- Error: B20 (total "would like to attend") uses =SUM(B12:B18) and therefore omits row 19 (MPS = 12). That makes B20 = 54 while the Carnegie total E30 = 66 and the narrative say 66 interested.
- Cause: the SUM range was not updated to include the last row (MPS). This is a range‑omission error.

Fix (one-step)
- Change B20 to =SUM(B12:B19)
- Result: B20 becomes 66 and matches E30 and the narrative.

Why this fixes it (simple)
- Row 19 contains 12 "would like to attend" responses (MPS). Including B19 adds those 12 to the total, reconciling the Directorate total with the Carnegie total and the written summary.

Recommendations to avoid recurrence (brief)
- Use identical ranges for parallel summary tables (e.g., always B12:B19 and E23:E29 are equivalent aggregates).
- Better: convert the summary rows to an Excel Table or use named ranges so formulas auto‑expand when you add rows.
- Even more robust: derive counts from the Participant List with formulas such as =COUNTIF('Participant List'!F:F,"MPS") or SUMPRODUCT to compute "would like to attend" from source data rather than hand‑entered cells.
