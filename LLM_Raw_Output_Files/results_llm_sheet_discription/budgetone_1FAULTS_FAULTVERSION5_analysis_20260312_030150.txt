Findings (short)
- Error: Cell D7 contains a typo: D7 = D5 * 615. It should be D5 * D4 (ASP × Units for 3rd quarter).
- Cause: Hard‑coded constant (615) instead of referencing the Units cell for Q3. This lowers Q3 revenue and cascades through Gross Margin, Operating Income, Taxes, Net Income and the Year totals/percent column.

Fix (how to correct)
1. Edit D7 and replace the formula with:
   =D5*D4
2. Press Enter. (Or copy B7 and paste to D7 to preserve consistent formula pattern.)

Why this fixes it
- Revenue per quarter is ASP × Units. Replacing the constant with the Units cell makes D7 compute Q3 sales correctly and lets the F‑column sums and all downstream formulas recalculate correctly.

Corrected key totals (after fix)
- F7 Sales total: 153,000
- F8 COGS total: 62,000
- F9 Gross margin total: 91,000
- F14 Total operating costs: 15,840
- F15 Operating income: 75,160
- F16 Taxes: 20,544.80
- F17 Net income: 54,615.20

What happens if not fixed (for comparison)
- D7 = 22 * 615 = 13,530 → F7 becomes 122,530 (sales), and all downstream totals are lower (you already listed these alternate results).

Recommendation
- Scan sheet for other hard‑coded numbers in formulas (look for unexpected numeric literals like 615). To avoid mistakes, build quarter formulas once (e.g., B7 = B5*B4) and fill/copy across.
