Summary of errors
1) L29 is wrong: it shows FORMULA =COUNT(B29:K29) instead of =SUM(...). That makes L29 a small integer (number of scores) and breaks the total and average for row 29 and the summary rows that depend on it.

2) Column M (Average) uses a fixed divisor of 10 (Mx = Lx/10). Many rows have missing homework cells, so dividing the sum by 10 gives a wrong average when fewer than 10 scores exist.

3) Because of (1) and (2), the aggregate averages in L47 and M47 will also be incorrect.

Quick fixes (exact formulas to use)
- Fix row 29 total:
  L29 = SUM(B29:K29)

- Replace the current M formulas (M3:M45) with either:
  Option A (best — simple): M3 = AVERAGE(B3:K3) and fill down to M45.
  Option B (if you want to show blank when there are no scores): M3 = IF(COUNT(B3:K3)=0,"", L3/COUNT(B3:K3)) and fill down.

Why these fixes work (short)
- SUM(Bx:Kx) correctly adds all numeric homework scores; COUNT(Bx:Kx) only counts how many numeric scores there are.
- AVERAGE(Bx:Kx) computes the mean of the present scores and ignores blanks automatically. Using Lx/10 assumes there are always 10 scores — that is not true here and yields incorrect averages.
- Fixing L29 removes a bad value that propagates into the per-row and summary averages.

Extra tip
- After fixing formulas, re-check rows with missing entries to confirm whether they should be blank or zero. If a missing score should be treated as 0, enter 0 (or change COUNT to COUNTIF(...,">="&0) logic) — otherwise keep them blank so AVERAGE ignores them.
