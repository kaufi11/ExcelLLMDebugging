**Explanation of the Solution:**

Inventory errors affect cost of goods sold and net income. If errors from previous years are not corrected in retained earnings, the balance sheet will not balance because assets (inventory) and equity (retained earnings) are mismatched.

By adjusting retained earnings for prior inventory errors (adding the error if inventory was understated, subtracting if overstated), the equity side reflects the true accumulated earnings, balancing the accounting equation:

**Assets = Liabilities + Equity**

This is demonstrated in **Sheet3**, where retained earnings include prior year error adjustments, resulting in a balanced sheet.

- Problem: Inventory errors not adjusted in retained earnings → imbalance.
