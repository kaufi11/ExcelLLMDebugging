Sheet name and purpose
- Sheet: "Table 22" — Percentage Distribution of Fall Term Undergraduate Grades by College, Grade Category, Level and Year (title in A1:A2).

Layout / structure
- Header: Row 3–4 split into two grade‑level blocks: "Lower Level" (columns E–O) and "Upper Level" (columns S–AF). Column A = College, column C = Year.
- Lower‑level grade columns: E = A, G = B, I = C, K = D, M = F/WF, O = Other (residual), Q = Total Number of Grades (count for lower level).
- Upper‑level grade columns: S = A, U = B, W = C, Y = D, AA = F/WF, AC = Other (residual), AF = Total Number of Grades (count for upper level).
- Each college block starts in column A (college name) with multiple year rows (C = year) containing percentage values in the grade columns and counts in Q and AF. Bottom section (A29+) contains TOTAL UNIVERSITY rows for 2000–1998 and a source line.

Key formulas and logic
- "Other" columns (O and AC) use a residual formula of the form =SUM(100-(E#+G#+I#+K#+M#)) or =SUM(100-(S# + U# + W# + Y# + AA#)) to make grade percentages sum to 100. Note: SUM() is superfluous but benign in most cases.
- Totals for the University across colleges:
  - Lower-level total number of grades for 2000 (Q30) = Q26+Q22+Q18+Q14+Q10+Q6
  - Upper-level total number of grades for 2000 (AF30) = AF26+AF22+AF18+AF14+AF10+AF6
  - Similar formulas for 1999 (Q31/AF31) and 1998 (Q32/AF32).
- Observed computed totals (based on the provided cell values):
  - 2000: Q30 = 47,993 (lower‑level total grades), AF30 = 57,857 (upper‑level total grades).
  - 1999: Q31 = 44,792; AF31 = 53,280.
  - 1998: Q32 = 40,676; AF32 = 51,470 (these are provided in the sheet).

Notable data points (examples)
- TOTAL UNIVERSITY (2000) percentages:
  - Lower level: A = 30.7%, B = 29.3%, C = 17.8%, D = 5.3%, F/WF = 6.6%, Other = computed residual.
  - Upper level: A = 36.6%, B = 31.3%, C = 16.0%, D = 3.9%, F/WF = 4.8%, Other = computed residual.
- Per‑college examples: Arts & Sciences (2000 lower A = 28.2%, B = 30.4%, total lower grades Q6 = 36,361? — actually Q6 = 36,361 as given; upper AF6 = 18,872).

Inconsistencies / issues to watch
- A few rows show misaligned or extra entries in intermediary columns (e.g., Education row contains values in F18 and H18 which are outside the declared grade columns); check column alignment for those rows.
- Hospitality (1999 and 1998) entries are "n/appl" — missing percentages.
- One formula anomaly: O14 is listed as =SUM(100-(E14-G14-I14-K14-M14)) which uses subtraction of the other columns rather than summing them; that will produce incorrect "Other" for that row. Review similar formulas for correctness and consistent use of parentheses.
- Some columns (e.g., R) are blank/unused; verify there are no hidden columns or offsets.

Overall summary
- The sheet lists grade percentage distributions by college, year, and level (lower vs upper). Percent columns populate A–F categories, with "Other" computed as the residual to total 100. Two count columns (Q for lower, AF for upper) give numbers of grades per row; university totals at the bottom sum college counts. Review the noted formula anomaly and alignment/missing data for Hospitality and Education rows.
