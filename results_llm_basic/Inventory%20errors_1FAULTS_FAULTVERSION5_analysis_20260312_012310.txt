Short answer — what's wrong
- The balance sheet is out of balance because prior-year inventory misstatements were not closed to retained earnings. Retained earnings cells are being set only to the current year's net income (e.g. D33 = E15, F33 = G15), so the prior-period inventory errors (17,000 for 2002; 25,000 for 2003) never get reflected in equity. The result: Assets ≠ Liabilities + Equity by the error amounts.

Why that happens (simple)
- An inventory understatement/overstatement in one year changes that year’s profit. When that year is closed, the profit (or the correction) should be included in retained earnings in the next period. The sheet instead ignores those prior-period corrections, so Inventory (an asset) and Retained Earnings (equity) are out of sync.

How to fix it (two options)
1) Accounting-correct way (recommended)
   - Adjust beginning retained earnings for the prior-year inventory error (i.e. close the prior-year inventory correction to retained earnings).
   - In your workbook that means: add the prior-period inventory correction to retained earnings for the next year.

   Exact quick fix in your file (cells in Sheet1/Sheet2 layout):
   - For 2002 retained earnings (column E): change D33 from =E15 to =E15 + 17000
   - For 2003 retained earnings (column G): change F33 from =G15 to =G15 - 25000

   (Sheet3 already shows this approach: D35 = E17+17000 and F35 = G17-25000 — that makes the balance sheet balance.)

2) Alternative: correct the inventory balances in the year they belong
   - Fix the ending inventory amounts in the year the error occurred (e.g. change B11, D11 etc. to their correct values). That will change COGS and net income accordingly and eliminate the need for an adjusting entry to retained earnings.

Why these fixes work (short)
- Adjusting retained earnings moves the prior-year profit/ loss effect of the inventory error into equity, restoring the accounting equation Assets = Liabilities + Equity. Correcting the inventory in the original year does the same by producing the correct prior-year profit.
