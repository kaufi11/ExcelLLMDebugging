Summary — the main error
- Cell C92 ("Other operating costs") uses =SUM(C93:C97) and therefore omits C98 (Travelling expenses R639,779).  
- Result: total expenditure (C100) is understated by R639,779, surplus (C102) and accumulated funds (C29 / C330) are overstated, and the balance sheet does not balance (Assets ≠ Equity + Liabilities).

Why this breaks the file (short)
- Omitting C98 makes Expenditure too small → Surplus too large → Accumulated funds and Capital & Reserves too large → Total Equity & Liabilities > Total Assets. That single range error propagates through many totals and the cashflow reconciliation.

Fix (exact steps)
1. Change C92 from:
   =SUM(C93:C97)
   to:
   =SUM(C93:C98)
   (also verify E92 is =SUM(E93:E98) — it already is.)

2. After step 1 Excel will automatically recalc dependent totals: C100, C102, C327, C330, C29, C28 and C42. No manual arithmetic required.

3. Reconcile the cash-from-operations cell: make C122 equal the calculated operations result (C511) to avoid inconsistent duplicates. Set
   C122 = C511
   (or check which is intended and link the other to it).

What the correction does (numbers after correction)
- C92 increases by R639,779 (adds Travelling R639,779).  
- C100 (Total expenditure) becomes R8,590,540.  
- Surplus C102 reduces from an overstated R675,124 to R35,345 (C74 − C100).
