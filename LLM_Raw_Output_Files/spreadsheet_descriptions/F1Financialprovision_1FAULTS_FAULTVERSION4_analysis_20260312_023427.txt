Summary of workbook structure and contents

Sheets:
1) Contact details
- Purpose: capture authority contact and submission instruction.
- Key cells:
  - B2: Name of Local Authority (input)
  - B3: Contact name (input)
  - B4: Telephone number (input)
  - B9: Submission e‑mail: SWStat@scotland.gsi.gov.uk

2) A: Financial Provision
- Purpose: capture cash/voucher financial assistance paid in year 1 Apr 2002–31 Mar 2003 under Sections 12 (adults) and 22/25 (children).
- Layout:
  - Title in A1 and instructions in A3.
  - Table 1 (Payments under Section 12)
    - Column headers: A6–D7 (Details; Number of Payments; Grant/Loan amount; Recovered amount).
    - Categories in A8–A13: Energy, Rent, Travel, Food, Asylum Seekers, Other.
    - Totals row A14 with formulas:
      - B14 = SUM(B8:B13)
      - C14 = SUM(C8:C13)
      - D14 = SUM(D8:D13)
  - Table 2 (Payments under Sections 22 and 25)
    - Instruction in A16; title in A18.
    - Column headers: B19 and A20–E21 (Number of Payments; Details; Amount of Grant/Loan; Amount Recovered; columns for Section 22 (B21) and Section 25 (C21) plus D21/E21 for amounts).
    - Categories in A22–A28: Energy, Rent, Travel, Food, Day Care for Children in need, Asylum Seekers, Other.
    - Totals row A29 with formulas:
      - B29 = SUM(B22:B28)
      - C29 = SUM(C22:C28)
      - D29 = SUM(D22:D28)
      - E29 = SUM(E22:E28)

- Notes: all category rows expect numeric entries for counts and amounts; totals use SUM ranges as above.

3) B: Direct Payments
- Purpose: record direct payments made under Section 12B (1 Apr 2002–31 Mar 2003).
- Layout:
  - Title in A1, description in A3–A5.
  - Adult Services table header rows A7–A9.
    - Age columns for counts: B (16–17), C (18–64), D (65+), E = row sum (16–17 to 65+) via E10:E14 formulas.
    - Value columns for payments: F (16–17), G (18–64), H (65+), I = value total via I10:I14 formulas.
  - Client groups rows A10–A14:
    - A10: People with Physical Disabilities
    - A11: People with Learning Disabilities
    - A12: People with Mental Health Problems
    - A13: Disabled Parents *
    - A14: Other (specify below)
  - Formulas:
    - E10..E14 = SUM(Bx:Dx) (sums counts by row)
    - I10..I14 = SUM(Fx:Hx) except:
      - I11 is explicitly set to 3 (fixed value) — notable anomaly.
    - Row 15 totals:
      - B15 = SUM(B10:B14)
      - C15 = SUM(C10:C14)
      - D15 = SUM(D10:D14)
      - E15 = SUM(E10:E14)
      - F15 = SUM(F10:F14)
      - G15 = SUM(G10:G14)
      - H15 = SUM(H10:H14)
      - I15 = SUM(I10:I14)
  - Notes in A16–A17 about guidance and specification for “Other”.

General notes and key formulas
- The workbook is organized as forms for data entry: contact info, two financial provision tables, and a direct payments table.
- Totals throughout use simple SUM(range) formulas as documented for each totals row.
- Check the hard-coded I11 = 3 (should it be SUM(F11:H11)?). Otherwise totals are consistent with SUM formulas.
