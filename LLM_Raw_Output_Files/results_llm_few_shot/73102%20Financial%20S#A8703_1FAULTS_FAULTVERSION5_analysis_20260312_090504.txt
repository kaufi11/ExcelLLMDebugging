Summary of the problem
- The sheet has two different "property & equipment" subtotals: row 30 (D30/C30/E30) and row 162 (D162/C162/E162). The detailed fixed-asset lines are in rows 155–161, but many higher-level formulas (Net property D34, Total current assets/Total assets flows) reference row 30. Row 30 is summing the wrong (empty/outdated) range (D24:D29), so Net property (D34) and the asset totals are wrong and the balance (Assets vs Liabilities & Capital) will not reconcile.

Faulty cells (examples)
- D30, C30, E30 — "Total property and equipment" use =SUM(D24:D29) etc. Those rows are not where the detailed asset amounts live.
- D34, C34, E34 — "Net property and equipment" use =D30+D32 (or =E30+E32, etc). Because D30 is wrong, D34 is wrong.
- This propagates to Total Assets and the sheet-level reconciliation (D178 / D283 / H291).

Why this is wrong (short)
- The detailed asset amounts are in rows 155–161 and their subtotal is D162. Row 30 is summing a different (older/empty) range, so the sheet is mixing two different groupings and under/over‑counts assets.

Quick fixes (pick one)

1. Change D30 to =SUM(D155:D161) (and C30 =SUM(C155:C161), E30 =SUM(E155:E161)).
2. Keep D34 = D30 + D32 (and C34/E34 unchanged). This restores the intended flow: gross PPE (row 30) → less accumulated depreciation (row 32) → net PPE (row 34).

Option B — fix the net formula (minimal change)
1. Change D34 to =D162 + D32 (and C34 = C162 + C32, E34 = E162 + E32).
This corrects net PPE without changing row 30. Use this only if you prefer not to change the row-30 formula.
