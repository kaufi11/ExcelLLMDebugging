High-level summary — purpose & layout
- Purpose: 12-month monthly cash flow projection with a Year 2 continuation and variance analysis by quarter.  
- Layout common to main sheets: months in columns C:N (1–12), O = year TOTAL (SUM of C:N), P = % of sales (O / O$57). Column B often holds labels/positions. Sections run top-to-bottom: 1) Cash on hand (beginning), 2) Cash receipts (a–f), 3) Total cash receipts, 4) Total cash available, 5) Cash paid out (detailed variable & fixed expense lines), 6) Total cash paid out, 7) Cash position end of month, then Essential operating data and a few metrics (contribution margin, break-even).

Sheets
1) Year 1
- Core projection sheet. Rows C6:N55 contain month-by-month values and formulas. O‑column totals are SUM(C:N) for each line (e.g., O8 = SUM(C8:N8)).  
- Cash on hand beginning formulas B6→N6 reference the previous month's "end of month" row (B6 uses SUM(B55), C6 uses SUM(C55), etc.). Note: row E55 is hard-coded =3 (anomaly).  
- Subtotals: variable (rows 17–22 → row 23), fixed (24–46 → row 47), total operating expenses (row 48 = variable + fixed).  
- Key metrics: O57 = SUM(C57:N57) (Sales Volume); B63 = O54 - O23 (Total Fixed Costs); B64 = 1 - P23 (Contribution Margin); F64 = (O54 - O23) / B64 (Break-even sales).

2) Year 2
- Continuation/second-year projection. Opening cash (B6) links to Year 1 N55; subsequent beginning balances reference prior month end (C6 = B55 etc.). Other rows replicate Year 1 layout and use same SUM patterns for totals and % of sales (P columns).  
- Some small inconsistencies vs. Year 1 (e.g., one Payroll Expenses line points at row 22 for O22 rather than O20—check mapping).

3) Variance sheets (Var,Y1,Q1; Var,Y1,Q2; Var,Y1,Q3; Var,Y1,Q4)
- Quarter-based variance analysis comparing Year 1 (benchmark) to Actuals and computing % variances. Format: groups of three columns per month in the quarter: Budget/Forecast (copied from Year 1), Actual, Variance = (Actual - Budget)/Budget (written e.g. =(D8-C8)/C8 or +(D8-C8)/C8).  
- Cells pull Year 1 month values via direct references (e.g., Var sheet C8 = 'Year 1'!C8). Totals & subtotals are SUMs of copied rows.  
- Essential operating data similarly copied and variance % calculated per month.

Common formulas & patterns
- Monthly totals: per-line O = SUM(C:N). Year totals repeated across sheets.  
- Monthly totals across months: row totals use SUM ranges (e.g., B14 = SUM(B8:B13) and O14 = SUM(O8:O13)).  
- Percent of sales: Pxx = +Oxx / O$57 (fixed reference to sales total in row 57).  
- Opening cash roll-forward: each month's beginning cash references previous month’s end-of-month (C6 references B55 etc.), Year 2 starts from Year 1 N55.

Notable issues / items to review
- Hard-coded E55 = 3 on Year 1 (likely unintended).  
- Several formulas use redundant SUM() around single arithmetic expressions (e.g., SUM(B15-B54)) — not harmful but inconsistent.  
- P57 on Year 2 is =+O57/O57 (evaluates to 1) — likely an error; intended percent-of-sales base should reference Year 2 sales (O$57) or different denominator.  
- Some misaligned references (e.g., Year 2 row for Payroll Expenses uses O22 in one place) — verify row mapping between labels and formulas.  
- Variance formulas assume benchmark (copied) values are in specific columns; ensure Actuals are entered in the intended Actual columns so variances compute correctly.  
- Many plus signs and leading + are stylistic (e.g., +O9/O57) — harmless but inconsistent.
