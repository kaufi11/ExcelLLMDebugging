Important general note

These will clear as soon as a non‑zero earnings total is entered — or you can protect the formulas using IF/HANDLING to avoid DIV/0 errors (examples shown below).

Specific problems, explanations and recommended fixes

1) STEP 1 — G22 is a hard-coded constant (=2)
- Current: G22 = 2
- Problem: G22 is used by STEP 2 (H14) as “tax withholding”, but G22 should be the total of the tax-withheld column (G11:G20). Having the hard-coded 2 produces wrong values downstream (for example H14 on STEP 2 = 2).

2) STEP 2 — many #DIV/0! results (division by zero)
- Cells showing #DIV/0!: K6, F8, F10, F12, F14, F16, F18, K20, K30, F34, F45 (and possibly others).

- Remedies:
  - Input a non-zero earnings total in STEP 1 so H6 > 0, or
  - Wrap percent formulas to avoid division-by-zero, for example:
    =IF($H$6=0, "", H8/$H$6)
    or
    =IF($H$6=0, 0, H8/$H$6)
  - Use IFERROR as an alternative:
    =IFERROR(H8/$H$6, "")

- Problem: F10 is the % for Saving (row 10) but formula references H8 (Giving), not H10 (Saving).
