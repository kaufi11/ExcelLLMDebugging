Findings (short)
- Cell Your Company!C22 is wrong: it contains the constant 7 instead of the intended formula. Because C41 (Total savings) sums C22:C39, that constant breaks the calculator.
- Many output cells will stay zero if you leave the input cells (C13, C14, C15, C16, C17 and the B% cells) at 0 — make sure you enter your inputs.

Fix (one-step)
1. Select Your Company!C22 and replace the contents with this formula:
   =C13/C14*C15*C17
2. Press Enter.

Why this fixes it (simple)
- C13/C14 = average inventory on hand (annual import value ÷ turnovers).
- Multiplying by C15 (average duty on parts) gives the duty value on average inventory.
- Multiplying by C17 (interest rate) gives the cost of money (cash flow / inventory cost of money) associated with that duty.
- Rest of the sheet (C24:C39 and C41) uses C22, so correcting C22 makes the total calculation (C41) correct.
