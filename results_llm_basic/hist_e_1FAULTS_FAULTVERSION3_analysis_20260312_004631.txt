Summary — main error found
- Cell H6 (Gross profit for 1997) has the wrong formula: it is =H5-H5 (always 0). It should be Net Sales minus Cost of sales for 1997.

Why this is a problem
- Gross profit being zero for 1997 makes all downstream numbers for that year incorrect: % of sales (H7), Operating profit (H9), Income before taxes (H16), Net income (H22) and any ratios that use those values.

Fix (two easy options)
1) Quick edit
   - Select H6 and replace the formula with =H4-H5 then press Enter.

2) Copy-correct formula from another year
   - Select a neighbouring correct cell (for example G6 or I6), Copy, then Paste Special → Formulas into H6. That ensures consistent relative references.

What to do after fixing
- Recalculate (F9) or let Excel auto-recalc. Check H7, H9, H16, H22, and any %/ratio cells for the corrected values.

Optional check
- Scan the sheet for other accidental self-references (like Xn-Xn) if a column was manually edited. The H6 error looks like a single typo; other similar rows appear correct.
