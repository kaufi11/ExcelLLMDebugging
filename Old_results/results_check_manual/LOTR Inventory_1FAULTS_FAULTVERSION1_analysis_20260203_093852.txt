Summary — main error found
- The Two Towers sheet, row 117 (cell F117) has the wrong formula. It is:
  F117 =SUM(L117:O117)
  It should include column K like the other rows (i.e. sum the 1st Printing as well). Because of that the "Have" count for that card excludes the 1st Printing value and all totals that roll up from that cell are wrong.

Why this matters
- F117 omits the value in K117 (1st Printing). That undercounts your "Have" for that card and therefore:
  - I117 (Have Total) is wrong for that row.
  - Any sheet totals (bottom-of-sheet sums and workbook rollups) that include F117 are also wrong.
  - Need (column J) for that card may be computed incorrectly as a result.

How to fix (simple)
1. On the Two Towers sheet change F117 to:
   =SUM(K117:O117)
2. Press Enter. This will add the 1st Printing value back into the Have total and correct dependent totals.

Quick check & preventative steps
- After fixing F117 check I117, J117 and the sheet totals at the bottom (e.g. F367/G367 etc) to confirm they update as expected.
- To avoid similar mistakes, standardize the formula pattern for column F (Have) and column G (Have Foil) in every set sheet. Typical patterns in this workbook are:
  - Have (F) = SUM(Kn:On)
  - Have Foil (G) = SUM(Pn:Tn)  (or SUM(Q:U) on sheets that have foil columns starting at Q — match the header layout)
- Use Excel’s Fill/Copy to copy a correct F formula from an adjacent row down the column rather than typing each one manually.

Optional: Find other occurrences quickly
- Use Excel’s Find (Ctrl+F) to search for =SUM(L in column F (or across workbook) — any other accidental starts at L instead of K will show up and you can correct them to start at K.
