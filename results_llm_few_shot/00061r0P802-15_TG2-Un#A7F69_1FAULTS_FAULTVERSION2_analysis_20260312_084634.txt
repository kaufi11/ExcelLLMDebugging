Faulty cell: A37

What’s wrong
- A37 has the formula =A36-1 (minus). That produces 34 (if A36 = 35) instead of 36.
- Consequence: A37 creates a duplicate number later (one value repeats) and one sequential number is missing from A37 onward.

How to fix (two simple options)
1) Quick fix (single cell)
   - Change A37 to =A36+1

2) Robust fix (prevents future slips)
   - Put a correct sequence formula in the whole ID column. For example, in A2 enter 1 and in A3 enter =A2+1, then fill (drag) A3 down to the end of the list.
   - Or use a row-based formula in A2 and fill down: =ROW()-1 (then fill down). This always creates a 1,2,3… sequence starting at row 2.

Why this works
- The IDs are intended to be a simple incrementing sequence. Changing the minus to plus restores the +1 increment so every row increases by one.
- Using a fill-down or ROW()-1 makes the sequence automatic and less prone to manual formula typos.

Optional check
- Some address columns use column I even though the header list skips I. Verify column headers align with data (H/I/J), but this is a layout/consistency issue, not a formula bug.
