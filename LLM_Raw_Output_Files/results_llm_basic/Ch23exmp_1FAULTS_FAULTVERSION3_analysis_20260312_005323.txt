Short summary of the problems I found
- Misaligned month headings vs formulas: the month labels are in columns B and D (B16 = August, D16 = September) but the core formulas for those months live in the next columns to the right (C and E). That causes many formulas to reference the "wrong" month cell (e.g. B21 uses C17 for sales). In short: labels and formulas are offset by one column.
- Wrong function used in the Formulas sheet: C24 is =COUNT(B21:B24) — should be a SUM of the operating expense lines.
- Several other totals/formulas that reference "prior" or "next" periods look inconsistent because of the column-offset problem (examples: C30/E30, C33/E33, F38/F42/F44). They may be correct once the column alignment is fixed.

Why these are errors (short)
- When labels and formulas are not aligned, a figure that should use the current-period sales/purchases will instead pick up a neighboring column and produce wrong numbers.
- COUNT counts numeric entries, not their sum — so COUNT(B21:B24) returns how many numeric cells, not the total expense.
