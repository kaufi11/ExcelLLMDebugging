You are right — E92 is wrong. Short summary, cause, impact and fix:

1) The error
- E92 (Other operating costs, 2001) uses =SUM(E93:E97) but it should include E98 as well.
- C92 (2002) is correct (=SUM(C93:C98)) — the two years use different ranges.

2) Why it’s a problem
- E98 (Travelling expenses for 2001) is omitted from Other operating costs for 2001. That understates Other operating costs by the full value of E98 and therefore understates Total expenditure (E100) and overstates the Surplus (E102). Those incorrect figures flow into other reports (e.g. Five Year Review entries that reference Financial Statements!E100, E102, etc.).

3) How to fix (quick)
- Edit cell E92 and change the formula to:
  =SUM(E93:E98)
- Press Enter and let Excel recalculate.

4) What happens after the fix (impact)
- E92 will increase by the value in E98 (E98 = 401,702.82 + 57,330.93 = 459,033.75).
- E100 (Total expenditure) will increase by 459,033.75.
- E102 (Surplus) will decrease by 459,033.75 (and downstream totals that depend on E102/E100 will update).

5) Extra recommendations
- Scan the sheet for other asymmetric ranges between the 2002 and 2001 columns (C92 vs E92 was a classic copy/paste/typing slip).
- Use Trace Precedents / Trace Dependents (Formulas tab) to check totals affected by a corrected cell.
