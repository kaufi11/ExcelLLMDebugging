Faulty cell
- N26

Error type
- Mechanical / Typo (hard-coded constant in formula)

What's wrong (short)
- N26's formula contains "+965" instead of the H26 cell reference. That injects 965 into the Studio Work row total and therefore inflates the Studio I subtotal (row 19) and the grand totals (row 35).

How to fix (two safe options)
1) Replace the formula in N26 with a proper range sum:
   =SUM(B26:M26)
2) Or fix the typo by replacing 965 with H26:
   =B26+C26+D26+E26+F26+G26+H26+I26+J26+K26+L26+M26

Why this fixes it (short)
- Using SUM(B26:M26) or the corrected explicit sum removes the accidental constant and correctly totals the row across columns B–M. That corrects N26 and cascades to correct N19 (Studio I subtotal) and the overall totals in row 35.

Quick preventive tips
- Prefer SUM(range) for row totals to avoid manual-typing mistakes.
- Use Find (Ctrl+F) to search for suspicious numbers (like "965") in formulas if totals look wrong.
