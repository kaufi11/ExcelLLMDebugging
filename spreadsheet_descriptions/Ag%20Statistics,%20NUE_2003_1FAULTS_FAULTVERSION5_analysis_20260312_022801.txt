Sheets
- World 1996
  - Purpose: Global fertilizer‑N budget for 1996 and cereal N budget/efficiency estimates.
  - Key inputs: B1/B2 world fertilizer‑N = 82,906,340 mT (E2); cereals assumed to consume 60% of total (E4 = 0.6*E2 = 49,743,804 mT).
  - Cereal production: rows for Wheat, Corn, Rice, Barley, Sorghum, Millet, Oats, Rye (E6:E13). Area in ha (G6:G13) and avg yield Mg/ha in H column via H = production/area.
  - N removal: %N per crop in C17:C24 (Wheat 2.13, Corn 1.26, Rice 1.23, etc.). N removed per crop = production*(%N/100) (E17:E24). Total removed = SUM(E17:E24) (E25).
  - Fertilizer vs soil split: fertilizer N removed = E25*0.5 (E28); soil contribution = E25*0.5 (E27).
  - NUE = fertilizer N removed / total N applied to cereals = E28 / E4 (E30).
  - Savings calculations: "per 1% increase in NUE" and 20% scenarios use formulas such as E32 = (E4*(0.343) - E28) and related monetary conversions using $/mT (E33/E34, G33/G34). Natural gas/energy cost notes and conversions are included (B52:B61).
  - Notes/footnotes map crop codes ((51)-1 etc.) and %N derivation methods.

Common structure & key formulas/constants
- Cereals assumed to absorb 60% of total fertilizer‑N (0.60 multiplier).
- Crop %N (used to compute N removed = production * (%N/100)):
  - Wheat 2.13, Corn 1.26, Rice 1.23, Barley 2.02, Sorghum 1.92, Millet 2.01, Oats 1.93, Rye 2.21.
- Soil vs fertilizer contribution assumed as 50%/50% (multiply total N removed by 0.5).
- NUE estimated as fertilizer‑N removed ÷ fertilizer‑N applied to cereals.
- "N fertilizer savings per 1% increase in NUE" formulas differ by sheet (example coefficients: 0.343 in World sheets, 0.426 in Developed 96, 0.295 in Developing 96) — these are used in expressions of the form: savings = (total_cereal_applied * coefficient) - fertilizer_N_removed.
- Monetary valuations use different $/mT figures in different years: $479/mT (1998), $636/mT (2001), $550/mT (2002).
- Energy/natural‑gas conversion notes: 52,249 BTU/kg NH3, 1,075 BTU/ft3 gas, 48.6 ft3 gas per kg N, $3.02/1000 ft3 gas → cost ≈ $0.146/kg N; example total kg used = 442,275,000 kg → $64,572,150.

Layout conventions
- Each sheet: header input cells for total fertilizer‑N and cereal share, then a block listing cereals with production (MT), sometimes area and computed yields, a block computing N removal per crop and totals, a split into soil vs fertilizer contributions, NUE calculation, and savings/valuation scenarios below.
- Many cells reference ranges with SUM() and simple arithmetic; several cells contain reference codes (e.g., -1, (51)-1) that point to footnotes at sheet bottom describing data sources and conversion methods.
