1) Wrong hard-coded multiplier in Shipping (row 86)

- Why it’s wrong: All similar rows use 0.16 for the minimum (and 0.25 for maximum). That strange number looks like an accidental paste/converted cell value.

2) Inventory cost section multiplies percentages instead of adding them (row 133)
- Problem: E133 = C130*0.04*0.18 and G133 = C130*0.06*0.25 multiply two different %s (4%×18%, 6%×25%) — that yields an incorrect (much smaller) number.
- Why it’s wrong: If the intent is to combine two independent savings effects (Method1 and Method2) you must add their dollar effects, not multiply their percentages.
- Two options to correct depending on intent:
  - If you want the combined dollar effect of both methods:
    - E133 = C130*(0.04 + 0.18)   (or = C130*(MinMethod1 + MinMethod2))
    - G133 = C130*(0.06 + 0.25)   (or = C130*(MaxMethod1 + MaxMethod2))
    - I133 = (E133+G133)/2
    - K133 = C130*(C131 + C133)   (if C131 and C133 contain the user-selected percents)
  - If you intended separate alternative methods (choose one), make two separate rows (Method1 and Method2) and don’t combine them.
- Pick the approach that matches your model; do not multiply the %s.

3) Division-by-zero risk in Payback & ROI formulas
- Problem: payback formulas divide by total savings (C255, E255, etc.). If any total is zero or blank you get #DIV/0.
- Fix: wrap with an IF or IFERROR. Example:
  - C262 = IF(C255=0, "N/A", ((C257+C258)/C255)*12)
  - Or use IFERROR: C262 = IFERROR(((C257+C258)/C255)*12, "N/A")

4) Consistency / maintainability improvements (recommended)
- Problem: Many rows use hard-coded multipliers (0.16, 0.25, 1, 0) scattered through the sheet. This makes the sheet error-prone and causes inconsistencies (see C57 vs C89 differences).
- Fix: Put Min%, Max% and “Your %” for each line into dedicated cells and reference those cells in the formulas. Example for receiver productivity:
