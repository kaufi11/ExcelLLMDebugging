Problems found
- C4 uses B3 (a blank/wrong cell). That makes the "difference" segment wrong and lets the minute/hour slices swap colors when minutes become a larger percentage.
- A4/F3 logic works but is awkward. F3 tests equality of floating values (A3=C3) — risky and unnecessary.

Quick fixes (replace the formulas exactly)

1) Make A4 explicitly the smaller of the two hand positions:
A4 = MIN(A3,C3)

2) Make C4 the absolute difference (with the 0.01 minimum):
C4 = MAX(ABS(C3 - A3), 0.01)

3) (Optional) Simplify F3 to avoid an equality test:
F3 = IF(A3 < C3, 1, 3)
(keep a three-state comparison only if you really need a distinct "equal" case)

Why this fixes it (short)
- A4 = MIN(A3,C3) ensures the first slice is always the smaller hand fraction (no swapping).
- C4 = MAX(ABS(C3 - A3), 0.01) always gives the positive gap between minute and hour (and enforces the 0.01 minimum). The original formula used B3 (empty/wrong cell) so the difference was computed incorrectly and the slices/colors could flip.
- Removing the exact equality check avoids floating-point equality problems.

If you prefer to keep the constant in B4 (0.01), use $B$4 in the formulas:
- C4 = MAX(ABS(C3 - A3), $B$4)

That will resolve the color-switching bug and make the slices consistent.
