Findings (short)
- One formula error: cell F14 (2001 Grade 7 percent) is =E14/170 instead of =E14/D14.
- All other percent formulas and the yearly SUM totals follow the correct pattern.

Why it's wrong
- F14 divides the passing count by the constant 170 (nonsense), not by the Grade 7 tested count in D14. That produces a huge, meaningless percent for Grade 7 (and would mislead anyone reading that one cell).

Correct value (2001 Grade 7)
- Correct formula: =E14/D14
- Correct percent = 229,177 ÷ 274,090 ≈ 0.83614 → 83.61% (rounded to two decimals).

Notes about totals
- The yearly "All Grades" percent (F17) is calculated from the summed counts (E17/D17) and is already correct (≈ 1,536,561 ÷ 1,882,232 ≈ 81.64%). Fixing F14 does not change E17, D17, or F17.

How to fix in Excel (steps)
1. Select F14.
2. Replace the formula with: =E14/D14
3. Press Enter.
4. Format the cell as Percentage (choose desired decimal places).
5. (Optional) Scan column F for any other hard-coded divisors (search for "/" followed by a number) to catch similar errors.

Why this fix works
- Percent passing must be Passing ÷ Tested. Using D14 references the actual tested count for Grade 7 so the cell computes the correct proportion; replacing the hard-coded 170 with D14 restores the consistent formula pattern used elsewhere.
