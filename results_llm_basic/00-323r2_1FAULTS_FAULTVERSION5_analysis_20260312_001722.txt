- Problem: Many numeric entries use comma decimals (e.g., "12,5 ns", "3,125"). If your Excel locale expects dots (12.5) those cells may be text and formulas will misbehave.
- Fix: verify the workbook locale or replace comma decimals with dot decimals (or convert text to numbers). To check quickly: use ISNUMBER(cell) or try Data → Text to Columns to convert.
- Why: non‑numeric values silently break SUMs and arithmetic and produce unexpected blanks/zeros.
