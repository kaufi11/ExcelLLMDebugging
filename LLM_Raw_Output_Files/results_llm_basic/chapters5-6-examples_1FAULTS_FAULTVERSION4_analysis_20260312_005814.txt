1) Wrong hard‑coded value for layoffs on "pricing" sheet
- Where: pricing!C15 is the number 1 (should be a formula).
- Why it’s wrong: that cell should calculate layoff cost (= # laid off * layoff cost/worker). Hard‑coding gives incorrect total costs.

- Explanation: C5 on the pricing sheet is # laid off for period 1; Sheet1!$B$20 is layoff cost/worker. Multiply to get layoff cost.

- Where: Sheet3 C24 currently =SUM(J5:J10)*31
- Why it’s wrong: it multiplies total demand by 31 (nonsensical) instead of summing (demand × unit price) for each period.

  =SUMPRODUCT(Sheet3!J5:J10, pricing!K5:K10)
- Explanation: SUMPRODUCT(demand, price) computes revenue correctly across periods.
