**Error Explanation:**

- This causes **Total Assets ≠ Total Liabilities + Equity**.

**Solution:**

- As shown in **Sheet3**, adjust retained earnings to account for the prior year's inventory error.
- Specifically, add the prior year's inventory error to retained earnings for the current year (e.g., in 2002 add +17000, in 2003 subtract -25000).
- This adjustment corrects equity so the balance sheet balances.

**Explanation of Solution:**

- Inventory errors affect net income and thus retained earnings.
- If errors are not corrected in retained earnings, the balance sheet will not balance.
- By adjusting retained earnings for prior year errors, the accounting equation (Assets = Liabilities + Equity) holds true.
- Sheet3 demonstrates this by including the inventory error adjustments in retained earnings (cells D35 and F35), making the balance sheet balance.

- **Sheet1**: Inventory errors cause imbalance because retained earnings are not adjusted.
