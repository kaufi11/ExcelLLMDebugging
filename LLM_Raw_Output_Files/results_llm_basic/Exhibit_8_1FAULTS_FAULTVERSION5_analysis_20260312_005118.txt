NAIC increase sheet — errors and fixes
1) D6 (yrly grth for 2000)
- Error: formula is =(C6-728)/728. That uses the constant 728 instead of last year’s value.
- Why wrong: yearly growth should compare 2000 to 1999 (C6 vs C5), not a hardcoded number.
- Fix: replace with =(C6-C5)/C5

2) E7 (total yearly Sng+Grp for 2001)
- Error: formula =E8-2258280 — it references the row below and subtracts a hardcoded number.
- Why wrong: you normally reference the actual 2001 total (input) or compute it from proper components. Referencing the next year and a constant is fragile and probably incorrect.
- Fix: either (a) enter the correct 2001 total directly in E7, or (b) change to reference the correct source cell (e.g., if you meant previous year use E6), but do not point to E8 and subtract a magic number. Example if 2000 was in E6 and you want % change: =E7-E6 (or similar) — choose the correct reference based on your data source.

3) E16, E17, E18 (grps hit the cap $Amt (% of total prem))
- Errors:
  - E16 = (0.46*C7) — multiplies 0.46 by C7 (NAIC Expenses) instead of total premiums.
  - E17 = (0.43*C8) — same issue for 2002.
  - E18 = (0.46*C9) — uses the wrong percent (0.46) and wrong column (C9).
- Why wrong: you should multiply the percentage of total premiums (D16..D18) by the corresponding total premiums (E7..E9), not the C column.
- Fix:
  - E16: =D16 * E7
  - E17: =D17 * E8
  - E18: =D18 * E9
  (Or, if you prefer explicit numbers: =0.46*E7, =0.43*E8, =0.35*E9.)

4) G16, G17, G18 ($Amt of Pd % of total database fee)
- Errors: formulas use hardcoded percentages and/or reference the wrong totals.
  - Example G16 = (0.22*E7) is OK numerically but uses a literal 0.22 instead of the F16 cell.
- Why wrong: use the F‑column (pd % of total database fee) so the formulas update automatically if percentages change.
- Fix:
  - G16: =F16 * E7
  - G17: =F17 * E8
  - G18: =F18 * E9

Notes for NAIC sheet
