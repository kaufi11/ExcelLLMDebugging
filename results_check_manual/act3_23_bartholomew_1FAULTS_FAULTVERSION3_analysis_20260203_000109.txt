Summary of the problem
- Column E (Week 4) should contain the AVERAGE of weeks 1–3, but the sheet has inconsistent formulas. That makes totals and totals-by-person wrong (one row is double-counted).
- Some weekly/summary ranges include row 13 instead of stopping at row 12.

Errors found (cells & why)
1. E9 (Smith) — currently =SUM(B9:D9).  
   - Problem: it sums weeks 1–3 instead of averaging them. F9 then sums B9:E9 and double-counts weeks 1–3.
2. E7, E8, E10, E11, E12 — some are =AVERAGE(Bx:Dx) (correct), but E9 is inconsistent. All E cells must be the same average formula.
3. F7:F12 (Total Hours) — these use =SUM(Bx:Ex) which is correct only if E is the intended week‑4 value (the average). Because E9 is wrong, F9 is wrong now.
4. Weekly Total row ranges:
   - B15 =SUM(B7:B13) and C15 =SUM(C7:C13) and G15 =SUM(G7:G13) — they include row 13 (empty) and should end at row 12. This is minor but should be corrected for clarity and to avoid errors if row13 later gets data.

Exact fixes (copy these into the cells and fill down where advised)
1. Put the same average formula into E7:E12 (Week 4). In E7 enter and fill down to E12:
   =AVERAGE(B7:D7)
   (Excel will adjust the row when filled down.)
2. Make sure totals by person use B–E. In F7 enter and fill down to F12:
   =SUM(B7:E7)
3. Fix Smith’s E9 (replace SUM with AVERAGE) — covered by step 1, but do it specifically if you just want to change that cell:
   E9 =AVERAGE(B9:D9)
4. Fix weekly totals row to use rows 7–12:
   B15 =SUM(B7:B12)
   C15 =SUM(C7:C12)
   D15 =SUM(D7:D12)  (already correct)
   E15 =SUM(E7:E12)  (already correct)
   F15 =SUM(F7:F12)
   G15 =SUM(G7:G12)
5. (Optional) If you want overtime calculated automatically (G7:G12) use:
   G7 =MAX(0, F7-40)
   then fill down. Adjust 40 if your overtime threshold differs.

Why this fixes it (short)
- Making E7:E12 consistent as =AVERAGE(Bx:Dx) ensures Week 4 contains the average of weeks 1–3 for every employee.
- F (Total Hours) then correctly sums the 4 weekly values (the first three actual weeks + Week 4 average).
- Fixing the weekly total ranges prevents accidental inclusion of extra rows and avoids incorrect aggregate totals.

How to apply quickly
- Enter =AVERAGE(B7:D7) in E7, drag the fill handle down to E12.
- Enter =SUM(B7:E7) in F7, drag down to F12.
- Update weekly totals (row 15) to the corrected SUM ranges.
