Summary of the errors I found
1. Missing or non‑numeric subscription counts (column F) for many rows. Column G uses =E*F, so blank/text F cells make G wrong or zero and make the sheet total (G407) incorrect.  
2. One explicit wrong formula: G25 is =E25*525 (hard‑coded 525) instead of =E25*F25. That will cause an incorrect per‑row total and wrong grand total.  
3. Some postage (column H) values are text (e.g. "incl.") or blank. If you ever calculate postage totals these will break numeric calculations.  
4. Possible text‑formatted numbers in E or F (numbers stored as text) — will cause SUM/F multiplication problems if present.

Quick fixes (do these first)
- Fix G25: replace the formula with =E25*F25.
- Ensure every F cell (F2:F404) has a numeric value. If a journal has zero subscriptions enter 0. Leaving them blank yields incorrect totals.
- Convert any numbers stored as text to numbers (select column F (and E if needed) → Data → Text to Columns → Finish, or multiply the range by 1 with Paste Special → Multiply).
- Replace non‑numeric postage values only if you need numeric postage calculations (e.g. change "incl." to 0 or a number in H).

How to find all problematic rows
Put this helper formula in a spare column (say I2) and fill down:
=IF(OR(E2="",F2=""),"Missing input",IF(ABS(G2 - E2*F2)>0.01,"Mismatch","OK"))
- "Missing input" = E or F empty.
- "Mismatch" = G differs from E*F (captures hard‑coded or wrong formulas).
- "OK" = G equals E*F.

How to correct all G formulas to be consistent
1. Put a correct formula in G2: =E2*F2  
2. Fill (drag or double‑click the fill handle) from G2 down to G404 to overwrite any incorrect formulas (including the hard‑coded G25). This standardizes every row.

Recalculate sheet totals
- After fixes, ensure F407 =SUM(F2:F404) and G407 =SUM(G2:G404) will reflect correct totals.

Why this works (short explanation)
- Column G must be a product of rate (E) and count (F). Blank or text F or a wrong formula (like *525) produces wrong per‑row totals and a wrong grand total. Converting text to numbers and standardizing G as =E*F ensures correct per‑row and overall sums.
