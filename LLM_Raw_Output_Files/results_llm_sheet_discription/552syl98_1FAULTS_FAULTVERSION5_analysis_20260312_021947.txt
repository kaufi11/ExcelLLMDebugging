Summary of errors I found and simple fixes

1) Wrong formula in B40
- Problem: B40 contains =-B39-2 which negates the date serial number and gives a wrong/meaningless date.
- Why it matters: breaks the date sequence for the rest of the schedule.
- Fix: Replace the formula with =B39+2 (or =+B39+2). How: double‑click B40 (or select & press F2), type =B39+2 and Enter.

2) Inconsistent URL in A7 vs the repeated URLs in row 7
- Problem: A7 = http://ce.ecn.purdue.edu/~puenvhyd while the many other row‑7 cells are http://ce.ecn.purdue.edu/~puevnhyd (letters swapped) — likely a typo.
- Why it matters: inconsistent link text across the header; users may click the wrong URL.
- Fix: Decide which is correct and overwrite the incorrect one. E.g. copy the correct string from G7 into A7.

3) Final exam cell B60 is not a date
- Problem: B60 contains text "? April/May" instead of a date/serial.
- Why it matters: breaks date calculations if you expect a date there; looks inconsistent.
- Fix: If the date is known, enter an actual Excel date (e.g. 1998-05-xx) or use a formula to compute from prior date (e.g. =B59+? ). If unknown, leave a note but keep it as text intentionally — just be aware it isn't a date.

Minor cleanup suggestions (optional)
- Leading plus signs in formulas (=+B15+2 etc.) are harmless but unnecessary; you can replace them with =B15+2 for clarity.
- Instead of filling the same URL into dozens of columns to span the printable width, use Merge & Center or Format Cells → Alignment → Center Across Selection, or place the link in the header/footer (Page Setup → Header/Footer). This reduces sheet clutter and accidental edits.
