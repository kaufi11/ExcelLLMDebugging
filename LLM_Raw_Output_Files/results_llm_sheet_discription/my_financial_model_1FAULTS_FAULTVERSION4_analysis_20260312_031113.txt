Summary — I found 3 major functional errors that will misstate revenue, COGS and the balance sheet (and thus profit, cashflow and retained earnings). I also list a couple of smaller formula bugs to tidy up. Fix those three first.

1) Revenue total omits Street Sales (big)
- Where: Income!E12 (and F12:P12).
- Problem: E12 is =SUM(E10:E10) — it only sums the Online row (E10) and leaves out Street (E11). That understates Total Revenue and every downstream number (gross/net profit, cashflow, retained earnings, BS).
- Fix: replace E12 with:
  =SUM(E10:E11)
  then copy across F12:P12.
- Why it fixes it: now Total = Online + Street as intended.

2) Sugar price growth uses wrong growth-rate cell
- Where: Income!F8:G8:...:P8 (Sugar price row).
- Problem: those formulas use $D$7 (lemon-price-growth cell) instead of the sugar growth-rate cell ($D$8). That can give the wrong sugar prices and thus wrong COGS.
- Fix: change F8 to:
  =E8*(1+$D$8)
  then copy across to P8.
- Why it fixes it: sugar price uses its own growth-rate input (D8) so ingredient costs are calculated correctly.

3) Long‑term asset original value references a liability (major BS/depreciation error)
- Where: Balance Sheet!D8 (Original Value)
- Problem: D8 is =D14 (D14 is the long‑term loan/liability). That makes your asset original value equal to the loan amount — so Depreciation and asset values become wrong.
- Fix — pick one appropriate source:
  - If the long‑term asset was bought in Cashflow (Cashflow!D9 = -100000), set Balance Sheet!D8 to:
    =-Cashflow!D9
    (this converts the negative cash outflow into a positive asset cost),
  - OR hardcode the correct asset cost (e.g., =100000) if you prefer an input cell.
- After that, Depreciation will correctly read the asset original value (Depreciation!D6 = Balance Sheet!D8) and compute correct annual depreciation.
- Why it fixes it: removes the incorrect link to liabilities and ties the asset to the investment amount.
