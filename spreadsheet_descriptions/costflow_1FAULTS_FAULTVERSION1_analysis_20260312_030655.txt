High-level overview
- Workbook contains 4 sheets: "Errors", "Valuing Ending Inventory", "NetIncome and Cash Flow", and an unused "Sheet3".
- Purpose: illustrate inventory/COGS errors and compare inventory costing methods (periodic/perpetual Weighted Average, FIFO, LIFO, Moving/Moving Average) and their impact on COGS, gross profit, taxes and cash flows.

Sheet-by-sheet summary

1) Sheet: Errors
- Layout: three scenario columns across B (Correct), D (Understating), F (Overstating) with year‑by‑year (Year 1 = error year, Year 2 = the correction year).
- Key inputs (Year 1): Beginning Inventory 100,000; Purchases 500,000; Ending Inventory correct = 150,000, underst/over adjustments = 125,000 / 175,000.
- Core formulas: Goods Available = SUM(beginning,purchases); COGS = Goods Available + (negative) Ending Inventory (SUM row formulas shown); Gross Profit = Sales + (−COGS); Taxes = −Pretax * 0.4; Net Income = Pretax + Taxes.
- Year 2 carries forward beginning inventory as the negation of prior year ending inventory (Beginning = −Prior Year Ending) and repeats the income statement structure with updated purchases/sales/expenses.
- Purpose: demonstrate how understating/overstating ending inventory in Year 1 affects COGS, pretax income, taxes, and net income across two years.
- Note: formulas are consistent (SUM, negation, multiplications for tax), and columns are mirrored for the three scenarios.

2) Sheet: Valuing Ending Inventory
- Layout: long worksheet showing multiple costing methods. Data section (left) lists transactions (dates, units, unit costs) and sales (C17, C18). Several method blocks follow:
  - Weighted Average — Periodic
  - FIFO — Periodic
  - LIFO — Periodic
  - Moving Average — Perpetual
  - FIFO — Perpetual
  - LIFO — Perpetual
- Core transaction data: Beginning inventory entries (C8=1000 @ $5), purchase (C9=6000 @ $5.25), later purchases (C12=5000 @ $5.50, C13=5000 @ $5.75). Sales: C17 = −4000, C18 = −7000 (total 11,000 sold).
- Common formulas:
  - Extended cost per line = Units * Unit Cost (E cells).
  - Totals: SUM ranges for units and extended cost (e.g., C10 = SUM(C8:C9), E10 = SUM(E8:E9), K10 = SUM(K8:K9), etc.).
  - Weighted averages: Unit cost = Total cost / Total units (e.g., K10/I10 or E27/C27).
  - Ending inventory and COGS computed by subtracting ending units or using remaining rows for each method (formulas like I11 = −I10 − SUM(C17:C18) to derive ending units).
- Perpetual sections compute running averages/remaining layers after each sale/purchase (formulas propagate previous totals into next rows).
- Some cells are placeholders or unresolved:
  - K11 and K12 marked "?" — likely need formula for ending inventory cost / COGS in that cell.
  - A few formulas reference hard-coded offsets (e.g., I8, I9 absolute refs) but follow the same pattern across method blocks.
- Purpose: compute ending inventory and COGS under each costing method given the same set of transactions.

3) Sheet: NetIncome and Cash Flow
- Layout: comparison table of results by costing method, with columns for Periodic (Weighted Avg, FIFO, LIFO) and Perpetual (Moving Avg, FIFO, LIFO).
- Key values:
  - Sales = 110,000 (consistent across methods).
  - COGS (examples shown): Periodic Weighted ≈ −60,014.71; Periodic FIFO = −58,500; Periodic LIFO = −61,500; Perpetual Average ≈ −58,607.14; Perpetual FIFO = −58,500; Perpetual LIFO = −59,000.
  - Gross profit = Sales + (−COGS) (formulas SUM of Sales and COGS).
  - Taxes = −Pretax * 40%; Net Income = Pretax + Taxes.
- Cash flow section:
  - Cash from sales = Sales.
  - Cash for purchases set as −56,250 (same across methods).
  - Cash for taxes = tax lines (method-specific).
  - Net cash flow = sum of sales, purchases, taxes.
- Holding profit section:
  - Future sales of remaining 6,000 units at $10 => 60,000.
  - Future COGS and future profit (holding profit) computed using method-specific ending inventory costs.
- Additional metrics: Historical GP% and Future GP% computed as GP / Sales and Holding Profit / Future Sales respectively.
- Purpose: show how choice of inventory method affects profitability, taxes, and cash flows, and how inventory carries profit into future periods.
