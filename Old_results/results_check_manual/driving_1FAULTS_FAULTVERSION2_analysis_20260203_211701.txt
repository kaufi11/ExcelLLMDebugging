I checked the workbook. There is one clear formula error and one general check I recommend.

1) Error (hard-coded number)
- Sheet: Balance of Business
- Cell: C11
- Current formula: =450/B$15
- Why it’s wrong: C11 should show row-11’s Sales $ as a percent of the total (like the other rows). The formula uses the literal number 450 instead of the cell B11, so the percent will be wrong whenever B11 changes.
- Fix: Replace the formula with =B11/B$15
- Effect: C11 will now update automatically when B11 or the total (B15) change. L11 (Variance) and any other dependent cells will then be correct.

2) Quick general check (recommended)
- Search for hard-coded numbers used inside formulas (they look like =1234/... or =450/...). Hard-coded constants in formulas are the most common source of hidden errors.
- How to find them quickly:
  - Use Find (Ctrl+F) search for the suspicious number (e.g., 450) or
  - Excel: Home → Find & Select → Go To Special → Constants or Formulas to review cells that aren’t pure references.
  - Or turn on Formulas view (Ctrl+`) to scan formulas quickly.
