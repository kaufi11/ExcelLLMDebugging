Findings (short)
- Error: Cell N26 contains a typo: the formula includes +965 instead of +H26. Exact shown: N26 = B26+C26+D26+E26+F26+G26+965+I26+J26+K26+L26+M26. That adds 965 to the Studio Work total and inflates category and weekly totals.
- Minor typos: G1 reads "Week9" (needs a space) and A28 reads "Client Liasion" (spelling: Liaison).

Why it's wrong (simple)
- +965 is a constant, not a cell reference. It makes the row total ~997.5 instead of the correct ~32.5, so all higher-level totals that include N26 (N19, N35, etc.) are wrong by +965.

Fix (exact steps)
1. Replace the formula in N26 with either:
   - =SUM(B26:M26)  (recommended), or
   - =B26+C26+D26+E26+F26+G26+H26+I26+J26+K26+L26+M26
2. Press Enter. Excel will recalc and remove the +965 inflation.
3. Fix labels: change G1 to "Week 9" and A28 to "Client Liaison".

Why the fix works (brief)
- =SUM(B26:M26) sums the 12 weekly cells automatically, avoiding manual-typed mistakes like stray numbers. Removing 965 returns N26 to the true sum (32.5), which corrects N19 and row 35 totals accordingly.

Recommendation
- Use SUM(range) for all row totals (column N) to prevent manual typing errors.
- Scan sheet for any other numeric constants accidentally embedded in formulas.
