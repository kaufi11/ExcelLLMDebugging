Errors found and solutions:

1. **Incorrect use of SUM for single cell arithmetic:**
   - Example: In Year 1, cell C6 has `=SUM(B55)`. SUM is unnecessary and misleading here because B55 is a single cell.
   - Similarly, in Year 1, cells B15, C15, etc. use `=SUM(B6+B14)` which is incorrect syntax. SUM expects a range or multiple arguments separated by commas, not an addition inside parentheses.
   
   **Solution:** Replace `=SUM(B55)` with `=B55` and replace `=SUM(B6+B14)` with `=B6+B14`. This applies to all similar formulas where SUM is used incorrectly for simple addition or referencing a single cell.

2. **Inconsistent or wrong cell references in Year 2 sheet:**
   - In Year 2, cell B6 formula is `'Year 1'!N55` which is correct (starting cash from Year 1 end).
   - But cells C6 to N6 use `=B55`, `=SUM(C55)`, `=SUM(D55)` etc., which are referencing the same sheet (Year 2) but with inconsistent use of SUM and wrong references.
   
   **Solution:** For the cash on hand in Year 2, each month should reference the previous month's "CASH POSITION END OF MONTH" from Year 2, not use SUM. For example:
   - C6 should be `=B55` (Year 2 B55)
   - D6 should be `=C55`
   - E6 should be `=D55`
   - And so on, without SUM.
   
3. **In Year 2, missing or incorrect formulas for payroll expenses:**
   - Cell A20 in Year 2 has label "Payroll Expenses (Direct Labor)" but the formula is in O22 and P22, not O20 and P20.
   
   **Solution:** Correct the formula placement to match the row label. Move or fix formulas so that Payroll Expenses (Direct Labor) row formulas are in row 20, not 22.

4. **Division by zero or circular references risk in % of Sales columns:**
   - Many formulas like `=+O9/O57` or `=+O17/O$57` assume O57 (total sales) is not zero.
   - If O57 is zero, this causes #DIV/0! errors.
   
   **Solution:** Use IFERROR or IF to avoid division by zero, e.g. `=IF(O57=0,0,O9/O57)`.

5. **Inconsistent use of "+" sign in formulas:**
   - Formulas like `=+O9/O57` have unnecessary "+" sign.
   
   **Solution:** Remove "+" sign; use `=O9/O57`.

6. **In Variance Analysis sheets, formulas like `=SUM(B15-B54)` are incorrect:**
   - SUM is used with subtraction inside parentheses, which is not correct.
   
   **Solution:** Replace `=SUM(B15-B54)` with `=B15-B54`.

Summary:
- Remove unnecessary SUM for single cell references or simple arithmetic.
- Fix references in Year 2 cash on hand row to correctly link previous month's ending cash.
- Correct misplaced formulas for payroll expenses in Year 2.
- Add error handling for division by zero in % of sales calculations.
- Remove unnecessary "+" signs in formulas.
- Fix SUM usage in variance sheets where subtraction is used.

These fixes will correct formula errors and improve accuracy and clarity.
