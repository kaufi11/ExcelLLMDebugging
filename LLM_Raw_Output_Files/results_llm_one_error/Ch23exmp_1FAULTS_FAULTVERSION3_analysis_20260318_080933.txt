Cell: Formulas!C24

Error: formula uses COUNT(B21:B24) instead of SUM(B21:B24).

Why it's wrong: COUNT returns how many numeric cells (e.g., 4), not the total of the expense amounts. That understates operating expenses and miscalculates operating income, taxes, and net income.

Fix: Replace the formula with =SUM(B21:B24)

Explanation: SUM adds the salaries, rent, depreciation and insurance to give total operating expenses; using SUM will restore correct downstream calculations (C25, C26, C27, etc.).
