**Solution:**

- For the circular reference in annex g row 18:  
  Change the Balance b/f formulas to refer to the previous month's Balance c/f, not the next month's. For example:  
  - I18 should be `=H18` (previous month balance c/f)  
  - J18 should be `=I18`  
  - K18 should be `=J18`  
  ... and so on. This way, the opening balance for each month is the closing balance of the previous month, avoiding circular references.

- For totals in annex g row 18 and 19:  
  Ensure consistency by summing or linking correctly. For example, T18 should be sum or linked properly, and T19 should be sum of T17 and T18.

- For variance % formulas where original forecast is zero:  
  No action needed if "N/A" is acceptable.

- For annex g T26 formula:  
  Verify that 'annex f'!H20 and H21 contain numeric values or zero to avoid errors.

---

**Explanation:**

- Circular references occur when a formula directly or indirectly refers to its own cell, causing Excel to loop endlessly. Changing the Balance b/f formulas to refer to the previous month's closing balance breaks this loop.

- Consistent totals ensure that sums and balances reconcile correctly.

- Variance % cannot be calculated when the original forecast is zero, so "N/A" is appropriate.

- Referencing empty or text cells in arithmetic formulas causes errors; ensure referenced cells contain numbers.
