Short answer — errors found
- SecA cell J47 is wrong: it contains the hard-coded number 6 instead of the normal formula (=SUM(R47:AE47)). That makes J47 (Assignment Points) and therefore I47 (Total Points) incorrect for that student.
- Minor inconsistency: the header cell J10 is SUM(R10:AF10) while every student row uses SUM(Rn:AEn). AF10 is outside the assignment columns (probably a harmless typo) — you may want to change it to SUM(R10:AE10) for consistency.

Why it's a problem
- Hard-coded J47 won't update when you change that student's assignment cells (R47:AE47), so the student's assignment total and capped total are wrong.
- The J10 AF10 mismatch is just inconsistent and could hide an extra column or cause confusion if AF later contains data.

How to fix (easy steps)
1. Fix the broken student formula (SecA):
   - Select cell J47 and replace its contents with:
     =SUM(R47:AE47)
   - Press Enter.
   - (Optional) Copy the correct J formula from a neighboring student row (e.g., J46 or J48) and paste into J47 to match formatting.

2. Make the header consistent (optional):
   - Select J10 and change to:
     =SUM(R10:AE10)

3. Check for other overwritten formulas (recommended):
   - Home → Find & Select → Go To Special → Constants → check Numbers. That shows cells with hard-coded numbers where formulas normally belong. Fix any you find by restoring the correct SUM(...) formulas.

Why these fixes work
- Replacing the hard-coded 6 with SUM(R47:AE47) makes the Assignment Points auto-calculate from the assignment columns for that row so totals update automatically.
- Making J10 use AE instead of AF removes an off-by-one range mismatch and keeps the template consistent and less error-prone.
- Using Go To Special catches accidental manual replacements of formulas so you can restore consistency across the sheet.
