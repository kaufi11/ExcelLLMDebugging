Errors found:

1. **Sheet: Seq Production Costs**
   - Cell **G21** formula: `=COUNT(G19:G20)` is incorrect. It counts numeric entries, but it should sum the costs like in B21 and D21.
   - Cell **B23** formula: `=B21+D21+G21` sums totals from columns B, D, and G, but G21 is a count, not a sum, so this will give a wrong total.

2. **Sheet: Tech Dev Costs**
   - Cell **B6** is 0, and cell **B18** formula `=B17/B6` will cause a division by zero error if B6 remains zero.

3. **Sheet: Bioinformatics Costs**
   - Cell **B6** is 0, and formulas like `=B17/$B$6`, `=B30/$B$6`, and `=B43/$B$6` will cause division by zero errors if B6 remains zero.

---

**Solutions:**

1. **Seq Production Costs Sheet:**
   - Change **G21** formula from `=COUNT(G19:G20)` to `=SUM(G19:G20)` to correctly sum the "Other Activities" costs.
   - Then, **B23** formula `=B21+D21+G21` will correctly sum the total costs.

2. **Tech Dev Costs Sheet:**
   - Ensure **B6** (Total Costs) is entered with the actual total funds requested (not zero).
   - This will prevent division by zero in **B18** (`=B17/B6`).

3. **Bioinformatics Costs Sheet:**
   - Similarly, enter the actual total funds requested in **B6**.
   - This will prevent division by zero errors in formulas that divide by B6.

---

**Explanation:**

- Using `COUNT` instead of `SUM` in G21 causes it to count how many cells have numbers rather than summing the costs, leading to incorrect totals.
- Adding a count to numeric totals (B21 and D21) in B23 results in an incorrect total cost.
- Division by zero errors occur when total cost cells (B6) are zero but used as denominators in percentage calculations.
- Entering actual total costs in B6 fixes these errors and allows percentage formulas to calculate correctly.
