Overall
- Workbook models exponential growth Q = A(1+r)^t with additive iid errors and compares three fit approaches: (1) linear fit to Q, (2) quadratic fit to Q, and (3) linear fit to ln(Q) (i.e., exponential model).
- 4 sheets: Data, Linear, Quadratic, ln(Q)=f(time). Inputs are centralized on the Data sheet.

Sheet: Data
- Header/description at A1–A3.
- Model inputs:
  - A in C4 = 100
  - r in C5 = 0.04
  - Additive error factor in C6 = 1
- Columns:
  - A: simulated iid random errors (A8:A58 contain numeric error draws).
  - B: time (0 to 50 in B8:B58).
  - C: Q values computed by formula Cn = $C$4*(1+$C$5)^Bn + (An * $C$6) — i.e., deterministic exponential plus additive noise. Note: C12 uses "56*$C$6" (not A12) — likely an input/typo anomaly.
  - D: ln(Q) computed as =LN(Cn).
  - E: duplicate time = Bn (for regressions).
  - F: time^2 = Bn^2.
- Rows 8–58 show the 51 observations (time 0..50) with generated Q and ln(Q).
