High-level overview — purpose, layout, key values and formulas

Workbook layout (sheets)
- P&L — Consolidated condensed statement of operations (summary). Shows three- and six-month columns for 2003 vs 2002, revenue/expense lines, other income/expense, tax, discontinued operations, EPS and share counts. Many cells compute quarter-over-quarter differences using a consistent pattern (e.g., H = K − O or H = +K14-O14).
- P&L Crib — Detailed supporting schedule for the P&L. Breaks revenue, cost of revenue, depreciation, G&A, restructuring, impairments, other income items and EBITDA by business unit and quarter. Contains many line-item formulas and comments that roll up to the summary P&L.
- BS — Consolidated condensed balance sheet (summary). Current assets, PP&E, intangibles/goodwill, current liabilities, long-term debt, deferred items and stockholders' equity (deficit).
- BS Crib — Detailed balance-sheet support: cash, restricted cash components, accounts receivable schedule, detailed current assets and liabilities, debt schedule and noncurrent liabilities. Feeds the summary BS.
- CF — Consolidated statement of cash flows with operating, investing and financing activities, non‑cash disclosures and quarterly/ year-to-date totals.

Key structure / formula patterns
- Summary rows in P&L and BS are mostly linked to the detailed "Crib" sheets. Many summary cells are SUM ranges (e.g., P&L K17 = SUM(K14:K16)) or difference formulas (e.g., P&L H14 = +K14 - O14).
- Quarter-to-quarter comparisons frequently use the form Hn = Kn − On (or similar) to show change.
- EBITDA and segment EBITDA lines are computed in P&L Crib as revenue less cost of revenue, depreciation, G&A and restructuring for each segment, then summed for consolidated EBITDA (e.g., D127 = SUM(D121:D126)).
- EPS calculations use aggregated loss items divided by share counts (e.g., K69 = K61 / K66).
