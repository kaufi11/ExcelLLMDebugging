Summary — main error found
- Faulty cell: D8 (Functionally deficient, 1992)
- Error type: Typo / sign error (mechanical)
- What’s wrong: The formula uses subtraction signs: =4186-3424-4479-5125-3339-5690. That produces a large negative number instead of the intended positive sum.
- Cells impacted: D6 (Urban deficient total = D7+D8) and D12 (All deficient total = D6+D9) in column D will be wrong because D8 is incorrect.

Fix
- Correct formula for D8: =4186+3424+4479+5125+3339+5690
  — or, better (less error-prone): =SUM(4186,3424,4479,5125,3339,5690)
  — best practice: if those values come from other cells, use a SUM of the cell range (e.g., =SUM(D?#:D?#)) instead of typing numbers.

Why this fixes it (short & simple)
- Changing the minus signs to plus signs makes D8 the sum of the component categories (a positive count). That restores D6 = D7 + D8 to the correct urban deficient total and then restores D12 = D6 + D9 to the correct total deficient bridges for 1992.
