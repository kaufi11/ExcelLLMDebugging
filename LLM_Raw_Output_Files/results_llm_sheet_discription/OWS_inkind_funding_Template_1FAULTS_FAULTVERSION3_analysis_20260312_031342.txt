Findings — error
- On the In-kind contribution template, cell F6 has the wrong formula: =D6/E6 (division). It should be multiplication like the other line items.

Why it's wrong (short)
- F6 = D6/E6 computes 30 / 150 = 0.2 instead of 30 * 150 = 4,500. That makes the Labor total, running totals and grand total too small.

How to fix (do this)
1. On sheet "In-kind contribution template" change cell F6 to:
   =D6*E6
2. Scan other F-column formulas for accidental "/" (division) and correct to "*" where the intent is unit price × quantity.

What changes after the fix (quick numbers)
- Before fix (current workbook): Grand Total (In-kind) ≈ 79,100.20
- After fix (change F6 to D6*E6): Grand Total (In-kind) = 83,600.00

(Why the totals change)
- Fixing F6 increases the Labor subtotal by 4,499.8 (from 5,000.2 → 9,500), which then flows into the running totals (G16 → G29 → G41) and the grand total (F43).

Extra tip
- Use consistent formulas when you copy rows (prefer =D?*E? for line-item cost) or use Excel’s fill-down so copy/paste errors like "/" → "*" are less likely.
