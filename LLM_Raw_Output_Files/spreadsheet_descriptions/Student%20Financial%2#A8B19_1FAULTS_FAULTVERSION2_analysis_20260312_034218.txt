Summary of workbook (concise)

Workbook contains three sheets: "Tution and Fees" (main tuition/fee tables), "Financial Aid" (need/non‑need summary and percentages), and "Financial Aid 2" (grants, loans, work‑study, and specific scholarship programs).

1) Sheet: Tution and Fees
- Layout:
  - Top section (A1–A13, F4–I12): multi‑year summary (columns F–I hold years 2003–2004 through 2000–2001). Rows list In‑State Tuition, Out‑of‑State Tuition, Required Fees, Room & Board (breakdown Room Only / Board Only), and Comprehensive totals for In‑State and Out‑of‑State.
  - Fee schedule breakdown (A14–K30): detailed per‑credit/credit‑range fee items for undergraduates for 2003–2004 (columns D–G) and 2002–2003 (columns H–K). Rows include Tuition per credit bracket, Required Fee components (Health, Insurance, Student Activities, Athletic, Campus Center, Educational & Technology, Technology Enhancements, ID Card, Assoc. Student Gov.), and totals.
  - Out‑of‑state section repeats similar structure (D32–K37).
  - Boarding fees table (D39–K43): Room/Board totals for Traditional, Suites, Apartments with totals computed.
  - Misc: page markers (e.g., G64 = "Page 38"), footer summary blocks (A67–H76) with a few alternate totals and year comparisons.

- Key formulas / calculations:
  - Many cells use simple arithmetic and SUM to combine components:
    - Example: G8 = G9 + G10 (Room+Board), I11 = I5 + I8 + I7 (In‑state comprehensive = tuition + room/board + fees).
    - Comprehensive totals use =SUM(range) or additions (e.g., F12 = F6 + F7 + F8).
    - Fee schedule totals: D30..K30 = SUM(D19:D29) etc.
    - Boarding totals: E43 = SUM(E41:E42) etc.
- Notable values / points:
  - In‑State tuition: 1168 (2003–04, 2002–03), prior years 854, 822.
  - Out‑of‑State tuition: 9039 (2003–04, 2002–03), prior years 7273, 7240.
  - Required Fees: ~1168 (2003–04), 1158 (2002–03).
  - Room/Board components present per‑housing type (room and board values by traditional/suites/apartment).
- Attention point: F8 is a hard value (4892) but corresponding cells for other years use formulas (G8 = G9+G10, etc.). That pattern implies the 2003–04 room/board value was entered directly rather than computed.

2) Sheet: Financial Aid
- Layout:
  - Top (A3–A17): Need‑based aid summary for Fall 2002 across three columns/categories (columns F–H appear to represent First‑time, Full‑time, Part‑time / Undergraduate groupings). Rows 5–17 show counts (# degree‑seeking, # applied, # determined to have need, # received aid, # received gift/self‑help, # whose need was fully met), and averages (percent of need met, avg package, avg gift, avg self‑help, avg loan).
  - Middle (A19–A31): Non‑need based awards, counts and averages.
  - Loans section (A27–A31): borrowing rates and average cumulative indebtedness for graduating classes (2001/2002).
  - 2001–02 Aid Awarded breakdown (A33–A47 / columns E–H): percentages and average package amounts for first‑time and all undergraduates, separated In‑State vs Out‑of‑State.
  - Page marker (F49 = "Page 39").

- Key formulas / patterns:
  - Mostly raw numbers and percentages; averages provided directly (no formulas provided in the dump).
- Notable values:
  - # degree‑seeking (Full‑time undergraduate): 2,799; First‑time full‑time: 664.
  - Average percentage of need met: 0.81 (first‑time), 0.80 (full‑time), 0.00 (part‑time).
  - Average need‑based package: 3,462 (first‑time), 3,969 (full‑time); avg need‑based loan amounts shown (e.g., F17 = 2,379, G17 = 3,852).
  - Borrowing: ~82% (2002) and 84% (2001) of grads borrowed; avg cumulative indebtedness ~$10,600 (2002), $10,400 (2001).
  - 2001–02 first‑time, first‑year: In‑state need‑based aid 75% with avg pkg 3,210; Out‑of‑state 68% with avg pkg 4,211.

3) Sheet: Financial Aid 2
- Layout:
  - Grants & scholarships section (A1–I13): Separate blocks for PELL (columns B–D), SEOG (E–G), Student Incentive (H–J), Athletic grants (B11–D13), Other grants (E11–G13), and total grants (I11–I13). Two years shown (2002 and 2000).
  - Guaranteed Loan Programs (A15–G20): FFELP subsidized, unsubsidized, and PLUS counts and totals for 2002 and 2000.
  - Other Loan & Work Programs (A22–L27): FWS (Work Study), Perkins Loans, Institutional Loans, Other Loans, plus summary totals for guaranteed/non‑guaranteed and grand totals (columns J–L). Years 2002 and 2000 included.
  - Scholarships/Loans by program (A29–M34): ROTC, NC Teaching Fellows, Nurse Education, Nurse Scholars with counts, totals, and averages for 2002 and 2000.
  - Page marker (G41 = "Page 40").

- Key formulas / values:

    - Total grants provided and totals for guaranteed loan programs shown (e.g., FFELP subsidized 2002: 1,684 recipients, $5,557,678).
    - Loans & totals 2002: J26 = 749,895 (loans), K26 = 8,426,281 (guaranteed loans), L26 = 9,438,041 (total loans).
    - Scholarships examples (2002): ROTC 9 recipients $42,048 (avg $4,672); NC Teaching Fellows 11 recipients $33,100 (avg $3,009); Nurse Scholars 16 recipients $68,500.

Overall structure and formulas (high level)
- Years run across columns in the tuition sheet (most summary tables use columns F–I or D–K for different blocks).
- Totals are computed primarily with addition and SUM formulas (e.g., =G9+G10, =SUM(G6,G8,G7), =SUM(D19:D29), =SUM(E41:E42)).
- The workbook combines itemized fee components, tuition by residency and credit load, housing options, and multiple views of financial aid (need‑based, non‑need, grants, loans, and program‑level scholarships).
- Minor inconsistencies are present (e.g., F8 is a hard number while same‑row cells for other years are formulas summing room+board).
