Good summary — I found two real formula errors in the Jan.-Dec.2002 sheet that will produce incorrect per-class and grand totals. Fixes are simple.

1) Wrong function in U52 (Total row under class on row 51)
- Problem: U52 is =COUNT(U51*$E$51). COUNT returns 1 (if product is numeric) or 0, not the numeric product you need.
- Effect: the U‑column "Total" for that class is wrong and every higher-level total that sums that cell is wrong.
- Fix: replace with the correct calculation. Use either
  - =U51*$E$51
  or
  - =SUM(U51*$E$51)
  (the simple product =U51*$E$51 is cleanest).
- Why: you want the RISE dollars for that company = number of participants (U51) × RISE cost (E51). COUNT was the wrong function.

2) BK261 overwritten with a constant instead of the sum formula
- Problem: BK261 currently contains the value 12 (hard-coded) instead of the usual formula =SUM(F261:BG261).
- Effect: BK261 is the "Number of Participants Per Class" for row 261. Because it is hard-coded it will not update when company participant cells in row 261 change; BL261 (student days), BM261 (public cost), and all rollups that include BK261 will be wrong/unstable.
- Fix: restore the formula
  - Put =SUM(F261:BG261) into BK261 (or copy BK from another class row and paste).
- Why: BK must always be the sum of the company participant columns for that class so downstream calculations remain correct.
