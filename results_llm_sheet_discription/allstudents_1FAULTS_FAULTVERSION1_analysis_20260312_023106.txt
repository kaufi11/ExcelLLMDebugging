Findings (short)
- One formula error: cell D60 (Spring 1998 — "All Grades" Tested) uses =SUM(D53:D58) but the Passing total E60 sums E53:E59. That means the tested total excludes Grade 10 (D59) while the passing total includes Grade 10. F60 (All Grades percent) =E60/D60 is therefore using a too-small denominator and will produce an inflated percent for 1998.

Why it's a problem (simple)
- Totals must sum the same rows for Tested and Passing. If Tested omits Grade 10 while Passing includes it, the percent = Passing / Tested is wrong.

Fix (exact)
- Change D60 to include the Grade 10 row. Edit D60 to:
  =SUM(D53:D59)

- Verify E60 remains:
  =SUM(E53:E59)

- F60 stays:
  =E60/D60

How to do it (step-by-step)
1. Select D60.  
2. In the formula bar replace SUM(D53:D58) with SUM(D53:D59).  
3. Press Enter.  
4. Check F60 updates to a correct percent.

Extra quick suggestions
