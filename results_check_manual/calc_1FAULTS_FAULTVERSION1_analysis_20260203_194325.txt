Summary of the problem
- The Sample Company sheet has a wrong formula in the Cash Flow / Inventory Cost of Money cell (C18). That formula divides by the duty rate and interest rate instead of multiplying by them, producing a huge incorrect value.

What is wrong (exact)
- Wrong formula in Sample Company C18:
  =C9/C10/C11/C13
- This divides by C11 (duty rate) and C13 (interest rate). The intended calculation is:
  (Annual import value ÷ Inventory turnovers) × Duty rate × Interest rate

Correct formula
- Replace the formula in C18 with either:
  =C9/C10*C11*C13
  or (clearer):
  =(C9/C10)*C11*C13

Why this fixes it (simple explanation)
- You must first get the average inventory (C9/C10), then apply the duty rate (multiply by C11) and interest rate (multiply by C13). Division by small decimals (like 0.1 and 0.12) inflates the result instead of calculating the expected cost of money.

Expected result (using Sample numbers)
- With C9=50,000,000; C10=4; C11=0.1; C13=0.12:
  =(50,000,000 / 4) * 0.1 * 0.12 = 150,000
