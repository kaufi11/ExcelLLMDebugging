Findings (short)
- Error: cell H12 on Sheet "1. Quarterly Statement (1)" has the wrong formula. It reads =SUM(H10:H11) but should include H9 as well.
- Impact: every downstream total and % in column H (H15 Total net sales, H17 Net Sales, H19 Gross profit, H22 Operating profit, H29 Income before taxes, H33/H35 Net income, H36 % of sales, and the regional totals that use H15/H46) are incorrect for that quarter.

Why it's wrong (easy)
- H12 is intended to be the total of the three product rows H9:H11 (Office imaging products, Computer peripherals, Business information products). Omitting H9 removes the Office imaging products amount (247,326), making totals too small.

How to fix (steps)
1. Edit H12 and replace the formula with:
   =SUM(H9:H11)
2. Press Enter. Excel will recalculate all dependent cells in column H automatically.
3. Verify:
   - H12 should become 569,874 (247,326 + 272,577 + 49,971).
   - H15, H17, H19, H22, H29, H33, H35 and H36 in column H will update to the correct values.

Prevention tips (quick)
- Use Fill Right / copy formulas across instead of typing each column separately to keep ranges consistent.
- After copying, use Home → Find & Select → Go To Special → Formulas or View → Show Formulas to scan for inconsistent formulas.
- Optionally, use named ranges or structured references so a single formula can be copied without manual range edits.
