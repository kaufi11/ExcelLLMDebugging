I inspected the formulas in both sheets and found one clear formula error and a couple of consistency issues that follow from it.

1) Incorrect SUM range (formula error)
- Sheet "1", cell H12 contains:
  =SUM(H10:H11)
  This omits H9. All other columns in row 12 use SUM(<col>9:<col>11). H12 should be:
  =SUM(H9:H11)

Why it matters
- Because H12 is used in totals and P&L lines, the wrong H12 value propagates to many downstream cells in column H and causes a mismatch between the "Sales by Product and P&L" totals and the segment/consolidated totals shown elsewhere (sheet "2"). For example:

  - H17 (copy of H15), H19, H20, H22, H23, H29, H30, H33, H35, H36 (all column H formulas that depend on H12/H15/H17 etc.) will be incorrect as a result.

2) Resulting inconsistency between sheets
- After fixing H12 to =SUM(H9:H11), sheet "1" column H totals (e.g., H15 Total of net sales, H22 Operating profit, H35 Net income) will align with the consolidated/segment values shown on sheet "2" (sheet "2" already shows the correct consolidated H37 = 759,277 and H38 = 86,640 which reflect the correct business-machine total including H9).
- Currently (before fix) sheet "1" shows H15 = 511,951 while sheet "2" shows H37 = 759,277 for the same quarter — this is the direct result of the H12 error.

3) Minor/formatting inconsistency (not an error but worth noting)
