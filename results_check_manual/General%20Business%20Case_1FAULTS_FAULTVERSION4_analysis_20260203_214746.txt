Summary of errors I found (short)  
1) Many "Variance" cells (column E on STMT OF OPERATIONS) use the wrong sign. They are written as =C#-A# instead of the intended variance =A#-C#. That causes variances to be the opposite sign of what they should be.  
2) A hard‑typed typo in STMT OF_OPERATIONS!C10: it reads =477-C9 (literal 477) instead of using the Net Sales forecast cell. That makes the Forecast Gross Profit wrong.

Why these are errors (short)  
- Variance = Actual − Forecast. Using Forecast − Actual flips the sign and gives misleading results.  
- A literal 477 is almost certainly a typo; C10 should calculate C8 minus C9 (forecast Net Sales less forecast COGS). A hard number breaks the roll‑up when forecast values change.

How to fix (exact steps & formulas, easy)  
1) Fix the variance column (make every variance = Actual − Forecast)  
- Go to STMT OF OPERATIONS. In E6 enter:
  =A6-C6
- Fill (drag) that formula down through the entire variance range (e.g. E6:E53 or wherever variances exist). This replaces all wrong =C#-A# formulas with the correct =A#-C# relative formula.

2) Fix the typo in C10  
- On STMT OF_OPERATIONS change:
  C10 from =477-C9
  to:
  =C8-C9

Quick checks after fixing  
- Verify a few variances manually (Actual − Forecast) to confirm sign.  
- Check that Gross Profit (rows A10/C10 and totals) updates when you change the inputs (Net Sales or COGS) — that confirms the hard 477 is gone.

Optional additional recommendation (audit technique)  
- Use Excel’s Find (Ctrl+F) to search for formulas containing patterns like "=C*-A*" or "=477" to locate other similar mistakes. Or use a helper column to recompute a sample variance and compare.
