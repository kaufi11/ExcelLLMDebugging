Findings (main error)
- Row 268 ("Public Cost") is wrong: each cell uses subtraction instead of addition. Example: F268 =SUM(F269-F270). That subtracts RISE Savings from RISE Cost.
- According to your own note (A25) Total public cost = total RISE tuition dollars + total RISE savings, so the formula must add, not subtract.

Fix (quick)
- Replace the formula in F268 with either:
  - =F269+F270
  - or =SUM(F269:F270)
- Then copy that formula from F268 across to BG268.

Why this fixes it (short)
- F269 contains the RISE tuition (RISE Cost) for that column, F270 contains the RISE Savings. Public Cost = tuition + savings, so adding those two gives the correct public cost per column. BI268 (the grand total) already sums F268:BG268, so it will show the correct grand total after this change.

Other minor cleanups (optional)
- Many cells use SUM around a single arithmetic expression (e.g. =SUM(C3-D3)). That's harmless but redundant. You can simplify to =C3-D3 or =C3-D3 for clarity.
