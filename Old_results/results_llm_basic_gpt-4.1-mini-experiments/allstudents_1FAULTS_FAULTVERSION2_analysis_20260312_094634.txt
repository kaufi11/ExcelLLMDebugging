**Error:**
In the "All Grades" rows (e.g., D17, E17, F17 for 2001), the sum ranges for Tested (D) and Passing (E) do not include Grade 3 data consistently. For example:
- In 2001 (row 17), Tested sum is =SUM(D11:D16) (Grades 4 to 10), but Passing sum is =SUM(E10:E16) (Grades 3 to 10).
This mismatch causes incorrect totals and percentages.

**Explanation:**
- The Tested sum excludes Grade 3 (D10), but Passing sum includes Grade 3 (E10).
- This inconsistency means the total Tested count is less than the total Passing count, which is logically impossible.
- The Percent Passing (F17) calculated as E17/D17 will be incorrect.

**Solution:**
Make the sum ranges consistent for both Tested and Passing columns by including Grade 3 in both sums.

For 2001 (row 17):
- Change D17 formula to =SUM(D10:D16) (include Grade 3)
- Keep E17 as =SUM(E10:E16)
- F17 remains =E17/D17

**Apply the same fix for all years:**
- Ensure Tested sums (column D) include Grade 3 row.
- Passing sums (column E) already include Grade 3 row.
- Percent Passing (column F) = Passing sum / Tested sum.

This will fix the inconsistency and give correct totals and percentages.
