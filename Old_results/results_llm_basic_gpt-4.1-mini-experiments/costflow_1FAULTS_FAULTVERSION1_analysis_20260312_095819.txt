### Errors found in the "Errors" sheet:

1. **Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) Calculation in Year 1 (Cells B12, D12, F12):**  
   Formula used: `=SUM(B10:B11)` where B11 is negative ending inventory.  
   - **Issue:** Adding a negative ending inventory to goods available for sale (positive) results in incorrect COGS.  
   - **Correct approach:** COGS = Goods Available for Sale - Ending Inventory (subtract ending inventory as positive number).  
   - **Solution:** Change formula in B12, D12, F12 to `=B10 + B11` (since B11 is negative, adding it subtracts the ending inventory). Using SUM is okay if signs are correct, but ensure ending inventory is negative.

2. **Year 2 Beginning Inventory (Cells B27, D27, F27):**  
   Formula: `=-B11` (negative of Year 1 ending inventory).  
   - **Issue:** Ending inventory in B11 is negative, so negating it results in a positive beginning inventory, which is correct. No error here.

3. **Year 2 Cost of Goods Sold (Cells B31, D31, F31):**  
   Formula: `=SUM(B29:B30)` where B30 is negative ending inventory.  
   - **Issue:** Same as Year 1 COGS, ensure signs are consistent. If ending inventory is negative, SUM works; if positive, subtract it.

4. **Ending Inventory in "Valuing Ending Inventory" sheet (Cell I11):**  
   Formula: `= -I10 - SUM(C17:C18)`  
   - **Issue:** Ending inventory calculation seems off. Ending inventory should be Goods Available for Sale minus units sold.  
   - **Solution:** Should be `= I10 + SUM(C17:C18)` if sales are negative units (C17 and C18 are negative). Check signs carefully.

5. **Ending Inventory Cost (Cell K11) and Cost of Goods Sold (Cell K12):**  
   Marked as `?` — formulas missing.  
   - **Issue:** These cells lack formulas, so inventory valuation and COGS cost are incomplete.  
   - **Solution:** For K11 (Ending Inventory Cost), formula should be unit cost * units in ending inventory. For K12 (COGS), formula should be total cost of goods available for sale minus ending inventory cost.

6. **Inconsistent use of SUM for adding positive and negative values:**  
   Throughout the sheet, SUM is used with negative values to subtract, which is fine if signs are consistent. But if signs are inconsistent, this causes errors.

### Explanation of solutions:

- **COGS formula:** Since ending inventory is entered as a negative number (to subtract), summing goods available for sale and ending inventory (negative) gives correct COGS. Just ensure ending inventory is always negative.

- **Ending Inventory Cost and COGS formulas:** These are essential to calculate the dollar value of ending inventory and cost of goods sold. Without them, the inventory valuation is incomplete.

- **Ending Inventory units:** Sales are recorded as negative units. To find ending inventory units, add sales (negative) to goods available for sale units.
