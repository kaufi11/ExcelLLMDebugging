Overview: the workbook contains 7 sheets solving time‑value problems (NPV/IRR) and simple corporate finance exercises. Each sheet is organized with inputs (top/left), cash‑flow rows, a sweep of discount rates (columns A/B or column J), NPV/discounted CF calculations, and short written conclusions. Key cells, formulas and any anomalies are noted per sheet below.

Sheet: Q1
- Purpose: DCF sensitivity table for cash flows at t=0..3 and IRR discussion.
- Inputs (row 1–2): Periods in D1:G1 = {0,1,2,3}; nominal cash flows in D2:G2 = {-85, 120, 100, -140}.
- Discount‑rate column: J4:J44 contains a sweep from -10% to +30% (various steps).
- Discounted CF rows (rows 4:44): Dn–Gn formula pattern: =$D$2/(1+$Jn)^$D$1 etc — each cell discounts the corresponding nominal CF by (1+rate)^period. Row Bn = SUM(Dn:Gn) gives total NPV at that rate.
- Key result/note: A46 states “The NPV is positive for discount rates which are >7% and <43%” — this non‑monotonic behavior occurs because of the negative future cash flow (t=3), producing multiple sign changes in the CF stream.
- IRR: E48 on the sheet uses =IRR(D2:G2) (Excel returns the positive ~7% root). Comments in B49–B55 discuss multiple IRR pathology.
- Anomaly: D19..G19 formulas are consistent except E19 shows a typo in your text: = $E$2/(1-$J19)^$E$1 (minus sign) — this is likely a copy error and should be +.

Sheet: Q2
- Purpose: Valuation of a small project under no‑debt and debt scenarios, with/without depreciation.
- Common inputs: tax rate C3 = 0.25, base discount rate C4 = 0.15 (no debt), project cost C9/C28/C49/C74 = -200, annual pre‑tax cash inflows (periods 1–4) = 80 each (D7–G7 etc).
- No‑debt, no‑depr block:
  - Taxes: D8:G8 = -D7*$C$3 (tax on cash inflow).
  - Total after‑tax CFs: row 10, discounting row 11 uses C11 = C10/(1+$C$4)^period.
  - Project DCF: C12 = SUM(C11:G11).
- Debt scenario (no depreciation):
  - Loan C17 = 100, interest rate C18 = 0.06.
  - Interest rows (D24:G24) = -$C$17*$C$18; taxable income adjusts; taxes computed; principal repayment represented (G29 = -100).
  - Cost of equity formula used: C20 = (0.15 - 0.06*(0.5)*0.75)/0.5 — a levering formula incorporating tax shield (uses debt/equity 0.5 and corporate tax 0.75 multiplier in this sheet).
  - Discounting of equity cash flows in row 31 uses the appropriate discount rates (cost of equity for levered).
- With depreciation blocks:
  - Depreciation inputs: C38/C59 = 2 (per‑year), carried into EBT calculations.
  - Tax loss carryforward handled via IF formulas (e.g., E46 = IF(D47=0,SUM(D45:D47),0)).
  - DCFs computed similarly; final equity value C32/C78 = SUM(discounted CFs).
- Notes: the sheet models tax shields, tax loss carryforwards, and shows how depreciation and debt change after‑tax cash flows and discounting.

Sheet: Q3 (Ford)
- Purpose: Simple financial statement extract (Ford Motor Company) and valuation snippets.
- Data: 1999–2001 income statement items in C:E rows (Revenue, Cost of Revenue, Gross Profit, Operating Income, EBIT, Interest Expense, Income Before Tax, Net Income etc.). Example key numbers: 2001 Revenue C3 = 162,412,000,000; Net income C21 = -5,453,000,000 (2001).
- Calculations:
  - 3‑year average earnings: C34 = AVERAGE(C28:E28).
  - Perpetuity value at 9% and 11%: C37 = C34/0.09; C51 = C34/0.11.
  - Market value: C41 = shares C39 * price C40 (C39 = 1,735,756,535; C40 = 15.55).
  - PVGO: C43 = C41 - C37 and C53 = C41 - C51.
  - P/E: C46 = C41 / C34; EPS/Price transformation in E45 = 1/(1 - C43/C41).
- Notes: these cells compute valuation statistics; watch for negative average earnings if data contains a loss (affects perpetuity formulas).

Sheet: Q4

- Inputs: Income tax expense D6 = 1,691,000,000; Income before taxes D7 = 5,670,000,000; Depreciation D9 = 803,000,000; Interest D16 = 289,000,000.

Sheet: Q5
- Purpose: P/E and E/P using Gordon growth model examples.
- Formulas: P/E = 1 / (r - g). Example inputs r=10%:
