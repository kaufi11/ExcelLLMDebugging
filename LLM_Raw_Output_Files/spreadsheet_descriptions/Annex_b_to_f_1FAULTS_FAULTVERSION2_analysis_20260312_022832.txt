Brief spreadsheet overview

Workbook purpose
- Mid-year Financial Forecast Update template (2002) for colleges: captures declaration, commentary, Income & Expenditure (I&E), Balance Sheet (BS) and Cash Flow (CF) forecasts and revisions (original vs. mid‑year/revised).

Sheets (high level)
1. Declaration - Annex B
   - Title, college contact fields (name, address, phone, e‑mail).
   - Declaration paragraph and signature area (Principal/Accountable Officer, date).
   - No calculations; text and signature inputs.

2. Commentary - Annex C
   - Title and instructions.
   - A4 links to Table 1 header: = 'Table 1.0 I & E - Annex D'!A4.
   - Large text box area for financial commentary on material changes (completing mandatory fields).

3. Table 1.0 I & E - Annex D (Income & Expenditure, consolidated)
   - Columns: H = Original FFR Forecast (2001‑02, £000), I = Mid‑Year Update / Revised Outturn, J = Variance (I−H), K = Variance % (IF(H=0,"N/A",(J/H)*100)), L = Reason text for material variances.
   - Income rows (Funding council grants, contracts, tuition fees, other grants, other income, investment income); totals use SUM(H9:H14) and SUM(I9:I14).
   - Expenditure rows (staff costs, restructuring, other operating, depreciation, interest) and Total Expenditure (SUM).
   - Surplus/(Deficit) calculations: surplus = Total Income − Total Expenditure; further lines adjust for gains/losses on asset disposals, taxation, minority interest, historical cost adjustments.
   - Many cells are placeholders = 0 by default.
   - Repeating formula pattern: J = I − H; K = IF(H=0,"N/A", (J/H)*100).
   - Units shown as £000.

4. Table 2.0 BS - Annex E (Consolidated Balance Sheet)
   - Same column structure (H original, I revised, J variance, K %).
   - Sections: Fixed assets (detailed breakdown), Endowment investments, Current assets (stocks, debtors, cash), Creditors due within 1 year, Creditors after 1 year, Provisions, Deferred capital grants (broken down), Endowments, Reserves and Total Funds.
   - Key formulas: Totals via SUM ranges (e.g., Total Fixed Assets = SUM(H7:H12)); Net current assets = H23 − H33; Total assets less current liabilities = H13+H15+H35; Total net assets = H37 − H44 − H47; Total Funds = H56 + H59 + H67.
   - Consistency check cell: H74 = H49 − H69 (should be zero); similar for I74/J74/K74.

5. Table 3.0 CF - Annex F (Operating Cash Flow, consolidated)
   - Two sections: ORIGINAL CASH FORECAST 2001‑02 (columns H:S months Aug–Jul and T total) and ACTUAL/REVISED FORECAST 2001‑02 (same layout for actuals/forecasts).
   - Rows: cash receipts (SFEFC funding, other grants, other cash), total cash in (SUM), cash payments (payroll, other payments, interest), total cash out, cash on/off deposit, net monthly inflow (H10−H15+H16), opening and closing balances (running carry‑forwards linked down the months), financing breakdown (cash, overdraft).
   - Key reconciliations:
     - “These cells should be zero” per month: H25 = H24 − H19 etc. (original section).
     - Reconciliation to balance sheet: T26 = T19 − ('Table 2.0 BS - Annex E'!H20 + H21 + H22) for original; T49 = T42 − ('Table 2.0 BS - Annex E'!I20 + I21 + I22) for revised.
   - Many monthly input cells default to 0; totals are SUM across months.

Common formula patterns and links
- Variance calculations: J = I − H throughout sheets.
- Percentage variances: K = IF(H=0,"N/A",(J/H)*100) used consistently.
- Totals use SUM ranges and are used downstream (I&E totals feed surplus lines; BS totals feed funds; CF balances reconcile to BS cash lines).
- Inter-sheet links: Commentary A4 links to Table1 A4; Table3 reconciliation links to Table2 cash/bank lines; various cross‑references ensure consistency across statements.

Key values / status
- Template values are primarily placeholders = 0 or formulas; no populated monetary data (all £000 cells show zero by default).
- Units shown as £000 in I&E and BS headings; CF shows £'000.

Validation checks
- “These cells must be zero”/“These cells should be zero” checks exist in BS and CF to ensure internal consistency (e.g., H74, H25..H26, H48..H49).
- Reconciliation cells compare CF closing balance to BS cash lines.

Overall layout and flow
- Declaration and commentary sheets capture narrative and sign‑off.
- Table 1 prepares P&L (I&E) original vs revised.
- Table 2 produces the consolidated balance sheet using I&E outcomes and deferred grants/reserves.
- Table 3 models monthly cash flow (original and revised) and reconciles back to the balance sheet.
- The workbook enforces consistent variance and percent calculations and includes reconciliation checks to flag inconsistencies.
