High-level summary
- Workbook purpose: fiscal summary of TFAC non‑sponsored funds. Each sheet is a fund-level fiscal page (1003, 1043, 1166, 1198, 1856) and a top-level Summary that aggregates those funds.
- Consistent layout on every sheet: Resources (carry forward), Revenues (by revenue class), Transfers, Total Net Resources, Expenditures (by function and by object class), Total Expenditures, Ending Balance. Columns D–I represent time periods: D=1999‑00 (Actual), E=2000‑01 (Actual), F=2001‑02 (Actual), G=2002‑03 / Period 14, H=2003‑04 (Projected), I=2004‑05 (Budget).

Sheets
- Summary: Aggregates the five fund sheets. Many cells are formulas referencing either each fund’s specific summary rows (e.g., ='1003'!D25) or using 3‑D SUM across '1003':'1856' (e.g., =SUM('1003':'1856'!D6:D6)). Major summary formulas: Total Revenues = SUM(D9:D13) (pulling each fund’s revenue cell), Total Transfers = SUM of transfer lines pulled across funds, TOTAL NET RESOURCES (D26) = Carry Forward + Revenues + Net Transfers (D6+D14+D24), TOTAL EXPENDITURES (D61) = sum of object‑class totals, ENDING BALANCE = TOTAL NET RESOURCES – TOTAL EXPENDITURES (D63 = D26 – D61).
- Fund sheets (1003, 1043, 1166, 1198, 1856): same structure as Summary but contain fund‑level numbers and intermediate formulas.
  - Carry Forward (row 6) is rolled forward with a formula like =+E6+D72-D25-D35 (prior column carry forward + prior col total expenditures − prior col revenues − prior col net transfers).
  - Total Revenues (row 25) = SUM of revenue classes D9:D24.
  - Net Transfers (row 35) = SUM of transfer lines D28:D34.
  - TOTAL NET RESOURCES (row 37) = D35 + D25 + D6.
  - TOTAL EXPENDITURES (row 72) = SUM of object‑class rows D56:D71.
  - ENDING BALANCE (row 74) = TOTAL NET RESOURCES − TOTAL EXPENDITURES.

Key formula patterns
- Summary uses either direct links to fund cells (='1003'!D25) for some rows or 3‑D SUM across '1003':'1856' for rows that are identical across funds (e.g., =SUM('1003':'1856'!D56:D56) to aggregate salaries).
- Fund roll‑forward/carry formulas reference the same sheet’s totals to maintain running balances.
- Totals are consistently computed by SUM over the expected ranges (revenues, transfers, expenditures).
