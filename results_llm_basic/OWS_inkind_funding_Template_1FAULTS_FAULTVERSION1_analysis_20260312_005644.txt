Summary of the error
- Sheet: In-kind contribution template
- Cell F16 (Total Labor) has the wrong formula: =COUNT(F5:F15)
  - COUNT counts how many numeric cells there are (returns 2 in your data), not their monetary total.
  - It should sum the dollar amounts.

Why this matters (example with your data)
- F5 = 50*100 = 5,000
- F6 = 30*150 = 4,500
- Correct labor total should be 5,000 + 4,500 = 9,500
- Current COUNT(F5:F15) = 2, so G16 = 2, and all downstream totals (G29, G41, F43) are too low by 9,498 (9,500 - 2).

Fix (short)
- Change F16 on the In-kind sheet to: =SUM(F5:F15)

How to do it
1. Select cell F16 on the In-kind contribution template.
2. Enter: =SUM(F5:F15)
3. Press Enter.
4. Verify G16, G29, G41 and F43 update to the expected amounts.

Brief explanation
- SUM adds the monetary values (what you want). COUNT only returns how many numeric entries are present, not their sum. After replacing COUNT with SUM, all running and grand totals will be correct.
