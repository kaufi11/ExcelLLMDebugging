Summary of workbook structure and contents

Sheets
- NAIC increase
  - Purpose: model NAIC database fee revenue, caps on fee change, and groups/insurers hitting the cap across 1999–2003 under an assumed 4% average premium growth.
  - Key headers/areas:
    - A column: years and narrative notes (A1 title, A3 assumption, A5–A9 years 1999–2003).
    - B: "NAIC Revenue" (B5 = 45,753,522; B6 = 47,440,639).
    - C: "NAIC Expenses" (C5 = 42,091,927; C6 = 44,535,927; C7 = 47,728,842; C8 = 49,836,788).
    - D: "yrly grth" — growth formulas per year (examples: D6 = (C6-390)/390 — unusual reference; D7 = (C7-C6)/C6; D8 = (C8-C7)/C7).
    - E: "total yrly Sng+Grp=" / "Fee revenue" — E8 = 22,602,605; E9 = 24,900,000; E7 formula = E8-2,258,280 (back-references next row).
    - F: "% (year over year change in E)" — F8 = (E8-E7)/E7; F9 = (E9-E8)/E8.
    - G / H: "Cap - Fee Chg Amt" split into Single (G) and Group (H) caps by year (G7..G9 = 50k, 65k, 85k; H7..H9 = 150k, 195k, 255k).
    - I: "% yrly grth for Group cap" — I8 = (H8-H7)/H7; I9 = (H9-H8)/H8.
    - J8: textual note about cap applying to companies > $0.25B.
  - Cap-hit estimates (text rows A11–A13): narrative estimates for 2001–2003 (counts, % of premiums, % of database fee).
  - Table (A15–G18) estimating groups hitting the cap:
    - Columns: estimated number of groups (C15), percent of total premium (D15), $ amount (E15), percent of database fee paid (F15), $ amount of fee paid (G15).
    - Example formulas: E16 = (0.46*C7) — multiplies 0.46 by C7 (C7 is NAIC Expenses = 47,728,842); E17 = (0.43*C8); E18 = (0.46*C9) (possible typo: uses 0.46 not 0.35).
    - G16 = (0.22*E7); G17 = (0.2*E8); G18 = (0.17*E9).
  - Small insurers table (A20–F21): year 2000 row shows insurers = 921, insurers that hit cap = 29, with D21 = 0.46 and F21 = 0.22.
  - Notable formulas/behaviors to review:
    - Several formulas reference cells that look incorrect for the intended calculation (e.g., D6 uses a hard-coded 390 denominator; E7 is derived from E8; E16/E17/E18 multiply percentages by C7–C9 though the header suggests they should use total premiums in E7–E9; E18 uses 0.46 instead of 0.35).
    - Narrative and numeric data are mixed in column A (makes parsing formulas/rows slightly harder).

- Consumer Price Index
  - Purpose: monthly CPI (All Urban Consumers) series by year, with annual and percent-change calculations (1991–2001 shown).
  - Layout:
    - A1–A8: metadata (series id CUUR0000SA0, not seasonally adjusted, U.S. city average, base period 1982–84=100).
    - Row 10: column headers Jan..Dec (B..M), N = "Dec% of chg" (Dec month-on-previous-Dec), O = Annual (annual average), P = "Ann % of chg".
    - Rows 11–21: years 1991–2001 with monthly values (e.g., 2000 row M20 = 174.0; 2001 row M21 = 176.7).
  - Key formulas:
    - N12..N21: Nn = (Mn - M(n-1))/M(n-1) — percent change in December from prior year's December (example N12 = (M12-M11)/M11).
    - P12..P21: Pn = (On - O(n-1))/O(n-1) — annual percent change (example P12 = (O12-O11)/O11).
    - Q column: Q12 = 140.3 etc; Q21 = 177.1; R21 = (Q21-Q12)/Q12 (percent change between Q21 and Q12).
    - S21 = (M21-M12)/M12 is present (dec-change over the 1992–2001 span).
  - Notes: monthly and annual values are provided; typical CPI change formulas are in place (monthly-to-month and year-over-year), and there are extra comparison formulas spanning non-adjacent years (Q and R and S cells).
