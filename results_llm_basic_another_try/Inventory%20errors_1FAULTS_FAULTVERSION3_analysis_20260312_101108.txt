**Solution:**

- Adjust **retained earnings** for prior year inventory errors to reflect the cumulative effect of inventory misstatements.
- In the balance sheet, add/subtract the inventory error amount to retained earnings for the current year.
- This adjustment ensures total assets equal total liabilities plus equity, balancing the statement.

**Explanation of Solution:**

Inventory errors affect net income and retained earnings. If an inventory error from a prior year is discovered, it must be corrected by adjusting retained earnings (equity) because the error affects prior periods' profits.

- Without adjusting retained earnings, the balance sheet will not balance as assets (inventory) are misstated.
- By adding the prior year inventory error to retained earnings, you correct equity to reflect the true accumulated profits.
- This adjustment aligns the balance sheet totals, ensuring the accounting equation balances.

**Summary:**

- **Sheet1 and Sheet2:** Inventory errors not adjusted in retained earnings → imbalance.
- **Sheet3:** Retained earnings adjusted for prior year inventory errors → balance sheet balances.
