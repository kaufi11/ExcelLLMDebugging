Summary of workbook structure and key contents

Overview
- Workbook contains 6 sheets focused on store sales, business balance, cash flow planning and margin analysis. Many sheets prompt the user to "Fill in the blue areas" — input cells are expected; totals and KPI cells are calculated.

Sheet-by-sheet summary

1) Store Mgr Sales Worksheet
- Layout: A1 title, A2 month line. Rows A4:A41 list daily rows (many repeated weekdays for a month). Columns B–Q are daily metrics:
  - B: Actual, C: Plan, D: Sales LY, E: Sales Prior,
  - F–H: Avg Transaction (TY/Plan/LY),
  - I–K: IPT (TY/Plan/LY),
  - L–M: Conversion (TY/LY),
  - N–O: Weather (TY/LY), P: Holidays, Q: Comments.
- Purpose: daily data capture for a month. No formulas were provided in this sheet in the spec.

2) Balance of Business
- Title in A1. Column headers in row 4: Department/Class (A), Sales $ (B), % to total (C), Margin $ (D), % to total (E), Markdown $ (F), % to total (G), GMROII (H), % to total (I), Space (J), % to total (K), Variance Sales to Margin (L).
- Rows 5–14: department rows with base values in B,D,F,H,J and formulas for percent columns referencing row 15 totals.
  - Example patterns: C5 = B5/B$15 (each department sales % = department sales ÷ total sales in B$15). Similarly E# = D#/D$15, G# = F#/F$15, I# = H#/H$15, K# = J#/J$15.
  - L# = C# - E# (variance between sales % and margin %).
  - Note: C11 is a fixed ratio formula =450/B$15 (hard-coded numerator).
- Totals in row 15:
  - A15: "Total", B15 = SUM(B5:B14), D15 = SUM(D5:D14), F15 = SUM(F5:F14), H15 = SUM(H5:H14), J15 = SUM(J5:J14).
- Purpose: department-level P&L percentages vs totals; highlights variance between sales % and margin %.

3) CashFlow (6-month cash flow budget planner)
- Header with date/prepared-by. Columns arranged in 6 repeating month blocks: Month 1 in C/D, Month 2 in E/F, … Month 6 in M/N. Each month has Budget (odd columns) and Actual (even columns).
- Sections:
  - Cash In (Sales, Other income/loans, etc.). Line A total cash received on row 13: e.g., C13 = SUM(C9:C12) and similarly across months.
  - Cash Out: fixed expense list on rows 18–34 (Suppliers, Rent, Payroll, etc.). Line B total cash paid out row 35: C35 = SUM(C18:C34), etc.
  - Monthly cash flow summary:
    - Line C (surplus/deficit) row 38: C38 = C13 - C35 (per month).
    - Line D Cash on Hand row 39 (user input), Line E Cash available row 40: C40 = C39 + C38 (carry-forward formula). Row 41 mirrors end-of-month balances (= row 40).
- Purpose: plan and track cash inflows/outflows and month-to-month cash available; standard rolling cash-on-hand logic.

4) Maintained Margin Worksheet
- Title and example vs your numbers layout.
- Example columns show Net Sales, Markdowns, Allowances, Employee Discounts, Shrink Reserve and Inventory to be accounted for (C9 = 16650).
- Key formulas:
  - C11 = Cost of Goods Sold (example 9174).
  - D9 = SUM(D4:D8) (user "Yours" inventory to be accounted for).
  - D11 = D9 * D10 (COGS = inventory × cost %).
  - D14 = D12 - D13 (Maintained margin = Net sales - COGS).
  - D15 = D14 / D12 (MM %).
- Purpose: calculate maintained margin and maintained margin % from inventory and cost %.

5) Maintained Margin Report
- Monthly maintained margin report, two columns COST (D) and SELLING (F).
- Key items:
  - Beginning Inventory (D5/F5), Receipts (D6/F6), Mark-ups (F7); Inventory to be accounted for D8 = SUM(D5:D7), F8 = SUM(F5:F7).
  - Net Sales: E16 = SUM(E14:E15) (gross sales minus returns).
  - Inventory reductions (sales, allowances, markdowns, employee discounts, shrink) summed in F25 = SUM(F19:F24).
  - Cost of goods sold derivation: E30 = 9174 (example), E28/E29 show inventory at selling and % cost (100%-MU).
  - Closing inventory: D39 = D36 - D37; F39 = F36 - F38.
  - Maintained margin example: C32/D32/E32 show Net Sales - COGS = 5,826 and MM% = 0.388.
- Purpose: reconciles inventory, COGS and maintained margin; mirrors worksheets used in retail inventory accounting.

6) Formulas
- Collection of common KPI formulas (instructions to fill blue areas).
- Examples (cell references from sheet):
  - Average Transaction: D5 = C5/C6 (Sales ÷ Number of Transactions).
  - Items per ticket: D8 = C8/C9 (Total items sold ÷ Total transactions).
  - Conversion rate: D11 = C11/C12 (Total transactions ÷ Total traffic).
  - GMROII: D14 = C14/C15 (Maintained margin dollars ÷ Avg inventory at cost).
  - Sales per sq ft: D17 = C17/C18 (Sales ÷ Selling area).
  - Turnover: D20 = C20/C21 (Sales ÷ Avg inventory).
  - Selling cost ratio, Return on Net Assets, Quick Ratio etc. all presented as value ÷ base with formulas shown.
- Purpose: KPI reference sheet with formula templates for store performance metrics.

Key relationships and dependencies
- Balance of Business percentages rely on row 15 totals (B$15, D$15, F$15, H$15, J$15).
- CashFlow uses per-month SUM ranges for inflows (rows 9–12) and outflows (rows 18–34) and computes surplus (Row 38) and rolling cash available (Row 40).
- Maintained Margin sheets interrelate inventory-to-be-accounted, cost% and COGS to produce maintained margin and MM%.
- Formulas sheet centralizes common KPI computations used elsewhere (e.g., GMROII, turnover, conversion).

Notes / recommendations
