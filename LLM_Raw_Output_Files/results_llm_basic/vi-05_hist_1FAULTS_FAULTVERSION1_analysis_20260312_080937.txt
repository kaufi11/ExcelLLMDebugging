Findings (short)
- On the Spring 2003 sheet cell H14 (Subtotal State Components — Veterinary per term) the formula is wrong: it is =SUM(H10:H12). It omits H13 (Student Financial Aid).
- This under-states the Veterinary subtotals and all downstream totals (H21 and H25) by the value in H13 (219.55).

Why it's wrong (short)
- H14 should sum the four state-component rows H10:H13. The current range stops at H12 so H13 is excluded and the subtotal is too small. That error propagates into Total Florida Resident Rate (H21) and Total Non-Resident Rate (H25).

Fix (exact steps)
1. On the Spring 2003 sheet edit H14 and change the formula to:
   =SUM(H10:H13)
   (or =H10+H11+H12+H13)
2. Press Enter. Excel will recalculate H21 and H25 automatically.

Effect of the fix (numbers)
- Before fix H14 = 4,486.46 (missing 219.55).
- Correct H14 = 4,706.01 (4391.26 + 46.40 + 48.80 + 219.55).
- H21 increases by 219.55 to 5,104.01.
- H25 increases by 219.55 to 13,890.29.

Extra recommendations (short)
- Scan other subtotal cells for similar off-by-one range mistakes (copy/paste errors).
- Apply consistent number formatting (two decimal places) across the sheet to avoid apparent rounding inconsistencies.
