Summary of the problems I found (short & clear), and how to fix them.

1) Faulty cells: Sheet SecA!J10, SecB!J10, SecC!J10
- Error type: Range typo / mechanical
- What’s wrong: Formula uses =SUM(R10:AF10) but your data stops at column AE. AF is the wrong end column (it may be blank now or may pick up unintended data later).
- Fix: Change to =SUM(R10:AE10)
- Why: keeps the sum consistent with all other rows (they use R:AE) and prevents accidental inclusion of extra column AF.

2) Faulty cells: SecA..SecC — I14:I50 (each student Total Points)
- Error type: Logical (extra credit incorrectly capped)
- What’s wrong: Formula =MIN(500,SUM(Jx:Mx,Px:Qx)) includes P and Q (extra credit) inside the MIN(500,...) cap. That means extra credit can be lost if SUM(J:M,P:Q) exceeds 500.
- Fix (recommended): Use
  =MIN(500,SUM(J14:M14)) + SUM(P14:Q14)
  and copy down for each student row (adjust row numbers).
- Why: This caps the normal 0–500 points, then adds extra credit on top. If you want extra credit to also be capped differently, change the +SUM(...) part accordingly.

3) Faulty cell: SecA!J47 (and check similar rows)
- Error type: Inconsistency / possible manual override
- What’s wrong: J47 is hard-coded as 6 while other J rows use =SUM(Rx:AE x). That may be intentional, but likely an accidental hard entry.
- Fix: If it should follow the same pattern, change to =SUM(R47:AE47)
- Why: consistency and to avoid mismatches if assignment columns change.
