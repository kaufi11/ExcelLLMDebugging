Main error
- Cell C19 uses COUNT(C20:C34) instead of SUM(C20:C34).

Why it’s wrong
- COUNT counts how many numeric cells there are (it returns 15 for C20:C34), not the total hours. You need SUM to add the hours (the correct sum of C20:C34 is 10).

How it affects the sheet
- C19 is wrong (15) so the column total in C35 (and any other formulas that include C19) is inflated by 5 hours (15 − 10 = 5).

Fix (quick)
1. Select C19.
2. Replace the formula with =SUM(C20:C34) and press Enter.

What to check afterwards
- Verify C19 becomes 10 and C35 reduces by 5 (from 81 to 76 in the data you showed).
- Use Find (Ctrl+F) to search for COUNT( in the sheet to make sure no other accidental COUNTs are used where SUM is required.

Short tip
- Use AutoSum (Σ) or copy consistent SUM formulas down/across to avoid accidental function changes.
