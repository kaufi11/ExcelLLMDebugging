Summary of workbook structure and contents

Workbook contains three sheets: "Financial Statements" (primary), "5 Year review" (summary trends & ratios), and "Audit Report" (sign-off and auditor opinion).

1) Financial Statements (main)
- Layout: traditional financial statements laid out down the sheet:
  - Balance Sheet (starting at A2 / A5): columns for Notes (B), 2002 (C), 2001 (E). Currency label "R" in C8/E8.
  - Income Statement (A45 onward): revenue, other income, financial income, expenditure and surplus.
  - Cash Flow Statement (A105 onward): operating, investing and financing activities and reconciliation.
  - Notes to the financial statements (A149 onward): accounting policies (1.x), and detailed notes 2–20 (tangible assets, investments, borrowings, commitments, etc.).
- Formula patterns:
  - Heavy use of SUM over ranges (e.g. C13 = SUM(C14:C15); C17 = SUM(C18:C21); C54 = SUM(C55:C60); C61 = SUM(C62:C69); C78 = SUM(C79:C91); C92 = SUM(C93:C97)).
  - Cross-references within the sheet to accumulate totals (e.g. C23 = C17 + C13; C100 = C92 + C78; C102 = C74 - C100 gives surplus).
  - Cash-flow reconciling formulas (e.g. C117 = SUM(C122:C125); C144 = C136 + C129 + C117; C147 = C144 + C145).
  - Notes detail calculations (tangible assets movements: C234 = SUM(C235:C239); accumulated depreciation C241 = SUM(C242:C246); depreciation totals C289 = SUM(C284:C288)).
  - Some cells use simple + or - prefixes (e.g. C29 = +C330; many formulas link cells with explicit signs).
  - Several helper cells/columns (G/H) contain flags, adjustments or checks (e.g. H70, G121, G147).
- Key values (2002, column C):
  - Non-current assets (C13): 235,040 (C14 204,331 + C15 30,709).
  - Current assets (C17): 2,416,775 (sum of inventory, receivables, short-term investments 1,969,772, cash 270,997).
  - Total assets (C23): 2,651,815 (C17 + C13).
  - Subscriptions (C54): sum of member subscription lines (detailed in C55:C60).
  - Other operating income (C61): 1,302,683 (sum C62:C69).
  - Total revenue (C74): sum of revenue lines (C61 + C54 + C71).
  - Total expenditure (C100): 7,950,761 (administration C78 6,762,079 + other operating costs C92 1,188,682).
  - Surplus for year (C102): 675,124 (C74 - C100).
  - Accumulated funds (C330): 1,514,616 (sum of opening accumulated funds, entrance fees, surplus, Sanlam shares).
  - Equity (C28): 2,461,788 (sum of accumulated funds C29, development funds C30 899,525, national funds C31 47,647).
  - Non-current liabilities (C34): 39,324; Current liabilities (C36): 790,482.
  - Total equity & liabilities (C42 formula = C36 + C34 + C28).
- Notes & detail:
  - Detailed note sections covering accounting policies (1.1–1.10), tangible assets (Note 4 with movement schedules), short-term investments, inventory, pension, taxation, lease / borrowings (Note 11), guarantees, commitments, and financial instrument commentary.
- Important observation: the sheet contains inconsistencies on totals — Total assets (C23 = 2,651,815) does not match Total equity & liabilities (C42 computed from component items). This indicates either missing/incorrect links or mis-placed balances (common reconciliation risk to review).

2) 5 Year review
- Purpose: condensed five-year trend and ratio table (columns for 2002, 2001, 2000, 1999, 1998).
- Links back to main sheet for key items (e.g. C15 = 'Financial Statements'!C54; C16 = 'Financial Statements'!C61; C18 = 'Financial Statements'!C100; C32 = 'Financial Statements'!C117; C33 = 'Financial Statements'!C129; C34 = 'Financial Statements'!C136).
- Contains derived items: Net surplus (C19 = C17 - C18), balance-sheet snapshot (tangible assets, financial assets, net current assets), cash retained, net investment, financing activities, and ratios (e.g. capital & reserves to total assets, current assets to current liabilities).
- Membership/student statistics and growth figures are included.

3) Audit Report
- Static sheet with approval/signature lines and auditor's opinion (RSM Betty & Dickson), audit scope and final opinion that statements fairly present position as at 31 Dec 2002.
- Dates and signatories included (President, CEO, audit date 17 March 2003).

Overall layout and design notes
- Workbook is organized top-to-bottom in a report layout (financial statements first, then notes), using columns C (2002) and E (2001) for amounts and B for note references.
- Reusable patterns: consistent use of SUM ranges for roll-ups, negative values represented with minus signs, and cross-sheet references used in the 5 Year review.
- Potential issues to check: the balance sheet imbalance noted above, several cells with manual arithmetic rather than consistent formulas (e.g. some E-column entries are explicit sums like 103735.39+26083.05+11314.95), and a few helper/check values in G/H columns that may indicate reconciliation work-arounds.
