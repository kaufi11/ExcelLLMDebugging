High-level overview
- Workbook contains 3 sheets: "Select Financial Data", "Statements of Earnings", and "Cash Flow".
- Columns: years across the top (Select Financial Data: B = 2003 through K = 1994; Statements of Earnings: B = 2003 to F = 1999; Cash Flow: B = 2003 to K = 1994).
- All dollar amounts are "add 000" (thousands) except per-share lines.

Sheet: Select Financial Data
- Sections: Consolidated Operating Results (Net Sales, Freight, Total revenues, Costs, Earnings from operations, Interest, Taxes, Net Earnings), Basic/Diluted EPS, Cash Dividends per share, Condensed Consolidated Balance Sheet Data (current/noncurrent assets, liabilities, shareholders’ equity) and totals.
- Key formulas:
  - Revenue/Cost subtotals use SUM over component rows (e.g., Total revenues = SUM(Net Sales, Freight) in each year).
  - Gross/profit-like lines are simple differences (e.g., row12 = Total revenues - Cost of operations).
  - Earnings and tax lines use differences and SUMs (e.g., Earnings before taxes = Earnings from operations - Interest - Other nonoperating).
  - Balance sheet section totals computed as SUM of component asset or liability rows (e.g., Total = SUM(B44:B49); Total liabilities & equity = SUM(B51:B58)).
- Notable values: Net Sales 2003 = 1,537,869; Freight revenues 2003 = 212,425; Shareholders’ equity 2003 = 1,129,847.
- Minor inconsistency: H14 formula shown as =H12+H13 (addition) while other years use subtraction (likely a typo).

Sheet: Statements of Earnings
- Condensed income statement for 1999–2003 with the same structure: Net Sales, Freight, Total revenues, Costs, Other operating items, Earnings from operations, Interest, Other nonoperating, Taxes, Discontinued operations, Extraordinary/cumulative adjustments, Net Earnings.
- Key formulas: SUMs and subtractions to derive subtotals and net earnings (e.g., Total revenues = SUM(Net Sales, Freight); Earnings from continuing operations = Earnings from operations - Interest - Other nonoperating).
- EPS section: Basic and Diluted EPS are provided per year and net EPS computed by SUM of components (earnings before extraordinary + discontinued +/- cumulative effects).
- Notable differences vs. "Select Financial Data": Net Sales and some line items differ slightly (two separate data presentations).

Sheet: Cash Flow
- Sections: Cash Flows from Operating, Investing, Financing activities and reconciliation to Cash and Cash Equivalents (beginning and end of year).
- Key formulas:
  - Operating: Net earnings adjusted by non-cash items and working-capital changes; Net cash provided by operating activities = SUM(adjusted lines).
  - Investing/Financing: each uses SUM over component rows to get Net Cash Used/Provided (e.g., Net Cash Used for Investing Activities = SUM(B20:B23); Net Cash (Used for) Provided by Financing = SUM(B27:B38)).
  - Net change in cash = Operating + Investing + Financing (B41 = B17+B24+B39).
  - Beginning cash for each year references the previous year's "end of year" (e.g., B42 = C44, C42 = D44, etc.); End of year cash = SUM(Net change + Beginning).
- Notable values: Net earnings 2003 (cash-flow sheet) = 93,623; Depreciation/amortization 2003 = 139,606; Additions to PPE 2003 = -120,638.
- Cross-year linkage: beginning cash cells pull prior-year end cash, ensuring continuity (K42 is a fixed 2,084 for the earliest year).

General notes on formulas and layout
- The model uses consistent patterns: component rows → SUM for subtotal → arithmetic differences for margins/earnings → SUM for totals.
- Many rows repeat across sheets (revenues, freight, costs, EPS) but the two income-statement sheets are not identical — they present slightly different underlying amounts for comparison/condensed reporting.
- Totals and year-to-year cash continuity are implemented with simple SUM and direct cell references; watch the noted H14 inconsistency and any blank/partial entries in some investing/other rows (asterisks/notes present in original that may indicate footnotes).
