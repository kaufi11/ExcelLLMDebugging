Summary — main error
- Faulty cell: E7
- Error type: Typo / mechanical error (hard‑coded wrong number)
- Current formula: =E5*846
- Correct formula: =E5*E4

Why it matters (short)
- E7 should compute 4th‑quarter Sales = Price per unit (E5) * Units sold (E4). Using 846 instead of E4 gives a wrong quarter revenue.
- That wrong value flows into F7 (Total Sales) and then into F5 (ASP/unit), F6 (CGS/unit), all %-of‑sales (column G), Gross Margin, Taxes, Net Income — so many totals are incorrect.

Fix and explanation
1. Replace E7 with: =E5*E4
   - This makes 4th‑quarter revenue correctly use the 4th‑quarter units cell instead of a hard‑coded number.
2. After changing E7, Excel will automatically recalc F7, F5, F6, G7:G17, and all dependent totals.

Extra check (quick)
- Scan the sheet for any other hard‑coded numbers (like 846) in formulas; they are usually signs of copy/paste or typing mistakes.
- Optional: if you expect quarterly ASP/CGS columns, consider showing per‑quarter ASPs (B7/B4 etc.) instead of only totals in F5/F6.
