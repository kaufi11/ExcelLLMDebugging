Found an error — and how to fix it.

Error
- Cell F74 (Total Liab. & Equity on the PUSH‑DOWN section) contains: =COUNT(F67:F73)
  - This is wrong. It returns the count of nonblank cells, not the dollar total of liability & equity line items.
  - Because F74 is used in the proof formulas (e.g., D76 = D65 + D74 and consolidation proofs), the proofs and totals will be incorrect when F74 is a COUNT.

Solution
- Replace the formula in F74 with: =SUM(F67:F73)

Why this fixes it (brief)
- F74 must be the numeric sum of the component liability & equity lines F67:F73. Using SUM returns the correct dollar total. Once corrected, the proof cells that add balance-sheet totals to liability/equity totals (rows 76 and 134) will compute correctly and your debit/credit checks will balance.

Quick test after fix
1. Edit F74 to =SUM(F67:F73).
2. Confirm D76 = D65 + D74 and E76 = E65 + E74; they should reflect proper totals.
3. Confirm the consolidation proof D134 and E134 also reconcile.

Optional: scan the workbook for any other accidental COUNT usages on financial-total lines and replace with SUM if found.
