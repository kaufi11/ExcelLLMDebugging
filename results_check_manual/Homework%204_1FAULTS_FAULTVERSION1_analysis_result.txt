I reviewed the formulas on both sheets and found a few real errors / inconsistencies and a couple of questionable modeling choices. Below are the problems I found, the effect they cause, and recommended fixes.

Summary of definite formula errors
1) Sheet "Early or Late" — broken chain starting at row 32
- Problem: Cell B32 contains the formula =F31. All prior rows follow the pattern Bn = E(n-1). The formula in B32 therefore appears to be a typo.
- Effect: Because B32 is wrong, E32 becomes 0 and the entire early-saver chain from row 32 onward collapses to zeros (B32:E48 become wrong). This breaks the Early Saver projection beyond row 31.
- Fix: Change B32 to follow the pattern and reference the previous end balance:
  - Correct formula: B32 = E31

(After fixing B32 the subsequent B33 = E32, B34 = E33 etc. will propagate as intended.)

2) Sheet "Vanguard" — textual “formulas” (not real formulas) in D15:D21
- Problem: Cells D15–D21 contain literal text strings like "= Ending Balance - Beginning Balance", "= $23,833.35 - $10,000", "= $13,833.35", "= (Ending Balance - Beginning Balance) / Beginning Balance", etc. These are not real Excel formulas and therefore do not compute numeric results; they are text.
- Effect: Capital gain and holding-period-return are not being calculated from the sheet’s numbers; displayed values are static/text and may be out of sync or rounded.
- Fix: Replace the text with real formulas that reference the sheet cells. For example, assuming Beginning Balance is B4 and Ending Balance is F13:
  - Capital Gain (put into D16): =F13 - B4
  - Holding Period Return (put into D19 or another cell): =(F13 - B4) / B4
  - Then format the holding-period-return cell as decimal or percent as required (e.g., 138.33%).
- Optional: If you want separate cells showing the decimal and the percentage:
  - D20 (decimal) = (F13 - B4) / B4
  - D21 (percentage) = same formula, formatted as Percentage

Other inconsistencies / questionable modeling choices (not strictly “errors” but worth checking)
1) Vanguard: fee basis
- E4 (fee) is computed as =E2*(B4 - C4 + D4) — i.e., fee is charged on the balance after adding the period return (B - load + return). The return is computed on (B - load) via D4 = D2*(B4 - C4).
- Comment: It is unusual (but not invalid) to charge the fee on the post-return value; many models charge fees on beginning assets or on average assets. Confirm whether the fee should be computed on beginning balance (B4 - C4) rather than (B4 - C4 + D4). If you want fee on beginning assets, change the fee formula to =E2*(B4 - C4).

2) Vanguard: C4 uses =0*B4
- Not an error (returns 0) but stylistically odd; simply entering 0 would be clearer.

3) Early or Late: blank cells treated as zero
- Many Later-year deposit cells (C19:C48) for Early Saver are blank/omitted and thus treated as 0 by the formulas. That looks intentional (early saver deposits only first 15 years); verify that is intended. The Late Saver deposits (I19:I48) sum to 30,000, and Early Saver C4:C18 sum to 15,000 as confirmed by the Cash Outlay cells.

Other small notes
- Cell references like =F31 (the erroneous one) are not a #REF error, because column F exists; that can hide the typo (it returns blank/0) rather than producing an easily spotted #REF. This is why B32 silently became 0 — the reference pointed to an empty column instead of the intended E31.
