Error found:
In the "Financial summary" sheet, the Beginning Cash Balance row (row 4) has circular references:
- C4 = C33
- C33 = C4 + C30

This means C4 depends on C33, and C33 depends on C4, causing a circular reference error.

Explanation:
- Beginning Cash Balance (C4) should be the cash balance at the start of the year, a fixed number.
- Ending Cash/Inv. Balance (C33) is calculated as Beginning Cash Balance + Net Income (C30).
- Linking C4 to C33 creates a loop, which Excel cannot resolve.

Solution:
- Replace the formulas in C4, E4, G4, I4, K4, M4 with the actual beginning cash balances for each year.
- Do NOT link beginning balance to ending balance of the same year.
- Keep the formula in C33 as =C4 + C30 (Beginning balance + Net Income).
- This breaks the circular reference and correctly calculates ending balances.

Summary:
- Beginning Cash Balance cells (C4, E4, etc.) must be static input values, not formulas referencing ending balances.
- Ending Cash/Inv. Balance cells (C33, E33, etc.) should be calculated as Beginning Balance + Net Income.
- This fixes the circular reference error and ensures correct cash flow calculations.
