High-level summary

- Purpose: illustrate inventory/COGS errors and compare inventory costing methods (weighted average, FIFO, LIFO — periodic and perpetual, moving average) and show impacts on gross profit, taxes, net income and cash flows.

Sheet-by-sheet

1) Sheet: Errors
- Layout: three scenarios across columns B (Correct), D (Understating), F (Overstating). Rows grouped by Year 1 (error year) and Year 2 (subsequent year).
- Key constants (Year 1): Beginning Inventory B8/D8/F8 = 100,000; Purchases B9/D9/F9 = 500,000; Ending Inventory B11 = -150,000 (correct), D11 = -125,000 (understated), F11 = -175,000 (overstated).
- Formulas: Goods Available = SUM(B8:B9) etc.; COGS = Goods Available + Ending Inventory (they use SUM of the two rows where ending inventory is negative); Gross Profit = Sales + (-COGS); Taxes = -Pretax * 0.4; Net Income = Pretax + Taxes.
- Year 2: Beginning inventory is linked to prior year ending inventory (B27 = -B11 etc.), purchases update to 600,000, ending inventory held at -150,000 for Year 2. Resulting Year 2 COGS, gross profit, taxes and net income are calculated similarly.
- Purpose: show how misstated ending inventory in Year 1 shifts COGS and net income in Year 1 and reverses in Year 2.

2) Sheet: Valuing Ending Inventory
- Purpose: worked examples of inventory valuation methods with transaction list and computations for periodic and perpetual approaches.
- Transaction data (common to all sections):
  - Beginning purchases: 2001-12-05 — 1,000 units @ $5.00 (C8,D8)
  - 2001-12-28 — 6,000 units @ $5.25 (C9,D9)
  - Purchases: 2002-01-11 — 5,000 units @ $5.50 (C12,D12)
  - 2002-01-30 — 5,000 units @ $5.75 (C13,D13)
  - Sales: 2002-01-05 = -4,000 units (C17), 2002-01-25 = -7,000 units (C18)
- Sections:
  - Weighted Average (Periodic): computes total units and total cost (SUM of units and extended cost), average unit cost = total cost / total units, ending inventory and COGS computed using average.
  - FIFO Periodic and LIFO Periodic: tabulated goods available, units sold (negative), remaining units; ending inventory built from remaining lots; extended costs computed.
  - Moving Average (Perpetual): updates average after each purchase and applies current average to each sale; tracks COGS accumulated (F column).
  - FIFO Perpetual and LIFO Perpetual: similar step-by-step layer usage for each sale, then aggregate to ending inventory and COGS.
- Formula patterns: extended cost = units * unit cost; running totals via SUM ranges; many cells use absolute row references (I$8, I$9) to pull beginning/purchase subtotals. Sales are entered as negative unit quantities to subtract from layers.
- Notes / Issues: Several cells show placeholders or questionable formulas:

  - Some references for ending inventory expense K41/K57/K76 etc point to negative sums (e.g., =-E47) — check sign conventions.
- Comments: the sheet demonstrates differences in ending inventory and COGS by method; uses both periodic and perpetual computations.
