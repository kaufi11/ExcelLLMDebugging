Summary — workbook structure and key formulas (concise)

Overall layout
- Three sheets (Sheet1, Sheet2, Sheet3) show the same Income Statement / Balance Sheet layout for three years (2001, 2002, 2003). Year headings are in C5 (2001), E5 (2002), G5 (2003). Income statement lines sit above (sales, COGS, gross profit, operating expenses, net income). Balance sheet (assets, liabilities, equity) is below.
- Inventory values link between the Income Statement (ending inventory reduces COGS) and the Balance Sheet (Inventory asset = ending inventory). Totals use SUM formulas.

Sheet1 — "Inventory error example"
- Purpose: interactive example (A2) — change beginning/ending inventory to see impact on COGS and net income.
- Key cells / formulas:
  - Beginning inventory: B8 = 165000, D8 = =B11, F8 = =D11 (beginning = prior year ending).
  - Purchases: B9, D9, F9 (given numbers).
  - Cost of goods available: B10 = B8+B9, D10 = D8+D9, F10 = F8+F9.
  - Ending inventory: B11 = 179800, D11 = 147200, F11 = 203970 (static in Sheet1).
  - COGS: C12 = B10-B11, E12 = D10-D11, G12 = F10-F11.
  - Gross profit: C13 = C6-C12, etc.
  - Net income: C15 = C13-C14, etc.
- Balance sheet links:
  - Inventory asset: B21 = B11, D21 = D11, F21 = F11 (copies ending inventory).
  - Total assets: C24 = SUM(B20:B23), E24 = SUM(D20:D23), G24 = SUM(F20:F23).
  - Total liabilities: C29 = SUM(B27:B28), etc.
  - Retained earnings: B33 = C15, D33 = E15, F33 = G15 (retained earnings = current year net income).
  - Total equity: C34 = SUM(B32:B33), etc.
  - Totals check: C35 = SUM(C29:C34), etc.
- Issue: Sheet1 does not balance — changing inventory alters both COGS/net income and the inventory asset, but prior-year retained earnings were not adjusted for any prior-period inventory error, so Total Assets ≠ Total Liabilities & Equity by the amount of the inventory error.

Sheet2 — shows the raw error detail (7-4B)
- Same structure as Sheet1 but ending inventory cells include explicit error adjustments:
  - B11 = 179800+17000 (inflated by +17,000), D11 = 147200-25000 (reduced by 25,000), F11 = 203970.
- All other links same as Sheet1 (retained earnings = current net income).
- Result: the balance sheet remains out of balance by the error difference — Sheet2 demonstrates the uncorrected errors carried into the statements.

Sheet3 — corrected accounting treatment (prior-year error closed to retained earnings)
- Same statement layout, but errors are handled by adjusting retained earnings (prior-year results) rather than leaving them in current-year BS accounts:
  - Ending inventory formulas: B13 = 179800+17000, D13 = 147200-25000, F13 = 203970 (these are the ending inventories used to compute COGS).
  - Beginning inventory: B10 = 165000, D10 = =B13, F10 = =D13 (beginning = prior period ending).
  - COGS and gross profit follow from these inventory values.
  - Retained earnings adjustments: B35 = C17, D35 = E17 + 17000, F35 = G17 - 25000 — prior-year inventory errors are closed into retained earnings (2002 adds back +17,000; 2003 subtracts 25,000).
  - Totals: C36/C37 etc use SUM as before.
- Result: Balance sheet balances because prior-year inventory errors have been reflected in retained earnings, aligning Assets with Liabilities + Equity.

What to look at / change
- Change beginning or ending inventory numbers (e.g., B11, D11, F11 or the formulas in Sheet2/Sheet3) and observe:
  - Immediate effect on COGS (C12/E12/G12), gross profit, net income (C15/E15/G15) and inventory asset (B21/D21/F21).
  - Whether retained earnings have been adjusted (Sheet3) — if not, the balance sheet will be out of balance by the error amount (as illustrated in Sheet1/Sheet2).
