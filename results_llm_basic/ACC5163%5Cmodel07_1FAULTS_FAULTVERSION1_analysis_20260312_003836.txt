I reviewed MODEL07 and found two clear formula errors on the PUSH‑DOWN WORKSHEET area (rows 54–74). These errors will make the new‑basis totals wrong.

Errors
1. Cell F67 (Payables & accruals) — formula is = (B67+C67-D67+E67).  
   Why it’s wrong: the push‑down worksheet’s New Basis (column F) should be based only on the old balance (column C) plus/minus the adjusting entries (columns D and E). For liability accounts the sign convention is Old − Dr + Cr (liabilities normally have credit balances). F67 incorrectly includes column B and has the wrong signs.

2. Cell F69 (Long‑term debt) — formula is the constant =2.  
   Why it’s wrong: it should be a calculated new balance (like the other liability/equity lines), not a hardcoded constant.

Fixes (what to change)
- Change F67 to:
  =(C67 - D67 + E67)
- Change F69 to:
  =(C69 - D69 + E69)

Why these fixes work (short)
- On the push‑down worksheet:
  - For asset accounts New = Old + Dr − Cr (that pattern is used in rows 54–65).
  - For liability & equity accounts New = Old − Dr + Cr (because Dr reduces a liability or equity, Cr increases it).
- The corrected formulas implement the correct sign convention and exclude column B (which is not part of the push‑down new‑basis calculation).

After you change F67 and F69, check F74 (Total Liab & Equity) and any dependent totals (D76, E76, etc.) — they should now balance properly.
