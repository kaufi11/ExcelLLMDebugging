Summary — the sheet has a sign error in Year 2 expenses.

What’s wrong
- Row 37 (the Year 2 "-Expenses" cells B37, D37 and F37) are entered as +125,000 (positive). Because your pretax line (B38/D38/F38) is SUM(Gross, Expenses) this adds the expense instead of subtracting it. That overstates pretax income and net income by exactly the expense amount × (1) (and taxes also change).
- Consequence (Correct → Erroneous):
  - Correct Year 2 Net Income (column B) should be 225,000. With the sign error it shows 375,000 (an overstatement of 150,000).
  - Similar +150,000 overstatements occur in the Understated/Overstated columns.

Why this is an error
- Expenses reduce profit. In your sheet you use negative numbers to represent deductions (e.g., Year 1 expenses = -100,000). In Year 2 you mistakenly entered 125,000 as positive, so the SUM(Gross, Expenses) adds it instead of subtracting it.

How to fix (two quick options)
1) Simple edit (recommended)
   - Change B37, D37 and F37 from 125000 to -125000 (type a minus sign before the number).
2) Paste Special (if many cells)
   - Put -1 in a spare cell, copy it, select B37, D37, F37 → Paste Special → Multiply → OK. This multiplies those cells by -1 and flips their sign.

What the fix does (after change)
- Column B Year 2:
  - Pretax = 375,000
  - Taxes = -150,000
  - Net Income = 225,000 (correct)
- Columns D and F will similarly show the correct (lower) pretax and net income numbers.
