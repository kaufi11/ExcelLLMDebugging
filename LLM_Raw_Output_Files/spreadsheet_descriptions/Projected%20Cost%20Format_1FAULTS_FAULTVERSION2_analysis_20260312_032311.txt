Summary of workbook: three related grant-cost projection forms (one per proposed grant year) for sequencing centers. Each sheet collects specified cost categories, computes direct/indirect totals and percentages, and links to “grand totals” used to derive average cost per read/base.

Sheets
1) Seq Production Costs (Sheet 1: Sequence production projected costs)
- Purpose: capture shotgun reads, Q20 bases, finished basepairs, plus “other activities” (BAC mapping, BAC-end seq, etc.), and compute average cost per read/base.
- Layout:
  - Header & instructions in A1:A8; grant year and center name in A4:A5.
  - Main data columns:
    - B — Shotgun (WGS and BAC-by-BAC) reads (counts and $).
    - C — Q20 Bases (counts and % formulas).
    - D — Finished Basepairs (counts and $/%).
    - G — Other Activities (text list in G10; $ and % in G columns).
  - Key rows:
    - Row 10: One year totals (B10 = total attempted reads; C10 = total Q20 bases; D10 = finished bases; G10 = list of other activities).
    - Row 11: Average cost calculations:
      - B11 = B21 / B10 (avg cost per shotgun read)
      - C11 = B21 / C10 (avg cost per Q20 base)
      - D11 = D21 / D10 (avg cost per finished basepair)
    - Rows 13–18: Cost categories (Amortized Equipment, Labor, Materials & Reagents, Sub-contracts, Travel, Other). Users enter $ in B, D, G columns; percent columns C, E, H are calculated as category / grand total (e.g., C13 = B13 / $B$21).
    - Row 19: Total Direct Costs: B19 = SUM(B13:B18), D19 = SUM(D13:D18), G19 = SUM(G13:G18) with corresponding % formulas.
    - Row 20: Indirect Costs (entered as $ in B20/D20/G20); % formulas reference grand totals.
    - Row 21: Grand Cost Totals:
      - B21 = SUM(B19:B20), D21 = SUM(D19:D20)
      - C21, E21, H21 set to 1 (100%); G21 = COUNT(G19:G20) (counts presence of other activity totals)
    - Row 23: One Year Total Costs (all activities): B23 = B21 + D21 + G21 (sum of grand totals)
- Notes: gray/pre-set cells contain formulas — do not overwrite. Equipment amortization is required over 3 years per instructions.

2) Tech Dev Costs (Sheet 2: Technology Development projected costs)
- Purpose: capture costs requested for technology development (improving throughput/quality, protocol implementation, etc.).
- Layout:
  - Header & instructions in A1:A7; total requested for year in B6 (initially 0).
  - Cost categories listed in A8:A14 (Equipment amortized 3 yrs, Labor, Supplies, Sub-contracts, Travel, Other incl. loan repayment).
  - B9:B16 — enter $ amounts for each category.
  - Key formulas:
    - B17 = SUM(B9:B16) (Total Direct Costs / Total Tech Dev Costs)
    - B18 = B17 / B6 (% of total costs) — B6 must contain the overall total for the year.
  - A19: Contribution to total read cost ($) — user-entered linkage field.

3) Bioinformatics Costs (Sheet 3: Bioinformatics projected costs)
- Purpose: capture infrastructure, assembly, and annotation costs (each a separate subsection) and compute totals/percentages and contributions to read costs.
- Layout:
  - Header & instructions in A1:A7; total requested for year in B6 (initially 0).
  - Infrastructure section (A7:A16):
    - B9:B16 — $ for Equipment (amort 3 yrs), Labor, Supplies, Sub-contracts, Travel, Other.
    - B17 = SUM(B9:B16) (Infrastructure total)
    - B18 = B17 / $B$6 (percent of total)
    - A19: Contribution to total read costs ($) — user-entered.
  - Assembly section (A20:A30):
    - B22:B29 — $ for Assembly equipment, labor, supplies, etc.
    - B30 = SUM(B22:B29) (Assembly total)
    - B31 = B30 / $B$6 (percent)
    - A32: Contribution to total read costs ($)
  - Annotation section (A33:A43):
    - B35:B42 — $ for Annotation equipment, labor, supplies, etc.
    - B43 = SUM(B35:B42) (Annotation total)
    - B44 = B43 / $B$6 (percent)
    - A45: Contribution to total read costs ($)
- Notes: same amortization conventions apply; many cells expect user entry and compute totals/percentages.

General notes and key formulas
- Equipment amortization: amortized over 3 years; amortized amounts are entered in Equipment rows.
- Percent columns compute category / grand total for that column (e.g., in Seq Production, C13 = B13 / $B$21).
- Grand totals:
  - Seq Production: B21 and D21 are the main grand totals for shotgun and finished categories; B23 sums B21 + D21 + G21 to give one-year total requested.
  - Tech Dev and Bioinformatics use SUM formulas for section totals (B17, B30, B43) and divide by B6 to get % of total.
- User inputs required: grant year, center name, numeric cost entries in non-gray cells (cost categories and one-year totals), list of other activities in Seq Production G10, and overall total requested in B6 on sheets 2 & 3 (used for % calculations).
