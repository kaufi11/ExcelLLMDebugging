Summary of errors found
1) Critical formula error (causes wrong 1997 income items)
- Location: Sheet1 (Consolidated Statements of Income), cell H6 (Gross profit for 1997).
- Problem: H6 is =H5−H5 (always 0). It should be Gross profit = Net Sales − Cost of sales (i.e. =H4−H5).
- Effect: Gross profit, Operating profit, Income before taxes and Net income for 1997 are wrong; that cascades to %‑of‑sales rows and any downstream calculations that use those numbers.

Corrected values for 1997 (after fixing H6 = H4−H5)
- Gross profit (H6) = 2,669,534 − 1,528,364 = 1,141,170
- Operating profit (H9) = 1,141,170 − 874,834 = 266,336
- Income before income taxes (H16) = 266,336 + (Other, H15 = −31,531) = 234,805
- Income before cumulative effect (H20) = 234,805 − (Income taxes 109,364 + Minority interests 6,628) = 118,813
(Notice H20 = 118,813 now matches the cash‑flow sheet net income for 1997.)

- Or copy a correct formula from a neighboring cell (e.g., C6) and fill right across the row so all years use the same pattern: select C6 (or a correct cell) → drag or Ctrl+R to fill through N6.
- After fixing, recalculate (usually automatic) and verify downstream rows (H7, H9, H16, H20, H22, H23) update correctly.
