Overview
- Workbook has 16 sheets; only Sheet1 contains data (Sheet2–Sheet16 present but empty).
- Sheet1 is a 2003/04 financial summary for "NHS HEALTH SCOTLAND / SCOTLAND AGAINST DRUGS" (values in £k). Date of preparation: 2 September 2003.

Layout
- Column A: section and line-item labels (Campaign, Administration, Challenge Fund, Public Relations, School Programme, Community Programme, Business Programme, totals; then Challenge Fund summary and Education Sector Initiative, and a Note).
- Column C: Annual allocation (numeric, £k).
- Column E: Year-to-date actual (labelled “4 month period” / actual).
- Column G: Remaining allocation (intended as allocation minus actual).
- Rows group items into sections with subtotals and grand totals. Many rows contain subtotals and totals.

Key items and structure
- Top labels: A1–A4 (title and date). E5–G7 contain column headings: “Year to date / Annual / actual: / Remaining / allocation / 4 month period / allocation”.
- Main campaign header at A8; detailed line items start at A10 (Staffing) and continue through A61 (Total campaign expenditure).
- Sections and their subtotal rows:
  - Administration (A12–A24). C24, E24, G24 = subtotals.
  - Challenge Fund administration (A26–A30). C30, E30, G30 = subtotals.
  - Public Relations (A32–A37). C37, E37, G37 = subtotals.
  - School Programme (A39–A45). C45, E45, G45 = subtotals.
  - Community Programme (A47–A51). C51, E51, G51 = subtotals.
  - Business Programme (A53–A59). C59, E59, G59 = subtotals.
  - Total campaign expenditure: row 61 aggregates the above subtotals.

- Scottish Drugs Challenge Fund (A65–A71): gross expenditure (C67/E67), income from donations (C69), and net expenditure (C71/E71/G71 = sums of gross + income).
- Education Sector Initiative (A73–A81): itemised lines C75–C80 with totals in C81/E81/G81.
- Note about donations at rows A83–A86: mentions £20,896.08 held in trust fund not included in figures.

Formulas and calculation logic
- Remaining column (G) is intended to be C - E. Most G cells use formulas in the form =SUM(Cx-Ex) (SUM used unnecessarily around a simple subtraction). Examples: G10 = SUM(C10-E10), G13 = SUM(C13-E13), etc.
- Subtotals use SUM over ranges:
  - e.g., C24 = SUM(C13:C23), E24 = SUM(E13:E23), G24 = SUM(G13:G23).
- Section totals aggregated by SUM of the individual subtotal cells in some places:
  - C61 = SUM(C10+C24+C30+C37+C45+C51+C59) — note this uses SUM around several additions instead of a range.
  - E61 and G61 use analogous SUM(...) of the subtotal cells.
- Net expenditure rows use addition: C71 = SUM(C67+C69), E71 = SUM(E67+E69).
- Irregularities to note:
  - The use of SUM around single subtraction expressions is redundant (e.g., =SUM(C13-E13) same as =C13-E13).
  - Row G19 is hard-coded as =3 rather than computed from C19 and E19.
  - Several E cells are blank (no YTD actual), so remaining amounts equal the full allocation in those rows.
  - Donations/income included as a negative value in C69 (C69 = -8).

Overall comments (concise)
- Sheet1 is a standard budget vs actual layout: Annual allocation (C), YTD actual (E) for a 4-month period, Remaining (G = C - E), with sectional subtotals and overall totals.
- Formulas are mostly correct but use redundant SUM wrappers and include one hard-coded G19. Some E values are missing (blanks). Other sheets are empty.
