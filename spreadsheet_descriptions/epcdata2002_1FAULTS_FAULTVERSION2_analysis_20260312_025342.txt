High-level overview
- Workbook is an education statistics dashboard (EPC online database) with a Contents sheet linking numbered tables to individual sheets (schools, teachers, qualifications, ICT, spending).
- Layout common pattern: rows = subjects/categories, columns = years or geographies; many sheets use formulas to compute percent changes, sums, per-head figures and cross-sheet totals.

Sheet-by-sheet summary (key contents and formulas)
1. Contents
  - Index of tables/sheets (Table No. → Title).

2. Schools & pupils
  - Numbers of schools by type and country (UK, England, Wales, Scotland, N. Ireland) and pupil counts (000s).
  - Key values: total schools C11 = 34,914; total pupils C21 = 10,107 (000s).

3. Teachers
  - Full-time equivalents and full/part-time headcount in England by year (1996–2002).
  - Key values: FTE total J11 = 417 (thousands) in 2002; full-time headcount J16 = 380.8; provisional notes and exclusions.

4. Teacher Trainees
  - Recruitment to initial teacher training (1997/98–2001/02) by sector and detailed secondary-subject breakdown.

5. GCSE entries
  - Subject rows with entries by year (1995–2002). Two %‑change columns and "% of total 2002".
  - Formulas: percent change e.g. =(I4-C4)/C4, % of total =I4/$I$80. "Check" rows use SUM over subject ranges.
  - Special: Science Combined computed from Double Award and Single Award: = (I54*2)+I56.

6. A Level entries
  - Same structure as GCSE (1995–2002), % change formulas use base years e.g. =(I4-B4)/B4, % of total using =$I$68, plus a check SUM row.

7. AS Level entries
  - AS data for 2001–2002 with % change and % of total formulas referencing C$68.

8–10. GNVQ Foundation / Intermediate / Advanced
  - Legacy vs new (Curriculum 2000) counts by subject and year, % change formulas and totals. Check rows sum ranges.

11. ICT Survey (England)
  - Time series (1998–2002) for % schools connected, avg computers per school, pupils per computer, avg expenditure per school; notable values: 2002 Internet connectivity primary & all schools = >99%; avg computers per school (All) 2002 = 50; avg expenditure per school (All) 2002 = £24,800.

12. ICT Expenditure (England 2001–02)
  - Expenditure split by school type and category (hardware, support, internet, software, training). Total expenditure (all schools) E6 = £563m; avg per school E16 = £24,800; avg per pupil E26 = £69.

13. Use & benefit of ICT (England)
  - 2002 survey: percent "Substantial/Some/None" use & perceived benefit by curriculum area for primary and secondary.

14. Spending summary (EPC)
  - EPC schoolbook survey mean number on roll, mean total spent and spend per head by country and school type (primary/secondary). Example: primary England (LEA) mean total spent E8 = £3,555; spend/head G8 = £17.69.

15. Total Spending (estimated)
  - Computes national total by multiplying mean spend (from survey) by number of schools: D8 formula = C8 * B8 (and analogous rows). Totals use SUM.

16. Primary Spend by Subject (EPC)
  - Mean amount spent by subject and region, plus mean per head by subject. Totals and mean spend/head presented; row B38 gives mean spend/head values.

17. Primary estimated total by subject
  - Uses number of schools from 'Total Spending (estimated)' and per-school subject means from 'Primary Spend by Subject (EPC)'. Example formula: B8 = B$7 * 'Primary Spend by Subject (EPC)'!B7. Totals sum across regions.

18. Secondary spend by Subject (EPC)
  - Same structure as primary but for secondary schools: mean amounts by subject and per-head values; totals and mean spend/head provided.

19. Secondary est. total by subject
  - Same approach as primary estimated totals: multiplies regional number of secondary schools by subject mean spend and sums to totals (formulas reference 'Total Spending (estimated)' and 'Secondary spend by Subject- EPC').

Common formula types & cross-sheet flows
- Arithmetic and aggregations: SUM ranges (check rows), direct multiplications (mean × count), and simple percent-change calculations e.g. =(new-old)/old.
- Cross-sheet references are frequent (e.g., primary/secondary estimated-by-subject sheets pull B7 values from 'Total Spending (estimated)' and per-subject means from 'Primary/Secondary Spend by Subject').
- Percent-of-total uses absolute references to TOTAL cells (e.g., =I4/$I$80 or =I4/$I$68).
- Checks: many sheets include "check" rows that SUM subject ranges to validate totals (help detect inconsistencies because of double-counting notes, e.g., Science Double Award counted twice).

Notes and special items
- Science Double Award: counted as two GCSE entries; Combined formula doubles double-award count and adds single-award.
- Navigation: several sheets include "Back to contents" cell.
- Numerous provenance notes cite DfES or Joint Council for General Qualifications.
