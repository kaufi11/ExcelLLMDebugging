Overview: The workbook is a multi-sheet statistical report of Standard Grade, Higher Grade and Certificate of Sixth Year Studies (CSYS) exam entries, awards, grade distributions, sex breakdowns, per‑candidate stats and appeals for 1998–2002. Each sheet follows a consistent layout: a title/header, subjects listed down column A, numeric data across columns (years, grades, elements) and summary rows with SUM or percentage formulas.

Key sheets and contents (concise):

- SG1 — "TREND IN ENTRIES FOR EACH SUBJECT AT STANDARD GRADE, 1998 TO 2002"
  - Layout: subjects in A7:A51, columns B–F = entries by year 1998–2002, column G = percent change 2001→2002.
  - Typical formula: G7 (and other G rows) = (F7–E7)/E7.
  - Subtotals (row 53): B53:E53 use SUM over B7:B51 etc.; F53 uses COUNT(F7:F51) (note: F53 is a COUNT, not a SUM); G53 = (F53–E53)/E53.
  - Totals (rows 55–63) include optional Writing elements and B63:F63 summations; G63 = (F63–E63)/E63.
  - Data note: many subjects have multi‑year counts (e.g., English ~59,901 in 2002).

- SG2 — "ENTRIES, AWARDS AND NUMBERS OF CENTRES FOR EACH SUBJECT AT STANDARD GRADE, 2002"
  - Layout: column B = total entries (2002), columns C–I = awards at grades 1–7, column J = number of centres.
  - Subtotals row 51 use SUM(B6:B50), SUM(C6:C50) etc.
  - Row 52 converts subtotal counts to percentages by dividing each grade sum by total entries: e.g. C52 = C51 / $B$51.
  - Totals rows (54–62) add the optional writing elements into the overall totals; row 62 aggregates these with formulas summing the writing tables + row 51 (see B62..F62 formulas).
  - Key values: large subject entries (e.g., English 59,901; Mathematics 59,047; All‑subjects subtotal B51 used later).

- SG3 — "PERCENTAGE GRADE DISTRIBUTION FOR EACH SUBJECT AND ELEMENT AT STANDARD GRADE, 2002"
  - Layout: subject/element rows with C = ENTRIES and D–J = % at grades 1–7, K = % no award.
  - Percentages appear precomputed to many decimals (example: English D11 = 12.5407%, E11 = 30.5521%, etc.).
  - The sheet also includes element breakdowns (Reading/Writing/Listening/Speaking/Knowledge & Understanding, etc.) for many subjects and an All‑subjects total in A177: entries 490,485 and overall % distribution (D177–K177).
  - Notes: Writing elements mark that awards are only at grades 1–4.

- SG4a (Male) and SG4b (Female) — "ENTRIES AND AWARDS BY SEX"
  - Layout: columns B = entries by sex, C = % of all entries, D–J = counts at grades 1–7; subtotals rows give total male/female counts and overall percentages.
  - Subtotal example: B52 (male subtotals) = 229,984; B52/$B52 used to produce percentages.
  - SG4c consolidates male/female: total entries, % male/female, and comparative columns showing % of male entries achieving grade bands (e.g., columns E–J present 1–2, 1–4, 1–6 rates for male and female).

- SG5 — "PER‑CANDIDATE STATISTICS (2002)"
  - SG5a: number of entries per candidate. Key totals: total candidates = 62,521 (B17), total entries = 456,288 (B18), entries per candidate ≈ 7.298 (B19).
  - SG5b–d: awards per candidate at different level groupings (credit-level 1–2, credit+general 1–4, credit+general+foundation 1–6), with cumulative distributions and awards/candidate. Example: awards/cand for credit (1–2) = 3.166; for 1–4 = 6.106; for 1–6 = 7.066.

- SG6 — "TREND IN APPEALS AND THEIR SUCCESS AT STANDARD GRADE, 1998 TO 2002"
  - Columns B–F = years 1998–2002. Key values: number of appeals in 2002 = 46,770; successful appeals in 2002 = 24,288; success rate ~0.52 (52% of appeals).

- HG1+2+3 — Higher Grade summary sheets
  - HG1: trend in entries 1998–2002 per subject (same layout as SG1). Example English: entries drop to 469 in 2002.
  - HG2: trend in pass rates 1998–2002 (percent pass rates per year per subject).
  - HG3: entries & awards for 2002 at Higher (columns C–G show A–E grades or passes). Example: English B29 = 469 entries; passes formula F29 = SUM(C29:E29).

- HG4a/b/c — Higher grade awards by sex and combined percentages
  - HG4a (male) and HG4b (female) show entries and grade counts by sex; HG4c combines into male/female % comparisons. Small cohorts (e.g., 469 English entries split 234 male /235 female).

- HG5 — Higher Grade candidate age profiles and pass‑rate summaries (2002)
  - Breakdown by age with average entries, average passes and pass rates. Totals: 469 candidates; all‑ages pass rate ~53.7% (F17).

- HG6 — "TREND IN APPEALS AND THEIR SUCCESS AT HIGHER GRADE"
  - Key: 2002 appeals = 69; successful = 25; success rate ~36%.

- CS1+2, CS3abc, CS5 — Certificate of Sixth Year Studies (CSYS)
  - CS1: trend in entries 1998–2002 per subject (e.g., English falls to 423 in 2002).
  - CS2: entries and awards for 2002 with grade counts A–E and centres column; example English B19 = 423; C19 = 54 A‑grades.
  - CS3a/b/c: male/female/all breakdowns and % grade A–C; CS3c shows e.g. English: total 423, % grade A (male 14%, female 12%), % A–C ~94%/92%.
  - CS5: appeals trend for CSYS 1998–2002. Key: 2002 appeals = 19; successful = 9; success rate ~47%.

Common formulas and patterns used across the workbook:
- Percent change year-to-year: (current year – previous year) / previous year, e.g. =(F7–E7)/E7.
- Sums: =SUM(range) for totals and subtotals (e.g., SUM(B7:B51), SUM(C6:C50)).
- Percentages: grade counts / total entries, e.g. =C51 / $B$51 or =C51/$B$51; many exact percentages are precomputed.
- Counts vs sums: note an unusual use in SG1 F53 = COUNT(F7:F51) (counts nonblank cells) rather than SUM; check if that was intentional.
- Aggregation of optional Writing elements is done by adding the writing rows into main subtotals (SG2 B62 = B60 + ... + B51 pattern).

Formatting/layout conventions:
- Each sheet starts with a title in A1 and candidate group in A3 (e.g., "ALL CANDIDATES", "MALE", "FEMALE").
- Years/grades/elements run across columns; subjects/elements down rows.
- Summary rows (Subtotals, Totals, Percentages) are at predictable positions (often near the bottom of the subject lists).
