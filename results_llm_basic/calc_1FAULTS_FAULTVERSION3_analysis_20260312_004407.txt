Problems found (short):

1) Cell C20 on "Your Company" (Average Interest Expense) is 0 but should show the calculated cash‑flow interest amount. The print sheet ("Text Only") reads C20, so it shows 0 too.  
2) Cell C41 on "Your Company" (Total Possible INzone Savings) is hard‑coded to 7. It should be the sum of the savings lines.  
3) Because the percentage input cells (B25, B28, B31, B34, B37) are 0, most component values are zero — make sure you enter your percentages.

Why these are errors:
- C20 being blank/zero means the reported average interest expense on the print sheet is wrong. In the Sample sheet this value equals the Cash Flow/Inventory Cost of Money calculation.  
- C41 = 7 is clearly a placeholder; the total must sum the calculated savings components, otherwise the total is meaningless.

Fixes (exact steps and formulas):

1) Fix Average Interest Expense:
- In "Your Company" cell C20 enter either:
  =C13/C14*C15*C17
  or simply
  =C22
This makes C20 match the Cash Flow/Inventory Cost of Money calculation (same as the Sample).

2) Fix Total Possible INzone Savings:
- In "Your Company" cell C41 enter a sum of the savings components. For example:
  =SUM(C22,C24,C27,C30,C33,C36,C39)
  (or =SUM(C22:C39) if there are no unrelated numbers in that range)
This replaces the hard-coded 7 with the actual total.

3) Enter your input percentages:
- Put your actual percentages into B25 (obsolete), B28 (waste), B31 (exports), B34 (returns), B37 (zone transfers). If they remain 0 the results will be 0.

Result:
- After steps 1–3 the "Text Only" sheet will pull the correct Average Interest Expense (C11) and correct Total Possible INzone Savings (C32), and all component amounts will reflect your entered percentages.
