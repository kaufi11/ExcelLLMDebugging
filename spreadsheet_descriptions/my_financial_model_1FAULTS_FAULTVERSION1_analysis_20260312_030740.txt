High-level summary — workbook models a lemonade business over years (columns E:P). Key inputs are in column D; calculations roll forward across years. Main sheets: Income, Balance Sheet, Cashflow, Depreciation, simple income, RoadMap.

1) Income (primary P&L / monthly model)
- Columns: E:P = years 2002–2013 (header in row 2). Column D contains key input rates and constants.
- Key inputs (column D):
  - Growth in cups (D3) = 0.05 (5% per period)
  - Price inflation (D4) = 0.08/12 (8% annual / monthly cell)
  - Sugar per cup (D5) = 0.2 cups
  - Lemons per cup (D6) = 1
  - Lemon price inflation (D7) = 0.08/12
  - Sugar price inflation (D8) = 0.08/12
  - Online sales share (D10) = 0.4; Street share (D11) = 1 - D10
- Volumes & prices:
  - E3 = 200,000 cups; subsequent years multiply prior year by (1 + $D$3).
  - E4 = $4.00 per cup; subsequent years grow by (1 + $D$4).
  - E7 (lemon $0.55) and E8 (sugar $0.375) grow by (1 + inflation) across years.
- Revenue:
  - Online revenue = $D$10 * cups * price (e.g., E10 = $D$10*E3*E4). Street revenue uses D11.
  - Totals (monthly) in row 12; annualized in row 13 = monthly * 12.
- COGS:
  - Lemons (row 15) = lemons per cup * cups * lemon price (E15 = E6*E7).
  - Sugar (row 16) = sugar per cup * cups * sugar price (E16 = E8*E5).
  - Totals in row 17.
- Gross profit (row 19) = Revenue (row12) - COGS (row17); annualized in row 20.
- Fixed costs (rows 22–28): manager ($7,500), credit card ($75), web-hosting ($500 in D24 used across years), advertising ($2,500) with some copy-formula propagation, insurance ($250), benefits = E22*0.25, legal = $50,000. Row 29 sums fixed costs.
- Depreciation (row 31) pulled from Depreciation!E7:P7.
- Net profit (row 33) = gross profit - fixed costs - depreciation. Annualized in row 34.
- Notes: Several SUM formulas sum a single cell (e.g., SUM(E10:E10)); many columns copy formulas across.

4) Depreciation
- Header links to Income.
- Pulls asset info from Balance Sheet for "Value at Beginning" (D6 = Balance Sheet D8).
- Depreciable life D7 = 7 (years).
- Annual depreciation (row 7) = IF(value at beginning > 0, $D$6 / $D$7, 0). So straight-line depreciation based on the beginning value and 7-year life.
- Value at end (row 8) = beginning - depreciation.
- Total accumulated depreciation (row 11) sums year-to-date depreciation (cumulative across columns).
- Depreciation values are used back in Income (E31:P31), Cashflow (row 7), and Balance Sheet (E9:P9 as accumulated depreciation and E10:P10 as net book value).
