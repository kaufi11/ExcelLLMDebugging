Summary of workbook structure and contents (concise)

General layout
- Columns C:R cover four years (2000–2003) by quarter: C–F = 2000 (Q1–Q4), G–J = 2001, K–N = 2002, O–R = 2003. Row 3 lists quarter names; B4 notes units = Millions of yen.
- Common formula patterns: SUM for totals, subtraction for margins (Net Sales − Cost = Gross profit), IF tests to avoid divide-by-zero for percent-of-sales (two variants: IF(...,"-  ",...) on Sheet1 and IF(...,"",...) on Sheet2).
- Notes/footnotes about accounting standard changes and restatements appear at bottom of sheets.

Sheet 0 — Table of Contents
- Simple index pointing to Sheet 1 (“Quarterly Statement (1): Sales by Product and P&L; Sales by Region”) and Sheet 2 (“Quarterly Statement (2): Segment Information by Product”).

Sheet 1 — 1. Quarterly Statement (1)
- Section: Sales by Product and P&L
  - Product lines: Business machines (broken into Office imaging products, Computer peripherals, Business information products), Cameras, Optical & other.
  - Row C12:R12 = Business machines total (FORMULA =SUM(<three product rows>)), though note H12 incorrectly uses COUNT(H9:H11) instead of SUM.
  - Row C15:R15 = Total net sales (SUM of Business machines total, Cameras, Optical).
  - Net sales repeated in Row 17 (Net Sales*1) by simple reference to totals above.
  - Cost of sales (Row 18) are input numbers. Gross profit (Row 19) = Net sales − Cost (e.g., C19 = C17 − C18).
  - SG&A (Row 21) input. Operating profit (Row 22) = Gross profit − SG&A.
  - Other income/(deductions) rows 25–27 sum to Row 28 (SUM). Income before taxes Row 29 = Operating profit + Other (Row 22 + Row 28).
  - Income taxes (Row 31) and Minority interests (Row 32) used to compute Income before cumulative effect (Row 33 = Row29 − SUM(Row31:Row32)).
  - Cumulative effect of accounting change present in G34 = 3,692 (applies only to 2001 Q1); Net income Row 35 = SUM(Row33:Row34).
  - Percent-of-sales rows use IF to avoid divide by zero (Row 20, 23, 30, 36).
- Section: Sales by Region (Rows 40–46)
  - Japan (Row 40), Overseas broken by Americas/Europe/Other (42–44); Overseas total Row 45 = SUM(42:44); Grand Total Row 46 = Japan + Overseas.

Sheet 2 — 2. Quarterly Statement (2): Segment information by product
- Segment blocks for Business machines, Cameras, Optical & other, Corporate & Eliminations.
  - For each segment: Net sales and Operating profit (inputs shown), plus a % of sales row that uses IF(segment_net=0,"",operating/ net).
  - Corporate & Eliminations (Row 30) contain negative net-sales and negative operating-profit entries (eliminations).
- Consolidation / reconciliation (Rows 36–39)
  - Consolidated Net sales Row 37 = SUM(segment net sales including Corporate/Elims) and Operating profit Row 38 = SUM(segment operating profits (including Corporate/Elims)).
  - Row 39 shows consolidated operating-profit as % of consolidated net sales (IF(...,"",...)).
- Purpose: provides segment-level detail and reconciles to the consolidated totals (Sheet1 values match these consolidated results).

Notable formula/consistency observations
- H12 uses COUNT(H9:H11) instead of SUM (likely a mistake; all other columns use SUM). Verify H12 if you expect a sum.
- Sheet1 percent IF expressions return the string "-  " for zero net sales; Sheet2 percent IF expressions return "" (blank). This is an intentional difference to watch for when parsing text vs blank.
- Cumulative effect value (G34 = 3,692) affects Net income only for 2001 Q1.
