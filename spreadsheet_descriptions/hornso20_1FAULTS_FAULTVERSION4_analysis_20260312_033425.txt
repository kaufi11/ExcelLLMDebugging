Summary of workbook structure and key contents

Sheets (6 total; three pairs of duplicates: “(2)” versions are identical copies)

1) E20-9
- Purpose: Job pricing for “Southwest Light & Gas.”
- Inputs (C2:C6, C8): Direct labor hours 16,000; direct labor cost 2,240,000; office rent 350,000; support staff salaries 860,000; utilities 246,000; estimated job hours 220.
- Key formulas:
  - C10 = C3 / C2 → Hourly direct labor cost = 2,240,000 / 16,000 = 140.00
  - C11 = SUM(C4:C6) / C2 → Indirect hourly rate = (350,000+860,000+246,000) / 16,000 = 91.00
  - C12 = C11 / C10 → Allocation rate = 91 / 140 = 0.65
  - C14 = (C10 + C11) * C8 → Predicted job cost = (140 + 91) * 220 = 50,820
  - C16 = C14 * 0.2 → 20% markup = 10,164
  - C17 = C14 + C16 → Total bid = 60,984

2) E20-9 (2)
- Duplicate layout; only difference in formula for C11:
  - C11 = SUM(C5:C6) / C2 → Indirect hourly rate excludes office rent: (860,000+246,000) / 16,000 = 69.125
  - Allocation rate = 69.125 / 140 = 0.49375
  - Predicted job cost = (140 + 69.125) * 220 = 46,007.50
  - Markup = 9,201.50; Total bid = 55,209.00

3) P20-1B
- Purpose: Journal/ledger entries for Work in Process (WIP), Finished Goods, Cost of Goods Sold.
- Layout: three side-by-side ledgers:
  - Columns B–C: (a) WIP — Job numbers and costs
  - Columns D–E: (b) Finished Goods — Job numbers and costs
  - Columns F–G: (c) COGS — Job numbers and costs
- Key data & formulas:
  - Date 2000-11-30 entries: Jobs 4 & 5 costs 500 & 500; Finished Goods Job 3 cost 1,300; COGS Job1 cost 1,400; totals: C6 = SUM(C4:C5); G6 = SUM(G4:G5).
  - Date 2000-12-31 entries: Jobs 7 & 8 costs 1,200 & 600; more finished/COGS entries; C10 = SUM(C8:C9); G12 = SUM(G8:G11).
  - Transfer/entry formulas: F14 = E4 + G6 (Finished Goods), G15 = F14 (WIP/transfer), F18 = G9+G10+G11, G19 = F18.
  - Sales/AR: F22 = 3,800 (Accounts Receivable), G23 = F22 (Sales Revenue).
  - COGS entry: F26 = 2,000; G27 = F26.
- Notes: sheet (2) is identical copy.

4) P20-3B
- Purpose: Job cost record (Alliance Rubber, Job No. 449 — 50 belts).
- Layout: job header (customer, dates) and detailed rows for Direct Materials, Direct Labor, Manufacturing Overhead, with totals and ledger transfers.
- Key cells & formulas:
  - Direct materials entries in C10–C12 (values 593, 598, 622) and requisition cost formulas in D10 (=20*15), D11 (=30*9), D12 (=12*12).
  - Direct labor: E10 = 1,754; E12 = 1,805; labor amount formulas F10 = 10*19, F12 = 40*18.
  - Overhead: H10 = 375 / 250; I10 = +F14 * H10 (I10 uses the labor total F14 — forward dependency).
  - Totals: D14 = SUM(D10:D13); F14 = SUM(F10:F12); I14 = I10 + F14 + D14 (Total cost).
  - Ledger transfers mapping totals to WIP, Finished Goods, Accounts Receivable and COGS:
    - F16 = D14 (WIP/Direct Materials), F19 = F14 (WIP/Direct Labor), F22 = I10 (WIP/Overhead)
    - F25 = I14 (Finished Goods), F28 = 50 * 110 (Accounts receivable = 50 units * $110)
    - F31 = F25 (COGS)
- Note: I10 references F14 (a total located below), which creates a forward reference dependency; the (2) sheet is identical.

Overall layout & observations
- Three distinct problem templates: cost-allocation/pricing (E20-9), inventory/ledger transfers (P20-1B), and job-cost record (P20-3B); each has a duplicate copy.
- Primary patterns: inputs in top-left blocks, computed hourly/rate values and job totals using simple arithmetic and SUM ranges, and ledger transfer formulas moving amounts between WIP, Finished Goods, AR, Sales, and COGS.
- Minor inconsistencies: typo “porfessionals”; one version of E20-9 excludes office rent from indirect costs; P20-3B has forward-referencing formula (I10 uses F14).
