- What: The latent-load-per-person formulas use / weight by F19 and F20 (sensible gains) instead of using the living/bedroom schedule sums (F38 and F39).

- Fix: Use the same weighting as the sensible calculation (use F38 and F39). Correct formula:

- Why: Latent load should be averaged by area schedule (how many people are in living vs bedroom over the year), not by the sensible gain numbers F19/F20. Current formula biases the result.

3) Leap‑year / day fraction error for simulation year 2000
- What: The workbook states "simulation year of 2000" (a leap year) but per-day fractions are being calculated dividing by 365 (and raw counts sum to 365).
- Where: Occupancy sheet: G28..G31 use =F28/365 etc; F28:F31 sum to 365 but year 2000 has 366 days.
- Fix options (pick one):
  - Best/flexible: compute fractions relative to the actual counts: G28 = F28 / SUM(F28:F31) (and copy down). That avoids hardcoding 365/366.
  - Or, if you want a fixed divisor: change 365 → 366 for year 2000.
- Why: Using 365 for year 2000 misrepresents day fractions by ~0.27% and makes weekday/weekend fractions inconsistent. Using SUM(...) is robust.
