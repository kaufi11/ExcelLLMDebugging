Summary — main problems
- Week 4 cells (E7:E12) contain =AVERAGE(B#:D#). They should be the actual Week 4 hours (numbers). The average formula there is wrong for a week column.
- Weekly totals row uses wrong ranges:
  - B15 uses B7:B13 (should end at row 12).
  - C15 uses C7:C13 (should end at row 12).
  - D15 uses D7:D11 (missing row 12).
  - G15 uses G7:G13 (should end at row 12).
- Overtime column (G7:G12) is entered manually (mostly zeros). It should be calculated from the totals.
- Gross Pay formulas (C20:C25) are wrong: (F + G) * Rate double‑counts overtime.
- Because E cells are wrong, F totals (and anything that depends on them) are also wrong (example: F11 = 92 because E11 was an average).

What to change (exact fixes)
1) Fix Week 4 (E7:E12)
   - Replace =AVERAGE(B7:D7) etc. with the actual Week 4 hours (enter the numeric hours for each employee). If you truly want an average, put that in a separate “Average” column — not in Week 4.

2) Fix Weekly Totals (row 15)
   - B15: =SUM(B7:B12)
   - C15: =SUM(C7:C12)
   - D15: =SUM(D7:D12)
   - E15: =SUM(E7:E12) (already correct)
   - F15: =SUM(F7:F12) (already correct)
   - G15: =SUM(G7:G12)

3) Calculate Overtime automatically (G7 and down)
   - Put this in G7 and copy down to G12:
     =MAX(0, F7 - 40)
   - This makes G the number of hours over 40 (adjust 40 if your overtime threshold is different).

4) Fix Gross Pay (C20:C25)
   - Use time‑and‑a‑half (common) and account for overtime without double counting. Two equivalent formulas:
     - Simple: =F7 * B20 + G7 * B20 * 0.5
       (Pays all hours at regular rate, then adds 0.5×rate for overtime)
     - Explicit: =(F7 - G7) * B20 + G7 * B20 * 1.5
       (Regular hours at rate + overtime at 1.5×rate)
   - Replace analogous formulas for rows 21–25.

Why these fixes work (short)
- Week 4 must be the actual hours; averaging weeks 1–3 in a Week 4 cell produces wrong totals and destroys payroll math.
- Weekly totals must sum the exact employee rows (7:12). Wrong ranges include blank rows or miss employees.
- Overtime should be derived from total hours (F) so it’s always consistent and not manual.
- The current gross formula (F + G) * rate adds overtime hours twice. The corrected formula either adds only the overtime premium (0.5×rate per OT hour) to the base pay, or calculates regular and overtime pay separately — both give correct gross pay.
