High-level summary — purpose and common layout
- Purpose: Monthly cash-flow projection for Year 1 and Year 2, plus variance analysis comparing actuals to Year 1 plan by month/quarter.
- Column structure (common): Column B = labels/subtotals; Columns C–N = months 1–12; Column O = TOTAL (SUM of C:N); Column P = % of Sales (most rows: Ox / O57). Rows group by major sections: beginning cash, receipts, total available, cash paid out (variable & fixed), other outflows, total paid out, ending cash, essential operating data (sales, A/R, inventory, etc.).
- Key recurring calculations:
  - Totals by month: e.g., row 14 = SUM(row 8:13) per month.
  - Total cash available (row 15) = beginning cash (row 6) + total receipts (row 14).
  - Total cash paid out (row 54) = SUM(total operating + other outflows).
  - Ending cash (row 55) = total available (row 15) − total paid out (row 54).
  - Sales total (row 57) in O57 used as denominator for % of sales in column P (many formulas reference O$57).
