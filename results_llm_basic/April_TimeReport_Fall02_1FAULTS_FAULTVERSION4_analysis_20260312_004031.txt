Findings (short)
- Cell N29 is wrong: it contains the hard-coded value/formula "7" instead of summing the row. All other row totals use a SUM(B#:M#) pattern; N29 should too.
- Because N29 is included in Studio I’s total (N19 = SUM(N20:N34)), that extra 7 is being added into N19 and thus into the final grand-total (N35). In short: one hard-coded 7 inflates Studio I and the sheet total.

Fix (one step)
- Replace the contents of N29 with:
  =SUM(B29:M29)

Why this fixes it (short)
- That makes N29 the correct sum of the QA row values (B29:M29). Since N19 sums N20:N34, N29 will no longer add an incorrect 7, and the Studio I total (N19) and overall total (N35) will be correct.

Optional verification
- After changing N29 to =SUM(B29:M29), confirm N29 becomes 0 (because B29:M29 are zeros). N19 and N35 should each drop by 7 compared to their current values.
