High-level summary
- Multi-sheet financial / capacity / price model for DRAM/LEM chips covering 2001–2006 (columns for years normally in C:H).  
- Purpose: calibrate demand (functionality-adjusted), compute industry capacity and price responses, calculate variable costs and capex for different node/ die‑size vintages, then run ROI/IRR for new‑build and upgrade scenarios (entrant vs incumbent Cournot case).

Sheets and key contents (concise)

1) fisher (Demand price index & base demand)
- Contains detailed device mix (64M–1Gbit) by year, ASPs by device, computes ASP/256M‑equiv and chained Fisher price indices (Laspeyres/Paasche → Fisher → chained P).
- Produces functionality per chip, Q (256M equiv) and linear demand parameters (Q = a − bP) using assumed elasticity (A47 = −1.5).
- Key formulas: SUMPRODUCT for revenue, SQRT for Fisher index, chained index in D40:H40, slope b = −elasticity * Q/P (rows C50:H50).
- Feeds price/functionality indices (fisher!C40 etc.) to LEM Demand Calibration and other sheets.

2) LEM Demand Calibration
- Uses an external semico revenue & unit forecast (row 3–4) and fisher indices to compute functionality ($/2001) and per‑chip function metrics.
- Calibrates linear demand curve constants (a and b in rows 15–16) with elasticity −1.5.
- Key outputs: calibrated a, b per year and functionality/chip (used by Capacity&Price).

3) LEM cost side
- Detailed capacity, yields, throughput-learning, un‑yielded dice, effective yielded die/wafer, variable cost per wafer and per yielded die, and equipment/building capex by vintage/node and die size.
- Rows grouped by vintage/node (0.25, 0.18, 0.13, 0.09, 0.065) and wafer diameter (200/300 mm) with repeated columns for 2001–2006.
- Computes Effective wafer starts/year (D18..I18), throughput learning multipliers (D64..I73), effective yielded die/wafer (D76..I85), Variable cost/wafer and per‑die (D88..I109).
- Capex: new equip and upgrades ($M per fab and per K wafer starts) and building capex (C229..C249 converted per node factor). Many formula links: equipment costs divided by (20*node_factor) to get $M per K starts (D136..).
- Provides the cost inputs used by Capacity&Price and ROI sheets.

4) DRAM cost side
- Simpler cost-side worksheet (industry capacity profile, effective wafer starts, average die/wafer, A&T yield, overhead rate C15=0.36).
- Computes baseline units produced (mil units), predicted price/ P (via fisher links), per‑wafer variable profit, cap cost/start, undiscounted profit/wafer, and 20K fab cash flow.
- Cash flow / IRR for entrant and Cournot incumbent cases (uses IRR(C39:H39,0.15) and IRR(C51:H51,0.15)).
- Key constants: baseline industry monthly wafer starts (C4..H4 = 1000 K/month), discount factor for announced vs effective capacity F7 = 0.6, 20K fab equivalency (row A5 formula = C4/20 etc).

5) Capacity&Price Calculation
- Aggregates economic capacity by vintage/node by pulling 'LEM cost side' capacity (C3:C12) and effective yields (LEM cost side D76..).
- Computes baseline units produced (C15..C24) and functionality produced ($2001) (C27..C36) using LEM Demand Calibration functionality per chip.
- Uses inverse demand (P = (Q − a)/−b) to compute P/chip and P/function, compares to variable cost to determine economic capacity: IF(var cost per chip > price → set capacity to 0) (rows C55:C64).
- Outputs: Total economic capacity (C65), retirement‑eligible capacity (C68:C77), and functions to produce additional output for new fabs/upgrades (C81..).
- Price and production after adding 20K starts: recalculates new prices (C104..C114) and price‑per‑chip cases for upgrade/new fab scenarios.

6) Static RoI calc '02
- Brings cost and demand outputs together to calculate cash flows and IRRs for a wide set of scenarios (new fab entrant, Cournot incumbent, upgrade 1/2).
- Rows compute K building and equipment costs (from LEM cost side), delta revenues and delta variable costs from capacity & price outputs, net cash flows and IRR over 6 years using IRR(range,0.15).
- Produces IRR for many combinations by vintage/node (C59, C61, C63, etc. and L59, N59 for incumbent case).

7) $ per bit (support sheet)
- Duplicate/parallel calculations to fisher but expressed per‑bit indices and revenues; used for cross‑checks and alternative indexing. Same structure as fisher: device mix, ASP, total rev, ASP/256M equiv, and linear demand calibration.

Model flow (how sheets connect)
- Demand baseline and price indices computed in fisher (and $ per bit) → LEM Demand Calibration uses semico forecast + fisher indices to get functionality price and linear demand parameters.  
- LEM cost side computes physical capacity, yields, variable costs, capex and effective cost per K starts.  
- Capacity&Price pulls LEM cost side and LEM Demand Calibration to compute economic capacity, price responses, retirement eligible capacity, and the effect of adding/upgrading fabs on output and price.  
- Static RoI calc and DRAM cost side compute cash flows and IRRs for new‑build entrants and incumbent (Cournot) cases using the price & cost outputs.

Key formulas, functions, and constants
- Elasticity-based slope: b = −elasticity * Q / P (fisher C50..), demand linearization Q = a − bP (a computed as Q + bP).
- Inverse demand for price after capacity shifts: P = −(Q − a)/b or P = (Q − a)/−b (used across Capacity&Price and DRAM cost side).
- IF to determine economic capacity: IF(var cost per chip > price, 0, baseline capacity).
- Capex/unit conversions: dividing total equip or building $ by (20 * node_factor) to get $M per K wafer starts.
- Cashflow aggregation and IRR: IRR(range,0.15) used repeatedly (Static RoI, DRAM cost side).
- SUMPRODUCT used to compute revenue from device mix and ASPs (fisher and $ per bit).

Important inputs / constants to watch
- Assumed elasticity = −1.5 (common across sheets).  
- Overhead SG&A = 0.36 (C15 / C47).  
- Discount factor for announced→effective capacity = 0.6 (F7 / F39).  
- Baseline industry wafer starts = 1000 K/month per column in DRAM cost and equivalent in other sheets.  
- 20K fab definition frequently used (C29 / C61 = 20 K starts/mo), many per‑fab conversions use factor 20 (20K starts/month baseline) and 12 months.

Primary outputs delivered by the model
- Year‑by‑year: price per chip (P/chip), price per unit functionality (P/function), baseline and post‑build production by vintage (bil units), total economic capacity and retirement eligible capacity, delta revenues/variable costs for adding/upgrading fabs.  
- Scenario financials: net cash flows for entrant versus incumbent cases and multiple upgrade/new‑build permutations, with IRR evaluations (IRR(...) shown in many cells).

Notes / caveats
- The workbook is heavily linked: many cells pull from fisher! and 'LEM cost side' — changing base inputs (device ASPs, elasticity, overhead, yields, node factors) propagates through prices, economic capacity flags, and IRR outputs.  
- Several near‑duplicate sheets (fisher vs $ per bit) perform equivalent index calculations in alternate units; LEM cost side is the detailed engineering/cost engine while DRAM cost side is a simpler comparator.
