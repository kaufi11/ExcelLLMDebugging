Faulty cell(s)
- G12 (and the dependent cells H12, G36, H36 which show wrong totals because of G12).

What’s wrong (short)
- G12 uses /=100 instead of *=100. That makes G12 a decimal (e.g., 0.30) while every other G cell is a percent (e.g., 30). That single wrong entry undercounts that institution’s market share and breaks the totals/HHI.

Exact bad formula shown
- G12: =$F12/$F$36/100

Fix (one-step)
- Replace G12 with: =$F12/$F$36*100

Better (cleaner) approach (recommended)
- Put this formula in G10 and fill down: =$F10/$F$36
- Then format column G as Percentage (Format Cells → Percentage). This avoids multiplying by 100 in formulas and keeps consistency.

Why this fixes it (short)
- All market shares must use the same scale. Changing G12 to use the same division-and-scale as the others makes the G-column add to 100% and makes H (squares) consistent, so totals (G36, H36) become correct.

Optional: After fixing, verify
- G36 should be ~100 (or 100.00% if formatted), and H36 will be the correct HHI sum.
