Summary of workbook structure and contents

Overall
- Workbook has three sheets: "Financial Statements" (detailed accounts and notes), "5 Year review" (multi‑year summary and ratios that pull from the Financial Statements sheet), and "Audit Report" (static audit sign‑off text).
- The Financial Statements sheet is laid out in conventional sections: Balance Sheet, Income Statement, Cash Flow Statement, followed by Notes to the financial statements and detailed schedules (tangible assets, investments, inventory, leases, etc.).
- The model relies heavily on SUM formulas, intra‑sheet references and a number of cross‑sheet links (the 5 Year review pulls specific line items from Financial Statements). Several cells use explicit arithmetic or link to other computed totals (e.g. accumulated funds references a summed schedule).

  - Cash & equivalents used in the cash flow are also reconstructed in a short schedule (C529:C531 and C532 = SUM(...)).
- Notes and schedules:
  - Numbered notes (1 – 20) containing accounting policies, schedules for tangible assets (note 4: cost, accumulated depreciation, movements), listed investments, inventory, short term investments, accumulated funds, development funds, lease details, other income, seminars, audit fees, and detailed breakdown of "Other administration expenditure".
  - Many supporting formulas in the notes (SUMs and cross references to the main statements). Example: tangible assets net carrying value C248 = C234 - C241 with cost and accumulated depreciation computed by SUM ranges.

Sheet: 5 Year review (layout and links)
- Multi‑column (2002, 2001, 2000, 1999, 1998) condensed income & balance summary and cash flow.
- Numerous cells link to the Financial Statements sheet. Examples:
  - Member subscriptions (E15) = 'Financial Statements'!E54
  - Other income (E16) = 'Financial Statements'!E61
  - Expenditure (C18/E18) = 'Financial Statements'!C100 / !E100
  - Cash retained C32 = 'Financial Statements'!C117
  - Net investment and financing C33/C34 also link to Financial Statements.
- Ratios: current assets to current liabilities and capital & reserves to total assets reference Financial Statements totals.
- Simple member and student headcount and growth columns included.

Sheet: Audit Report
- Static text: auditors’ opinion, signatories, dates. No formulas.

Notes on model characteristics / potential points to watch
- Heavy reliance on SUM ranges and a number of cross‑references—changes in note rows can propagate widely.
- Some cells are recorded as "FORMULA =<constant>" (e.g., C72 =1416) or reference totals in non‑obvious places (e.g., C29 = +C330) — review links if you re‑structure rows.
- A few auxiliary columns (G, H, I) contain marks or intermediate values; some starred cells (G31, G34, G40) appear as annotations.
- The 5 Year review replicates figures by linking to the Financial Statements—good for consistency but sensitive to row/label changes.
