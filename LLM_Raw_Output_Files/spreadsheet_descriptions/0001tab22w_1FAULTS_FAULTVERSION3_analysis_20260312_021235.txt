Sheet name and purpose
- Single sheet: "Table 22 - Percentage Distribution of Fall Term Undergraduate Grades" (title rows A1–A2). It shows percentage grade distributions by College, Grade Category (A, B, C, D, F/WF, Other), Level (Lower/Upper), and Year (2000, 1999, 1998).

Layout and key columns
- Header rows: row 3–4 set Level (Lower Level at E3, Upper Level at S3) and column labels (A: College, C: Year).  
- Lower-level grade % columns: E = A, G = B, I = C, K = D, M = F/WF, O = Other, Q = Total Number of Grades.  
- Upper-level grade % columns: S = A, U = B, W = C, Y = D, AA = F/WF, AC = Other, AF = Total Number of Grades.  
- R column is present but blank. Data rows for each college (Arts & Sciences, Business Administration, Hospitality Management, Education, Engineering, Health & Public Affairs) and a TOTAL UNIVERSITY block.

Formulas and calculations
- "Other" columns (O and AC) are computed as 100 minus the sum of listed percents, using formulas like O6 = SUM(100-(E6+G6+I6+K6+M6)). (The SUM wrapper is redundant but harmless.)  
- University total grade counts: Q30 = Q26+Q22+Q18+Q14+Q10+Q6 (sums lower-level counts across colleges); AF30 similarly sums upper-level totals. Year-specific total rows (Q31/AF31) follow the same pattern. Some totals for 1998 are hard-coded in Q32 (40676) and AF32 (51470).  
- Several AC "Other" formulas mirror the lower-level approach for upper-level percents: e.g., AC6 = SUM(100-(S6+U6+W6+Y6+AA6)).

Key values (examples)
- TOTAL UNIVERSITY (2000): lower-level percentages E30..M30 = 30.7% (A), 29.3% (B), 17.8% (C), 5.3% (D), 6.6% (F/WF); upper-level S30..AA30 = 36.6% (A), 31.3% (B), 16% (C), 3.9% (D), 4.8% (F/WF).  
- 1998 totals shown: lower-level total count Q32 = 40,676; upper-level AF32 = 51,470.

Data notes and inconsistencies to check
- Some rows contain "n/appl" for Hospitality Management (1999 and 1998) in many percent cells.  
- A few rows include extra intermediate columns (e.g., F18, H18, Z22) that break the strict E/G/I/K/M pattern for some Education and Engineering rows — these appear to be duplicate/auxiliary entries and should be checked for accidental shifts.  
- Possible formula typo: O20 uses M21 in the subtraction instead of M20 (likely should be M20).  
- Several cells show 0 in otherwise blank intermediate columns (e.g., H10, H14).  
- "Other" formulas rely on the listed percent columns summing properly; any missing or misaligned entries will produce incorrect "Other" values.

Overall
- The sheet is a simple tabular report: colleges × years with paired lower/upper-level grade-percentage columns and counts. Percent "Other" is computed as the residual to 100; university totals are computed by summing college counts. Verify column alignments and the noted formula typo to ensure accurate "Other" and total calculations.
