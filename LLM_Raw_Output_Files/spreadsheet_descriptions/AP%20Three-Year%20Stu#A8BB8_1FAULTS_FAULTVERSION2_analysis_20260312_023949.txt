Row structure and repeating blocks
- Each school (and group "Richmond County High School Programs - Overall Achievement") is presented in a block of rows:
  - Rows for AP grade levels 5,4,3,2,1 (B has grade number; corresponding counts per subject in the subject/year columns).
  - A "Total Exams" row that sums the grade rows for that school (formulas like =SUM(C5:C9), =SUM(D5:D9) etc). Example: row 10 sums rows 5–9.
  - A "% of Grades 3+" row (percent of exams with grade ≥3 per subject per year).
  - A "Mean Grade" row (mean score for that subject/year).
- Blocks repeat for each school: Academy of Richmond County, Butler, Cross Creek, Davidson Fine Arts Magnet School, Glenn Hills, Hephzibah, A. R. Johnson, T. W. Josey, Lucy C. Laney, Westside.
- There are two major subject/year groupings on the sheet (one covering a set of subjects for 2001–2003, another for additional subjects and earlier years 2000–2002), plus grade-level breakdown tables for Richmond County (rows ~101–107 and following) showing totals by grade cohort (9th/10th, 11th, 12th, Coll., Other) and %3+ / mean grade.

Data characteristics and notes
- The sheet is a dense matrix: for each school × subject × year, raw counts by AP grade (5–1) are reported, followed by totals, %3+, and mean.
- Many cells are zero or blank (especially for less-offered subjects), and some percent/mean rows reflect small sample sizes (single- or low-digit totals).
- The sheet contains two similar large blocks covering different subject sets and overlapping years (2000–2003 and 2001–2003), plus grade-level roll-ups for Richmond County.
- Most aggregation relies on straightforward SUM formulas across the grade-level rows; percent and mean values appear pre-calculated (not shown as formulas in the provided excerpt).
