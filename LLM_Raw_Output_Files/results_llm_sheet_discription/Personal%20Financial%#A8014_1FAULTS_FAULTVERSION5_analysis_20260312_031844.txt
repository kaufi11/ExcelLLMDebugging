Thanks — I reviewed both sheets and found the definite formula mistakes and a few odd formulas that can produce wrong totals. Short, clear fixes below.

Summary of the real errors and fixes

1) STEP 1 — Giving subtotal missed a row
- Error: E33 = SUM(E29:E30) — excludes E31 (there are 3 giving rows E29:E31).
- Fix: change to =SUM(E29:E31)
- Why: leaving out row 31 undercounts the "giving" total used downstream.

2) STEP 1 — Emergencies / Future Needs subtotal uses single cell only
- Error: E43 = SUM(E42) — effectively just E42; should include E41 (Emergencies).
- Fix: change to =SUM(E41:E42)
- Why: otherwise the emergencies total is missing and E43 is wrong.

3) STEP 1 — Future plans total uses only one cell
- Error: M43 = M42 — ignores M41 (if used).
- Fix: change to =SUM(M41:M42)
- Why: ensures the "future needs/plans" total includes both rows and feeds correct saving totals.

4) STEP 2 — Saving percent formula points to Giving
- Error: F10 = H8/H6 (this re-uses the giving cell H8)
- Fix: change to =H10/H6
- Why: F10 should show saved % of income; pointing to H8 gives the wrong percent.

5) STEP 2 — Taxes cell is incorrect
- Error: H14 = 1 (hardcoded 1) and F14 = H14/H6 (therefore nonsense)
- Fix: set H14 to the taxes withheld total from Step 1. For example:
  H14 = 'STEP 1 OF 2'!G22
  (leave F14 = H14/H6)
- Why: H14 must contain the actual tax dollars (from Step 1) so F14 can compute the tax % of earnings.

6) Minor / cosmetic (not strictly breaking, but worth cleaning)
- I33 = SUM(G32+I32) and M33 = SUM(K32+M32) are unusual constructions. Replace with:
  I33 = G32 + I32  (or =SUM(G32, I32))
  M33 = K32 + M32  (or =SUM(K32, M32))
- Why: SUM(G32+I32) works but is confusing; using direct addition or SUM with commas is clearer and less error-prone.

Why these matter (short)
- Wrong ranges or hardcoded values produce undercounts/overcounts in Step 1 totals (giving, saving, taxes). Step 2 pulls those totals to compute % allocations and the final budget. Any incorrect source value will cascade and produce wrong %s and a wrong final surplus/shortfall.
