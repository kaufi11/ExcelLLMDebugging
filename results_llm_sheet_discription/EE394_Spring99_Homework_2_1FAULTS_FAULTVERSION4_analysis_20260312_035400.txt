Thanks — I inspected the sheet and found the clear mistakes and a possible modelling issue. Short, direct fixes and explanations below.

1) Many ATAN2 calls have the arguments reversed (wrong phasor angles)
- Problem: ATAN2 in Excel is ATAN2(y,x) where y = imag, x = real. The workbook mostly uses ATAN2(real,imag). That produces wrong angles and wrong quadrants for VS, IS, RhoV, etc.
- Where: every cell like J4, J5,... J54 and N4..N54 (VS and IS angle columns), plus angle uses inside B32/B33 and similar places.
- Fix: swap the two arguments. Example replacements:
  - J4: change =ATAN2(G4,H4)*180/PI()  → =ATAN2(H4,G4)*180/PI()
  - N4: change =ATAN2(K4,L4)*180/PI()  → =ATAN2(L4,K4)*180/PI()
  - B32 (part of RhoV): replace ATAN2(B28,B29) with ATAN2(B29,B28) and ATAN2(B30,B31) with ATAN2(B31,B30).
- Why: ATAN2(imag,real) gives the correct phasor angle (argument of complex number). Using the wrong order computes arctan(real/imag) which flips/rotates the angle and misplaces the quadrant.

2) Typo in one cosine argument (single-cell error)

- Fix: change E21 to =COS($B$15*D21*1000)
- Also check any other rows for similar accidental constants.

3) Potential (intent) error for current magnitude IR (check 3‑phase vs single‑phase)
- Problem: B21 = B7*1000/B6 computes I = S*1000 / V. If S is MVA and V is kV this gives I (A) only for single‑phase. For three‑phase systems the correct formula is I = S(MVA)*1000 / (√3 * V(kV)).

  - =B7*1000/(SQRT(3)*B6)
