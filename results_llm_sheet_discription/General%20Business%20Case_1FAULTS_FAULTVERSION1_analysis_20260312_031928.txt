Findings (short)
- Two formula errors on the STMT OF OPERATIONS sheet:
  1. A typo in C10: it contains =C8-26 instead of =C8-C9.
  2. The Variance column (column E) is inconsistent: some rows use =A#-C# (Actual minus Forecast) while many use the reversed =C#-A#. That makes many variances and subtotals show the wrong sign and breaks variance totals.

Why these are problems
- C10 wrong → Gross Profit (Forecast column) and all downstream Forecast and Variance calculations that depend on C10 are incorrect.
- Mixed signs in column E → individual variances, subtotal variances (E15, E41, E50, E51, E53, etc.) and any dashboard numbers that rely on them will be misleading (positive shown as negative and vice versa).

How to fix (quick steps)
1. Fix the typo in the Forecast Gross Profit:
   - On STMT OF OPERATIONS: select C10 and change the formula to:
     =C8-C9
   - Press Enter.

2. Standardize the Variance column to one convention (recommended: Variance = Actual - Forecast):
   - On STMT OF OPERATIONS: in E6 enter:
     =A6-C6
   - Drag/fill that cell down through the full variance range (all rows that currently show differences — e.g., E6:E53 or until the last variance row).
   - This will replace any reversed formulas (=C#-A#) with the correct =A#-C# relative references.

Alternative fast method if there are many isolated reversed formulas
- Identify reversed formulas by scanning column E or by comparing E# to -(existing formula). For a few cells you can simply replace =C7-A7 with =A7-C7, etc.

Verify
- After changes:
  - Check that each E# = A# - C# for all variance rows.
  - Confirm E15 = A15 - C15, E41 = A41 - C41, E50 = A50 - C50, E51 = A51 - C51, E53 = A53 - C53.
  - Recalculate workbook (F9 or Ctrl+Alt+F9) and verify SUMMARY F26 (Current Month Profit/Loss) matches STMT OF_OPERATIONS A53 and that variance totals now look logical (positive if Actual > Forecast).

Why this solution works
- Correcting C10 fixes the Forecast Gross Profit source so Forecast subtotals are mathematically correct.
- Using a single variance convention (Actual − Forecast) ensures each row and each subtotal is consistent and the roll-up variances (sums/differences) are mathematically coherent.
