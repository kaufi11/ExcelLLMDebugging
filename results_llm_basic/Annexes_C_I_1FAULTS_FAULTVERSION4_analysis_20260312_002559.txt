I checked the workbook and found three clear formula errors (plus one likely-improper sum range). Each error, why it’s wrong, and the exact fix are below — short and simple.

1) Sheet: annex f — cell K68 (Variance % for Total Funds)
- Problem: K68 = K48 (it simply copies the % for “Total Net Assets”). That’s a copy/paste error. K68 should compute the variance % for Total Funds (J68 vs H68), not reuse K48.
- Fix: Replace formula in K68 with:
  =IF(H68=0,"N/A",(J68/H68)*100)
- Why this fixes it: This computes the % variance on the Total Funds row the same way every other % cell does, and prevents showing an unrelated percentage.

2) Sheet: Annex I — cell H27 (Underlying operating surplus/deficit)
- Problem: H27 = SUM(H10:H26). That range is wrong (it includes cells that are not the add-backs and may omit the intended cells). It’s ambiguous and can pick up headers/blanks or unrelated cells.
- Fix: Make the sum explicit for the rows you want to include (the add‑backs you show on the sheet). For example, if you want:
  - Surplus after depreciation (H11)
  - Add back exceptional restructuring (H14)
  - Add back SSAP24 (H17)
  - Add back other one-off items (H20)
  then use:
  =SUM(H11,H14,H17,H20)
- Why this fixes it: Explicitly summing the intended contribution lines avoids including wrong rows and makes the calculation auditable and correct.

3) Sheet: annex g — Totals in column T reference S (wrong totals)
- Problem: Several “Total” cells in column T are set to equal the July value (Sxx) instead of the annual total. Example: T22 = S22 and T23 = S23. That means the “Total” column is showing only July instead of SUM(H:S).
- Fix: Replace those T-column formulas so each total sums across months (H:S) the same way other totals do. Examples:
  - T22: =SUM(H22:S22)
  - T23: =SUM(H23:S23)
  - Similarly update T45, T46 (in the Actual/ Revised section) to =SUM(H45:S45) and =SUM(H46:S46)
  - Then T24, T47 (which sum T22:T23 or T45:T46) will be correct.
- Why this fixes it: Column T is intended to be the annual total for each row; summing H:S ensures T shows the real total and keeps year totals consistent.
