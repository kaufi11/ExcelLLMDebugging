I checked the Occupancy workbook and found two types of clear errors (plus one recommendation). Short summary, locations and fixes below.

1) Wrong weighting in the “Latent load per person” formulas (unit/denominator error)
- Where it appears:
  - Sheet "Occ L0" cell F10
  - Sheet "Occ L1-WD" cell F10
  - Sheet "Occ L1-DU" cell F10
  - Sheet "Occ L2-WD-DU" cell F11
  - (Possibly other summary sheets that tried to average latent gains the same way)
- What’s wrong:
  - Those formulas weight latent gains using Occupancy!F19 and Occupancy!F20 in the denominator (and numerator), i.e. they divide by the sensible-per-person numbers instead of weighting by the schedule sums (F38/F39). That mixes incompatible units and gives a wrong average.
- Example wrong formula:
  =+(Occupancy!F22*Occupancy!F19 + Occupancy!F23*Occupancy!F20) / (Occupancy!F19 + Occupancy!F20)
- Correct formula:
  = (Occupancy!F22*Occupancy!F38 + Occupancy!F23*Occupancy!F39) / (Occupancy!F38 + Occupancy!F39)
- Why this fixes it:
  - F22/F23 = latent BTU/person/hr (living/bedroom). F38/F39 = the schedule totals for living/bedroom (weights). To produce a weighted-average latent BTU/person you must weight by schedule totals (F38/F39), not by sensible-per-person numbers (F19/F20). This keeps units consistent and yields the intended average.

2) Accidental negative signs in one hourly aggregation (typo producing negative occupancy)
- Where it appears:
  - Sheet "Occ L2-WD-DU" cell D25
- What’s wrong:
  - Formula uses leading minus signs:
    = -'Occ L3-WD-DU-LR'!E25 - 'Occ L3-WD-DU-LR'!F25
  - That will return a negative value when it should be adding the two source columns.
- Correct formula:
  = 'Occ L3-WD-DU-LR'!E25 + 'Occ L3-WD-DU-LR'!F25
- Why this fixes it:
  - The cell is supposed to sum the two detailed columns; the stray negative signs invert the sign and break schedule fractions/values.

3) Recommendation: check sheet-name consistency (spaces) and any other similar copy/paste errors
- I noticed formulas referencing sheets with slightly different naming styles (e.g. 'OccL2-WD-DU' vs 'Occ L2-WD-DU'). If you rename sheets or have mixed names Excel will produce #REF errors. Confirm all sheet-name text in formulas matches the actual tab names.
- Also scan the other hourly sum/copy formulas for accidental minus signs or wrong cell references (these mistakes often come from copying formulas across many cells).
