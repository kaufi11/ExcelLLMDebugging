2) Unsafe/unclear formulas (recommendation)
- Many "Remaining" cells in column G use =SUM(Cx-E x) (e.g. G10, G13, G14, G15, …).
- Problem: =SUM(Cx-Ex) works (Excel evaluates the subtraction then SUMs the single result) but it's confusing and can hide errors or break if someone edits the formula expecting a range.
- Fix: use plain subtraction: =Cx-Ex (for example G10 -> =C10-E10)
- Why: clearer, standard practice, less error-prone.

3) Grand totals written oddly
- Faulty cells: C61, E61, G61 use =SUM(C10+C24+C30+C37+C45+C51+C59) (same pattern for E/G).
- Problem: SUM with a single arithmetic expression is unnecessary and nonstandard. It will work, but it's harder to read and can mask mistakes. Also if you later want to include additional rows, these hard-coded references can be missed.
- Fix (preferred): =SUM(C10,C24,C30,C37,C45,C51,C59)
  Or if your layout allows and you want one contiguous range: =SUM(C10:C59) but be careful not to double-count subtotal rows.
- Why: comma-separated SUM arguments are explicit; using a range makes future maintenance easier.
