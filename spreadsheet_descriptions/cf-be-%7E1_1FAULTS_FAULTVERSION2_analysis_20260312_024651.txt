Summary of workbook structure and key formulas (concise)

Overall layout (common to Year 1 and Year 2)
- Purpose: Monthly Cash Flow Projection for a 12‑month period (months in columns C:N), with TOTAL in column O and % of Sales in column P.
- Rows grouped into numbered sections:
  1) Cash on Hand (beginning of month) — row 6
  2) Cash Receipts (rows 8–13) with subitems a–f and O column totals
  3) Total Cash Receipts — row 14 (monthly SUM of rows 8:13)
  4) Total Cash Available — row 15 = beginning cash + receipts
  5) Cash Paid Out — many expense rows (materials, labor, payroll, admin, utilities, etc.) rows 17–53
     - Subtotals:
       - Variable expenses subtotal: row 23 (SUM of 17:22)
       - Fixed expenses subtotal: row 47 (SUM of 24:46)
       - Total operating expenses: row 48 = row23 + row47
     - Additional outflows: loan principal, capital purchases, reserve, owner withdrawal (rows 49–53)
  6) Total Cash Paid Out — row 54 (SUM of outflow rows 48:53)
  7) Cash Position End of Month — row 55 = Total Cash Available (row15) − Total Cash Paid Out (row54)
- Essential Operating Data (rows 56–63): Sales Volume, AR, Bad Debt, Inventory, AP, Depreciation.
- Contribution margin and break‑even:
  - Total Fixed Costs (B63) = +O54 − O23
  - Contribution Margin (B64) = 1 − P23
  - Break‑even Sales (F64) = (O54 − O23) / B64

Key formula patterns
- Monthly totals use SUM across the month column ranges (e.g., O8: O13 summed into O14).
- Column O generally contains row totals across months: O# = SUM(C#:N#).
- Column P generally shows each row as a percent of sales using O$57 as denominator: P# = O# / O$57.
- Many single‑cell subtotals use SUM() around arithmetic (e.g., B15 = SUM(B6+B14)) — functional but unnecessary.
- Year 1 row55 (end cash) formulas: B55:N55 = row15 − row54 for each month (some formulas use SUM wrappers).
- Year 2 starting cash (B6) pulls Year 1 N55: B6 = 'Year 1'!N55; subsequent months in Year 2 are chained from prior month end cash.

Variance / Quarter sheets (Var,Y1,Q1 ... Q4 and quarter-specific variants)
- Purpose: Variance analysis comparing Actuals to Year 1 projections and computing % variances.
- Structure: For each month column there are Budget (Year 1 value), Actual, and Variance columns. Variance formulas are (Actual − Budget) / Budget (often written as =(D8-C8)/C8 or similar).
- Most variance sheets fetch budget numbers directly from 'Year 1' (e.g., C8 = 'Year 1'!C8) and compute actuals & percent variance in adjacent columns.
- Quarter sheets map sets of months to quarter comparisons (Q1..Q4 and Q2–Q4 sheets follow same pattern).

Notable items / possible issues to review
- Several formulas use SUM() around simple arithmetic (e.g., SUM(B6+B14)) — no error but unnecessary.
- A few apparent mistakes/oddities:
  - Year 1 K55 formula shows SUM(K15-90) — likely a typo; should be SUM(K15-K54) or K15-K54.
  - Year 2 P57 formula is =+O57/O57 which always yields 1 — probably intended to be =O57/O$57 or a different reference.
  - Some payroll / expense row references in Year 2 are misaligned (e.g., row20 uses O22 in Year 2 listing) — check row mapping.
  - Many formulas include a leading "+" or use SUM on single differences — these are harmless but inconsistent style.
- Percent of sales denominators use O$57 (total sales in row 57) — ensure O57 is populated correctly each year; otherwise P‑column %s will be wrong.
