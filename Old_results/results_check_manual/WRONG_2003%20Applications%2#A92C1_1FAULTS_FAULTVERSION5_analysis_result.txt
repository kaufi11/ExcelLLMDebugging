1) Double‑counting between Non‑Profit and Rehab totals (affects County total)
- Problem: County!E13 ("Total Requested") = SUM(E9:E12) where
  - E9 = Municipal!E22 (Municipal total)
  - E10 = 'Non Profit'!E22 (Non‑Profit total)
  - E11 = Rehab!E8 (Non‑Profit Rehabilitation total)
  Because several Rehab projects also appear in the Non‑Profit list (for example CADCOM '41514' Non Profit!E3 = Rehab!E2, Community Cupboard Non Profit!E4 = Rehab!E3, Resources for Human Development Non Profit!E17 = Rehab!E6), part of the Rehab total is already included in 'Non Profit'!E22. Summing both Non‑Profit and Rehab totals in County!E13 therefore double‑counts that overlapping amount (approx. 79,014 based on the three overlapping entries).
- Effect: County!E13 (7693312.56) overstates the true requested total by the overlapping Rehab amounts.
- Suggested fix:
  - Decide whether Rehab projects should be included in the Non‑Profit list or reported only in Rehab. Then:
    - Option A: Remove Rehab rows from the Non‑Profit list so 'Non Profit'!E22 excludes rehab, OR
    - Option B: Change County!E13 to use a non‑overlapping aggregation (e.g., SUM of Municipal + Non‑Profit excluding Rehab + County) or use distinct named ranges. Explicitly document which totals are aggregated where.
