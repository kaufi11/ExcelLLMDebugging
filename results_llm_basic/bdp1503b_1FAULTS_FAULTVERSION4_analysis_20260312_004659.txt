I inspected Sheet1. Main problems, why they matter, and how to fix them (short and simple):

1) Hard-coded value instead of formula
- Problem: G19 contains =3 (a fixed number).  
- Why it’s wrong: it won’t update if C19 or E19 change.  
- Fix: Replace with =C19-E19.  
- Explanation: Remaining = Allocation − Actual, so it must reference the cells.

2) Using SUM() around a subtraction (odd/inconsistent)
- Problem examples: G10 =SUM(C10-E10), G13 =SUM(C13-E13), many others.  
- Why it’s bad: SUM() is meant for ranges or lists; SUM(C10-E10) works because Excel evaluates the subtraction first, but it’s confusing and inconsistent. It can hide intent and cause mistakes later.  
- Fix: Replace with direct subtraction, e.g. G10 =C10-E10 (and similarly for all G rows).  
- Explanation: This is clearer and standard: Remaining = Allocation − Actual.

3) SUM(...) with plus signs instead of proper arguments
- Problem examples: C61 =SUM(C10+C24+C30+C37+C45+C51+C59), E61 =SUM(E10+...).  
- Why it’s odd: SUM accepts either ranges or comma-separated arguments. SUM(C10+C24+...) works because Excel evaluates the addition first, but it’s unconventional and less readable.  
- Fix: Use one of:
  - =SUM(C10,C24,C30,C37,C45,C51,C59)  (preferred), or
  - =C10+C24+C30+C37+C45+C51+C59
- Explanation: Makes intent clear and avoids confusion.

4) Possible data/consistency issue: donations

5) Blank E (actual) cells are treated as zero
