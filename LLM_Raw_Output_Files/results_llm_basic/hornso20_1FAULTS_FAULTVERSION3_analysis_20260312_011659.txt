1) Sheet E20-9 — Error (present in the duplicate sheet E20-9 (2))
- Problem cell: C11
- Wrong formula: =SUM(C4:C6)*C2
- Correct formula: =SUM(C4:C6)/C2
- Why: C4:C6 are total indirect costs. To get an hourly indirect rate you divide total indirect costs by total labor hours (C2). Multiplying inflates the rate by a factor of total hours.

(Everything else on E20-9 is correct once C11 is fixed — C10 = C3/C2, C14=(C10+C11)*C8, etc.)

2) Sheet P20-3B — Errors in manufacturing-overhead calculations
- Problem cell: I10
- Wrong formula: =+F14*H10
- Correct formula (most likely): =F10*H10
- Why: I10 should be the overhead applied to that particular labor entry, not the grand total of labor (F14). Use the direct-labor amount for the same row (F10) times the overhead rate (H10). If the overhead rate is per labor-hour instead of per labor-dollar, the formula should be (labor hours for that row) * H10 — but using F14 (total labor) is definitely wrong.
