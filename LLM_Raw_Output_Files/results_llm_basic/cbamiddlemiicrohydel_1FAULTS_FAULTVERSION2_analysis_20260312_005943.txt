1) Critical formula bug (fix this first)
- What is wrong: cell R19 (the discount-factor row for costs) uses
  =Q19/(1/1.12)
  That divides by (1/1.12), i.e. multiplies by 1.12, so the discount factor jumps up in 2011 and every DF to the right is wrong. Because G20:AA20 (PV of costs) multiply yearly costs by these DFs, costs from 2011 onward are massively over‑stated. NPV and B/C are therefore wrong.
- Why it matters: once one DF is wrong the cascade R19→S19→T19… makes all later discount factors wrong and all present values that use them wrong.
- Correct formula: change R19 to
  =Q19*(1/1.12)
  or simpler
  =Q19/1.12
  (This matches the pattern used in H19:I19:… and keeps DF = 1/(1.12)^(n-1).)
- Quick fix steps:
  1. Edit R19 and replace with =Q19/1.12
  2. Fill/copy R19 to the right (S19:AA19) so the chain continues correctly.
  3. Recalculate workbook. Present-value rows (G20:AA20 and C20) will update and NPV and B/C will change.
