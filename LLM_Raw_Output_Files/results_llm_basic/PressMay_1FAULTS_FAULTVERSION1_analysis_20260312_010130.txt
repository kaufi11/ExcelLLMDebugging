Summary of the error
- Row 6 (cell D6) is wrong. Its formula is =E6/0.7 but every other MEEC Price cell uses =E#*0.7.  
- Result: D6 gives ~28.56 instead of the expected ~13.99 (E6 = 19.99).

Why it's wrong (short)
- Division by 0.7 increases the US price; multiplication by 0.7 reduces it. The sheet intends MEEC Price = 70% of USPrice, so it must be E*0.7.

How to fix (quick)
1. Replace D6 with:
   =E6*0.7
   (or to round to 2 decimals)
   =ROUND(E6*0.7,2)
2. To ensure every row is correct, put this formula in D2 then fill (drag or double‑click the fill handle) down through D429:
   =ROUND(E2*0.7,2)
3. Format column D as Currency (or Number with 2 decimals).

How to check for other mistakes
- Use Find (Ctrl+F) in Excel and search for "/0.7" or for "/" in column D formulas to catch accidental divisions.
- Or add a temporary helper column with:
   =IF(ROUND(D2,2)=ROUND(E2*0.7,2),"OK","Check")
  and fill down to flag rows where D ≠ E*0.7.
