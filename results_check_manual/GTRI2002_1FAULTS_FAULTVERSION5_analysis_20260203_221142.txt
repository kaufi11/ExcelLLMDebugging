Short answer — I found two clear errors that will distort totals and any charts:

1) 2002 Report — C30 is wrong (multiplication instead of division)
- Problem: C30 is " =G30*1000000 " but every other C-row uses Gn/1000000. G30 = 6,367,581 so the formula multiplies by 1,000,000 (result ≈ 6.37E+12) instead of converting pounds to millions of pounds.
- Effect: any sums or averages that include C30 are inflated by a factor of 1,000,000.
- Fix: change C30 to =G30/1000000. After fixing, C30 will be 6.367581 (millions of pounds) and column totals will be correct.

2) 2003 Report — stray values in G20 and H20
- Problem: G20 = 8.449074 and H20 = 0.6381540899 sit in columns that have no headers in the 2003 Report table (B–F and K–O are the actual tables). Those two numbers look like misplaced data (probably from a different table) and are not labeled.
- Effect: they can confuse readers and will be included by any formulas or charts that use a contiguous range that includes columns G–H, producing wrong results or extra series.
- Fix: either delete G20 and H20, or move them to the correct column(s) if they were intended elsewhere. Then re-check any charts or SUM ranges that might have been picking up columns G:H.

Extra quick checks (recommended)
- Use Trace Precedents/Dependents on any suspicious totals to see where bad cells feed results.
- Use Go To Special → Constants to spot stray numbers in ranges that should contain only formulas.
- When fixing a single-row formula, copy the correct formula down the column to ensure consistency.
