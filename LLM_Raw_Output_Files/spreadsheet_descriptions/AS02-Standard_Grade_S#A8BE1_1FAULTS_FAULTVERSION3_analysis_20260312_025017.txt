Overview: The workbook contains Standard Grade (SG), Higher Grade (HG) and Certificate (CS) tables reporting entries, awards, grade distributions, sex breakdowns, per-candidate statistics and appeals (1998–2002). Layout is highly regular: subjects in column A, counts/entries in left columns, grade columns (1–7) across, summary rows (subtotals / totals) and percentage/ change columns to the right.

Sheet-by-sheet summary (concise)

- SG1 — "TREND IN ENTRIES" (1998–2002)
  - Columns: A = Subject, B:F = entries by year (1998–2002), G = 2001/2002 % change.
  - Change formula example: G7..G49 use =(F#-E#)/E#.
  - Subtotals row (B53:E53 use SUM over subject range). F53 shown as COUNT(F7:F51) (odd — likely should be SUM). G53 = (F53-E53)/E53.
  - Some missing/placeholder values (e.g. F44 = "-" for Office and Information Studies).

- SG2 — "ENTRIES, AWARDS AND NUMBERS OF CENTRES, 2002"
  - Columns: B = entries, C–I = awards at grades 1–7, J = number of centres.
  - Subtotals (row 51) use SUM across rows; percentage rows (row 52) compute each grade column divided by total entries (e.g. C52 = C51/$B$51).
  - A separate block for Writing element subjects; final totals (row 62) combine writing + main subtotals.

- SG3 — "PERCENTAGE GRADE DISTRIBUTION, 2002"
  - Same subject order; C = entries, D–J = % at grades 1–7, K = % no award.
  - Percentages are precomputed (not formulas shown) for each subject and element. All-subject totals in A177..C177 (All subjects = 490,485 entries; overall % distribution in D177..K177).

- SG4a / SG4b / SG4c — "ENTRIES AND AWARDS BY SEX"
  - SG4a: Male candidate counts and grade breakdowns (per subject).
  - SG4b: Female candidate counts and grade breakdowns.
  - SG4c: Combined comparative stats (% male / % female of entries and % of male/female achieving grade bands). Columnar layout provides total entries, % male/female, and various grade-band comparisons.
  - Subtotals: SG4a B52 = 229,984 (male entries), SG4b B52 = 226,304 (female entries), totals B63/B63 around 243,939/246,546 in other rows; combined total entries B62 (SG4c) = 490,485 and overall subtotals B52 (SG4c) = 456,288 in one view (several totals correspond to inclusive/exclusive of writing elements).

- SG5 — "PER-CANDIDATE STATISTICS"
  - SG5a: distribution of number of Standard Grade entries per candidate (total candidates B17 = 62,521; total entries B18 = 456,288; entries/cand B19 ≈ 7.298).

Common formulas and patterns
- Change over time: (F#-E#)/E# used repeatedly to compute year-on-year percent change (SG1 G col, HG1 G col, CS1 etc).
- Aggregation: SUM ranges for subtotals/totals (SUM(B7:B51), SUM across grade columns).
- Percentages: column totals divided by overall total (e.g. C52 = C51/$B$51), and many precomputed percent distributions in SG3/HG/HG4 sections.
- Counts: COUNT used in at least one place (SG1 F53 = COUNT(F7:F51) — appears anomalous if intended to be SUM).
- Cumulative and banded statistics: SG4c and SG5 use cumulative banding (1–2, 1–4, 1–6) and % male/female comparisons.
