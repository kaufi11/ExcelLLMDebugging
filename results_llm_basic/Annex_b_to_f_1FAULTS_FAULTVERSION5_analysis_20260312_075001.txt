1) Cashflow ↔ Balance sheet reconciliation uses the wrong cell (Total vs year‑end)
- What’s wrong: The reconciliation cells in the cashflow sheet are comparing the Total column value (T19 or T42) to balance sheet items, but they should compare the year‑end (July) closing balance. Example:
  - Cell T26 formula: =T19 - ('Table 2.0 BS - Annex E'!H20 + 'Table 2.0 BS - Annex E'!H21 + 'Table 2.0 BS - Annex E'!H22)
    - T19 here resolves to the Total column value, but the balance-sheet references are the Original FFR year-end (column H). The reconciliation should compare the July closing balance (S19) to the balance‑sheet year‑end values (H20:H22) — not the Totals sum.
  - Same pattern in the revised/actual reconciliation (T49): it compares T42 against I20:I22 when it should be the July closing (S42) vs I20:I22.
- Why it matters: Totals (T19 / T42) are sums across the months and are not the same as the year‑end closing cash figure. This causes false non‑zero reconciliation results and breaks the “these cells must be zero” checks.
- Fix (exact formulas):
  - Replace T26 with:
    =S19 - ('Table 2.0 BS - Annex E'!H20 + 'Table 2.0 BS - Annex E'!H21 + 'Table 2.0 BS - Annex E'!H22)
  - Replace T49 with:
    =S42 - ('Table 2.0 BS - Annex E'!I20 + 'Table 2.0 BS - Annex E'!I21 + 'Table 2.0 BS - Annex E'!I22)
- Explanation: S19 is the July closing cash balance in the cashflow table (the figure you must reconcile to the balance sheet year‑end numbers in column H). Likewise S42 is the July closing for the Actual/Revised section and must be reconciled to the balance sheet Mid‑Year column I.
