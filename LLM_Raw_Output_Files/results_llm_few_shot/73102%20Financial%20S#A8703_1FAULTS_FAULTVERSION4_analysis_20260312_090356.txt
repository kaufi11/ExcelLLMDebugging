Summary of the problem
- The sheet has inconsistent/duplicated property & equipment subtotals which causes the Assets total to be built two different ways and can make Assets ≠ Liabilities+Capital.
  - There are two gross PPE subtotals: D30 (SUM of D24:D29) and D162 (SUM of D155:D161). They both reference the same detail accounts.
  - Total Assets (D178) is built by adding the gross PPE (D162) and accumulated depreciation (D170) separately, while elsewhere Net PPE (D34) is calculated from D30 and D32. Mixing these two aggregation methods produces inconsistencies / risk of double counting.

Faulty cells (most important)
1) D34 (Net property and equipment) — inconsistent source
   - Current formula: =D30 + D32
   - D30 is a subtotal that references the detailed lines and D32 = D170 (accumulated depreciation). This works, but it mixes one subtotal (D30) with another subtotal (D170) while there is a direct gross subtotal D162 available. It is fragile/inconsistent.

2) D178 (TOTAL ASSETS) — inconsistent aggregation
   - Current formula: =D83+D96+D114+D132+D139+D144+D152+D162+D170+D175
   - This adds gross PPE (D162) and accumulated depreciation (D170) directly, while elsewhere the sheet already has a Net PPE cell (D34). Because there are duplicate gross subtotals (D30 vs D162) and multiple ways of rolling up PPE, totals can disagree.
