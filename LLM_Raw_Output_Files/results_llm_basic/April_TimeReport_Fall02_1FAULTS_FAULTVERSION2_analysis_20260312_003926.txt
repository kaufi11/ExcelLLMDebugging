Findings
- Cell H19 uses COUNT(H20:H34) while every other column in row 19 uses SUM(...). COUNT returns the number of numeric cells (15), not the sum of hours (21).
- Because H19 is wrong, the Studio I subtotal (H19) and the overall weekly total (H35) for Week 10 are both incorrect by 6 hours (21 expected − 15 actual = 6).

Why it's wrong (short)
- COUNT counts how many numeric cells are present. You want the total hours, so you must SUM the numeric values.

Fix (do this)
1. Select H19.
2. Replace the formula with:
   =SUM(H20:H34)
3. Press Enter.

Result
- H19 will become 21 (the sum of H20:H34) instead of 15.
- H35 (the Week 10 grand total) will automatically increase by 6 to reflect the correct total.

Recommendation
- Scan the sheet for other accidental COUNT (or AVERAGE) uses where SUM is intended. A quick check: use Find (Ctrl+F) for "COUNT(" to locate any similar mistakes.
