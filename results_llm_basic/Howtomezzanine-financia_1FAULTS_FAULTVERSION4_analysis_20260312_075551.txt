I checked both sheets and found the main issues. Short summary, fixes, and why they matter.

1) Wrong operator in H14 (Services per sq ft)
- Error: H14 = $C14 * $C$5 (multiplying total Services by Total Area)
- Problem: H-column is “per sq ft”. Multiplying makes the per-sq-ft number huge and corrupts every subtotal that uses H14.
- Fix: change to H14 = $C14 / $C$5
- Why: dividing the total annual Services cost by Total Area gives the correct cost per sq ft.

2) Inconsistent / incorrect total + markup calculation in column C and F (TOTAL CHARGE)
- Error:
  - C37 = SUM(C12:C35) * 0.9  (mysterious 0.9 multiplier)
  - F37 = C37 + SUMPRODUCT(C12:C35, F12:F35)
- Problems:
  - C37 arbitrarily reduces the sum of costs by 10% (0.9) — that’s unexpected unless you intentionally want a 10% discount; it’s undocumented and likely wrong.
  - F37 adds the SUMPRODUCT of costs*markup to C37 rather than applying each markup to its cost. That gives the wrong total charged amount when different items have different markups.
- Fixes:
  - If you want C37 = total of the base annual costs, use: C37 = SUM(C12:C35)
    (remove the *0.9) — unless you intentionally want a 10% reduction (then document it).
  - For the total after applying each item’s markup, replace F37 with:
    F37 = SUMPRODUCT(C12:C35, 1 + F12:F35)
    (this applies each item’s markup then sums)
- Why: SUMPRODUCT(C, 1+F) computes sum of cost*(1+markup) for each line, which is the correct way to get the total charged amount when markups differ by line.
