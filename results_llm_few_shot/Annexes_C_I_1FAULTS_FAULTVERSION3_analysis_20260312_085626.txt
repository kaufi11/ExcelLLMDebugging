I checked the workbook and found a few clear formula errors (likely caused by copy/paste or range slip‑ups). Below are the problems, why they’re wrong, and the simple fixes.

1) Wrong percent variance cell
- Location: annex e, cell K68
- Problem: K68 contains =K48 (copies a percent from a different line). That gives a meaningless % for “Total Funds.”
- Fix: replace with the standard variance % formula used everywhere else:
  =IF(H68=0,"N/A",(J68/H68)*100)
- Why: K68 must calculate J68 as a percent of H68 (not reuse K48).

2) “Financed by” monthly totals use only July instead of summing months
- Locations (original cash forecast): annex g
  - T22 currently =S22 (should be the total across months H:S)
  - T23 currently =S23
  - T24 currently =SUM(T22:T23) — this depends on the wrong T22/T23
- Locations (actual/revised forecast): annex g
  - T45 currently =S45
  - T46 currently =S46
  - T47 currently =SUM(T45:T46)
- Problem: T22/T23/T45/T46 only pick the last month (S) rather than the sum for the whole year, so total financing numbers are wrong.
- Fixes (apply both to original and actual sections):
  - T22 → =SUM(H22:S22)
  - T23 → =SUM(H23:S23)
  - T24 → =SUM(H24:S24)   (or keep =SUM(T22:T23) after you fix T22/T23)
  - T45 → =SUM(H45:S45)
  - T46 → =SUM(H46:S46)
  - T47 → =SUM(H47:S47)   (or keep =SUM(T45:T46) after you fix T45/T46)
- Why: totals in column T must sum all months (columns H:S), not copy one month.

3) Balance rows: total column T references wrong cells
- Locations: annex g
  - T18 currently =H18 (opening balance total)
  - T19 currently =H18 (closing balance total)
  - T41 currently =H41
  - T42 currently =S42 (inconsistent)
- Problem: the T column for balance lines should show the July closing (or consistent total), not a stray H18 reference. Current formulas are inconsistent and produce wrong reconciliation figures.
- Recommended fixes:
  - For opening/closing balance rows use the July column (S) in T (this is consistent with other rows showing monthly chains):
    - T18 → =S18
    - T19 → =S19
    - T41 → =S41
    - T42 → =S42
  - Alternatively, if you prefer column T to be the sum across months (less common for balances), use =SUM(H18:S18) etc. Choose one convention and keep it consistent.
- Why: the T column should consistently represent the intended aggregation (monthly sum or final month); current mixed references break the cash reconciliation.
