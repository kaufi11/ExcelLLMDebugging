Summary of workbook: "Consolidated Historical Data (Quarterly)" — 3 sheets (0–2). Units shown: Millions of yen. Columns C:R hold quarter-by-quarter data for 2000–2003 (C = 2000 Q1, D = 2000 Q2 … R = 2003 Q4). Footnotes document an accounting-change restatement and sales-recognition standard adoption.

Sheet 0 — Table of Contents
- Simple TOC: lists pages/sections (Quarterly Statement (1), Sales by Product and P&L, Sales by Region, Quarterly Statement (2), Segment Information by Product).
- Timestamp in B25.

Sheet 1 — "1. Quarterly Statement (1)"
- Sections:
  - Sales by Product and P&L (rows 8–36): product rows (Office imaging, Computer peripherals, Business information), Business machines total (row 12), Cameras (13), Optical (14), Total net sales (row 15). Net Sales row (17) links to Total net sales.
  - P&L flow: Cost of sales (row 18), Gross profit = Net sales − Cost of sales (row 19), % of sales = Gross profit / Net sales with IF to avoid divide-by-zero (row 20), S.G.&A. (row 21), Operating profit = Gross profit − S.G.&A. (row 22), % of sales for operating profit (row 23).
  - Other income/deductions (rows 24–28): Interest & dividend income, Interest expense (negative), Other net; row 28 sums them (SUM).
  - Income before taxes = Operating profit + Other income (row 29), % of sales (row 30).
  - Income taxes (31), Minority interests (32), Income before cumulative effect = Income before taxes − (taxes + minority) (row 33).
  - Cumulative effect of accounting change present in G34 (3,692) and Net income = SUM(income before cumulative effect, cumulative effect) (row 35). Net-income % of sales uses IF (row 36).
- Sales by Region (rows 38–46): Japan and Overseas (Americas, Europe, Other areas). Overseas total uses SUM; Total uses SUM(Japan, Overseas).
- Key formula patterns: many totals are SUM ranges, differences for profit lines, and IF(condition, alternate, ratio) for % of sales.

Sheet 2 — "2. Quarterly Statement (2)" (Segment information by product)
- Segment rows:
  - Business machines (Net sales row 9, Operating profit row 10, % of sales row 11 using IF).
  - Cameras (Net sales row 16, Operating profit row 17, % row 18).
  - Optical & other products (Net sales row 23, Operating profit row 24, % row 25).
  - Corporate and Eliminations (Net sales row 30, Operating profit row 31 — these are negative).
- Consolidated (rows 36–39):
  - Consolidated Net sales = SUM of segment Net sales (row 37).
  - Consolidated Operating profit = SUM of segment operating profits (row 38).
  - Consolidated % of sales uses IF to avoid divide-by-zero (row 39).
- Key formula patterns: segment aggregation via SUM(C9,C16,C23,C30) etc.; % rows consistently use IF(range=0,"",numerator/denominator).

General notes
- Layout is consistent across quarters/years — same rows repeated for each quarter (C:R).
- Formulas are mostly column-wise copies: SUM for aggregation, simple arithmetic for margin/profit lines, and IF tests to prevent division-by-zero in percentage rows.
- Footnotes (rows ~47) explain restatement due to adoption of new sales-recognition standards and a 2001 classification change for loss on disposal.
