Findings (short)
- Kauai Community College sheet (KauCC) — cell E20 uses =COUNT(C20:D20). That is wrong: COUNT returns how many numeric cells (result = 2), not the sum of articles. E20 should be the sum of C20 and D20 (112).
- Across sheets the bottom "Total" formulas in row 29 are not ideal. Many sheets use E29 =SUM(C29:D29). That gives the correct number only because C29 and D29 already hold column totals, but it is fragile and not best practice.

Why these are errors (short)
- COUNT(C20:D20) does not add values. COUNT simply counts numeric cells (so it returns 2), producing a wrong Total for Image Collection on KauCC.
- Using E29 =SUM(C29:D29) ties the E total to manually entered/other totals in row 29. If you recalc or change row totals, or if you want E29 to reflect the sums of individual database totals, the robust approach is to sum the E column (E10:E28). Same for B29/C29/D29.

How to fix (exact formulas to use)
1) Immediate fix for KauCC cell E20:
   - Replace =COUNT(C20:D20) with =SUM(C20:D20)

2) Make totals robust on every sheet (recommended):
   - B29 =SUM(B10:B28)
   - C29 =SUM(C10:C28)
   - D29 =SUM(D10:D28)
   - E29 =SUM(E10:E28)
   (Apply these formulas on each sheet)

How to apply quickly
- Select the wrong cell (E20 on KauCC), type the new formula, press Enter.
- To update totals: enter the B29/C29/D29/E29 formulas once and copy the same pattern to other sheets (right-click sheet tab → Move or Copy, or copy/paste formulas).
- Or on one sheet use AutoFill or drag the formula across then copy the corrected totals to other sheets.

That will correct the wrong value and make your totals dynamic and reliable.
