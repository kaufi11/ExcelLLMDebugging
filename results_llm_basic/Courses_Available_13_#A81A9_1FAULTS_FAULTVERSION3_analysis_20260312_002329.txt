Summary — the numbering breaks at row 34 because of one bad formula.

Error
- Cell A34 contains =-A33-1. That subtracts A33 and 1 (giving a negative), so A35 and all following numbers are wrong.

Quick fix
1. Edit A34 and replace the formula with =A33+1 (or =+A33+1).
2. Copy/fill A34 down through the rest of column A to restore the sequence.

Why this fixes it
- =-A33-1 computes -(A33)-1 (negative). =A33+1 computes the next sequential number.

Safer, more robust formula (optional)
- Put 1 in A4. In A5 use:
  =IF(B5<>"",A4+1,"")
  then fill down.
- This only numbers rows that have a course name in column B and prevents gaps or stray numbers when rows are empty.

Notes
- The leading + in formulas (=+A4+1) is harmless in Excel; you can leave it or remove it.
