1) DRAM cost side — wrong price reference in 2001 var-profit

  - D31 is “price after fab add” (not the baseline price).
- Correct formula: =+D13*D9*D10 - D16 - D9*D17

- Why it matters: D21 is var profit per wafer for 2001. Using D31 instead of D13 pulls in the post‑addition price and gives an incorrect profit number for 2001, which flows into wafer profit, cash flows and the IRR for that scenario.
- Fix: Edit D21 to reference D13 instead of D31, then recalc.

2) DRAM cost side — amortization typo for H column

3) fisher sheet — malformed Paasche & inconsistent Laspeyres ranges in column D
There are copy/paste / range errors in the 2002 (column D) index formulas. Fix both Laspeyres (D37) and Paasche (D38) so they match the pattern used in other years.

- Problems (cells):
  - fisher!D37 (Laspeyres) currently uses range C24:C27 and D31:D34 (wrong base-year ranges).
  - fisher!D38 (Paasche) is malformed: it has extra *(1/(1-D28)) and misses the correct +D28*1/D35 term.
- Correct formulas:

- Why it matters: Laspeyres/Paasche are the building blocks for the Fisher price index (fisher!D39) and the chained index (fisher!D40 etc). Wrong ranges/extra terms give the wrong price index, which feeds LEM Demand Calibration and all price/quantity inversions — ultimately corrupting price, revenue, and IRR results everywhere downstream.
- Fix: replace D37 and D38 with the corrected formulas above, then recalc the fisher chained index and any dependent sheets (LEM Demand Calibration, Capacity&Price Calculation, Static RoI, etc).
