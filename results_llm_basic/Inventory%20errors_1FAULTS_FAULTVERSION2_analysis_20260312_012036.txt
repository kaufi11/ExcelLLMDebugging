Short answer — two separate problems:

1) A plain Excel formula error (wrong function)
- Where: Sheet2 cell C24.
- Problem: C24 says =COUNT(B20:B23) so it returns 4 (count of cells) instead of summing the asset amounts.
- Fix: change C24 to =SUM(B20:B23).
- Why: COUNT returns number of numeric cells; SUM adds the values. This gives the correct Total Assets for 2001.

2) An accounting/logic error (prior‑period inventory not closed to retained earnings)
- Symptom: the balance sheet “doesn’t balance” even though arithmetic formulas reference the inventory cells correctly.
- Cause: ending inventory was wrong in prior year(s). That error affected Cost of Goods Sold and Net Income in the prior year, but retained earnings for the next year were not adjusted to reflect the prior-year error. So Assets (inventory) changed without the matching change to Equity (retained earnings), leaving Assets ≠ Liabilities + Equity.
- How to fix (two equivalent approaches):
  a) Correct the ending inventory amounts themselves so the income statement/net income for the prior year is correct (e.g., B11 = 179800 + 17000 → 196,800; D11 = 147200 - 25000 → 122,200). The corrected ending inventory will flow into COGS and Net Income and fix the balance sheet.
  b) If the prior-year income was already closed, adjust retained earnings for the prior-period error. Example shown on Sheet3:
     - For 2002 retained earnings (cell D35) use =E17 + 17000 (add the 17,000 prior-year understatement).
     - For 2003 retained earnings (cell F35) use =G17 - 25000 (subtract the 25,000 prior-year overstatement).
     Use the exact amounts equal to the inventory errors in the prior year(s).
- Why that works: an inventory misstatement in year N changes net income in year N. When you open year N+1, retained earnings must reflect the corrected cumulative income through year N. Adjusting retained earnings or correcting the prior-year inventory restores the accounting equation: Assets = Liabilities + Equity.

Quick checklist to make the file balance:
