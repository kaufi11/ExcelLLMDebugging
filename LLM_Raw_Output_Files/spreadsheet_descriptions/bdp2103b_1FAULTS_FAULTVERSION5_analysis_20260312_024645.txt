Overview
- Workbook contains at least 16 sheets; only Sheet1 is populated in the data you provided. Sheet1 is a 2003/04 financial projection for "Scotland Against Drugs / NHS Health Scotland".
- Layout repeats a standard budget table three times (headers at rows ~6, ~44, ~87) and covers programme categories, monthly forecasts and year-to-date / year-end columns.

Structure and key columns on Sheet1
- Column A: Expenditure category (sections and line items).
- Columns C and D: Annual allocation (C = budget allocation; D = revised annual allocation).
- Column F: "period" / Year-to-date (6 month) actuals.
- Columns G–L: Monthly forecasts for Oct (G) through Mar (L).
- Column M: 12-month forecast (period total). Almost all M cells are formulas: Mx = SUM(Fx:Lx).
- Column O: Projected underspend / year-end variance, generally calculated as D - M (most Ox cells are FORMULA =SUM(Dx - Mx) or equivalent).
- Header rows annotate meanings: F = Year to date / actual, M = Forecast (12 month), O = Projected year end (over/(-)).

Formula patterns
- Most monthly totals: Mx = SUM(Fx:Lx).
- Most subtotals: row subtotals use SUM over the constituent rows, e.g., C26 = SUM(C15:C25), D26 = SUM(D15:D25), and similar for monthly columns.
- Category totals combine subtotals, e.g., C72 = SUM(C12+C26+C31+C38+C56+C62+C70).
- Underspend / projection column: Ox typically computed as D x − M x (many shown as FORMULA =SUM(Dx-Mx) which is functionally Dx−Mx).
- Net lines combine gross and income (e.g., C80 = SUM(C76 + C78)).

Notable inconsistencies / potential issues to review

- O95 is listed as FORMULA =SUM(E95-M95) — E95 is not used elsewhere and likely a typo; expected D95-M95. This should be corrected to avoid an erroneous underspend calculation.
- Some rows have missing monthly inputs (intended blanks), which is normal for lumpy expenditure but worth checking completeness.
