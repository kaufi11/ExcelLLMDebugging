1) Major data error — antigorite (atg) has wrong molar mass and molar volume (big effect)
- What’s wrong
  - database sheet, row for atg (A38): B38 = 4535.95 (g/mol) and C38 = 1712.972 (cm3/mol). Those are ~16× too large for antigorite.
  - rock mineral modes sheet, row for atg (A38): the chemical formula is wrong (given as Ca2Al3Si3O12(OH) — that is not antigorite).
- Why it matters
  - Any rock or mineral calculations that include antigorite will be strongly biased (density, H2O content, moduli, velocities). The large gfw inflates molar-volume-related calculations and H2O wt% etc.
- Fix (exact steps)
  1. In database!B38 set the correct molar mass for antigorite (Mg3Si2O5(OH)4). Exact stoichiometric molar mass = 277.118 g/mol. Put: 277.118
  2. Recompute molar volume consistently (database!C38). Use the density in D38 (2585 kg/m3) to produce the molar volume:
     - Formula to use: =B38/(D38/1000) or =B38*1000/D38
     - With B38=277.118 and D38=2585 you get C38 ≈ 107.227 cm3/mol.
  3. In rock mineral modes!C38 fix the chemical formula to the standard antigorite/serpentine formula: Mg3Si2O5(OH)4 (and update the description D38 to “antigorite / serpentine” if you want).
- Result
  - All mineral/rock properties that use antigorite will immediately become realistic. (Minerals sheet and rocks sheet values that depend on B38/C38 will update.)
