Findings (short)
- On sheet "2003-04 per credit hour" cell H14 is wrong: it contains =COUNT(H10:H13) instead of =SUM(H10:H13).
- Because H14 is used in H21 and H25, the error propagates and makes the Veterinary per-term totals (resident and non‑resident) far too small.

Why it's wrong (simple)
- COUNT returns the number of numeric cells (here 4), not the sum of their values. You need SUM to add the dollar amounts.

Quick fix
1. Go to sheet "2003-04 per credit hour".
2. Select H14 and replace the formula with:
   =SUM(H10:H13)
3. Press Enter. Totals (H21, H25) will update automatically.

Corrected example values (after fix)
- H14 (Subtotal State Components) becomes 5,435.23
- H21 (Total Florida Resident Rate/Term, Veterinary) becomes 5,862.03
- H25 (Total Non‑Resident Rate/Term, Veterinary) becomes 15,892.53

Prevention / check
- Use Find (Ctrl+F) to search for COUNT( in the workbook to catch accidental COUNTs used where SUM is intended.
- Use consistent formula copying across columns (copy the correct SUM formula from another column to H14) or show formulas (Formulas → Show Formulas) to visually scan for mismatches.
