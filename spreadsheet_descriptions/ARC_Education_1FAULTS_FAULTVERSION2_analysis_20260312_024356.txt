Summary of workbook structure and contents

Overall
- Workbook contains metro-area education statistics by school system (rows for systems: CLAYTON, COBB, DEKALB, DOUGLAS, FULTON, GWINNETT, ROCKDALE, ATLANTA, BUFORD, DECATUR, MARIETTA, plus TOTAL).  
- Each sheet presents time series across columns (years) with metrics appropriate to that sheet (attendance & teachers, graduates, dropouts, teacher advanced degrees, state test scores, SAT, university degrees).  
- Missing / unavailable data consistently marked "**". Several cells contain nonstandard entries (text ratios like "16-1", a few numeric anomalies such as 1.548 in B15 of Student‑teacher).  
- Notes and source citations appear on most sheets (Georgia Dept. of Education; report card URLs).

Sheet-by-sheet (concise)

1) Student-teacher
- Purpose: Student-to-teacher data for metro systems across years (1990‑91 through 1996‑97 in two blocks).  
- Layout: Repeating 3‑column blocks per year: Average Daily Attendance, Teachers, Ratio (e.g., B/E/H = attendance columns; C/F/I = teacher counts; D/G/J = ratios).  
- Totals: Several TOTAL rows; many totals are formulas: e.g., K37 = SUM(K26:K36), L37 = SUM(L26:L36). Other year totals use SUM (e.g., row56 in second block contains SUM formulas for attendance and teachers). Some TOTAL cells are left "**".  
- Notes: Continuation header rows for subsequent years; some inconsistent/missing values (Atlanta, Buford, etc).

2) Public Grads
- Purpose: Number of public high school graduates by system, years 1990‑91 to 1998‑99.  
- Layout: Systems in rows, years in columns B:J.  
- Formulas: TOTAL row (row16) uses column sums: B16:J16 each = SUM(B5:B15), SUM(C5:C15), etc.  
- Missing data: Many "**" entries for Atlanta, Buford, Decatur, Marietta in early years.  
- Note: Bottom note: "** Not available".

3) Drop-out
- Purpose: Dropout rate per 1,000 students (grades 8–12) across years (1989‑90 through 1998‑99).  
- Layout: Years in columns B:K; systems in rows.  
- No formulas shown; source and measurement note (for 1994‑95 & 1995‑96 dropouts measured 9‑12).

4) Teachers adv degrees (Teachers With Advanced Degrees)
- Purpose: Percent and counts of teachers with advanced degrees for multiple year ranges (1989‑1990 through 1999‑2000 presented in two blocks).  
- Layout: For each year block there are two columns: Percent and Total No. (e.g., B/C, D/E, F/G...). Systems in rows.  
- Formulas/format issues: Upper block shows Percent as percentages (e.g., 50.9) and totals as integers. Lower block (1995‑2000) lists Percent as decimal fractions (e.g., B21 = 0.4690...) and totals in adjacent columns.  
- Data quirks: Some totals are non-integers (e.g., 1995.8), inconsistent percent formats across blocks.

5) State test (Average scores, 8th graders)
- Purpose: Average scores on state criterion‑referenced tests for Reading and Math.  
- Layout: Two major sections: Reading (rows 6–18) and Math (rows 23–35). Years across columns B:K covering 1989‑90 to 1998‑99. Includes State Average row for both subjects.  
- Data oddities: Early columns (1991‑92 etc) sometimes show small integers where older scaled scores appear (likely formatting issues).

6) SAT
- Purpose: Average SAT scores by system, years 1990‑91 through 1999‑2000 (columns B:K).  
- Layout: Systems in rows; many "**" values where data are unavailable. State Average provided (row16).  
- Notes: Source / note about missing data.

7) university (Degrees & certificates conferred)
- Purpose: Degrees conferred by Metro-area University System schools (four‑year and two‑year institutions), years 1990–1999 (columns B:K).  
- Layout: Two blocks: Four Year Colleges (Georgia Tech, Georgia State, Kennesaw State, Southern Poly) with a Total row (row9), then Two Year Colleges with a Total row (row15).  
- Formulas: Totals use SUM formulas (e.g., B9 = SUM(B5:B8), C9 = SUM(C5:C8), etc.; B15 = SUM(B12:B14)). Note: some formulas appear to reference incorrect ranges (C15 = SUM(C5:C14) looks like a likely typo and should probably be SUM(C12:C14)).  
- Sources listed.

Key formulas and computational notes
