Short answer — cause and fix

1) Root cause (why the statement doesn't balance)
- An inventory misstatement changed ending inventory (an asset) and therefore cost of goods sold → net income, but retained earnings on the balance sheet were not adjusted for the prior‑period inventory errors.  
- Result: Assets changed but Equity did not reflect the cumulative prior errors, so Assets ≠ Liabilities + Equity.

2) The explicit errors in the workbook
- Sheet2 shows the inventory errors: 2001 ending inventory is overstated by 17,000 (B11 = 179,800 + 17,000) and 2002 ending inventory is understated by 25,000 (D11 = 147,200 − 25,000). Because retained earnings were left as current net income, the balance sheet is out of balance.
- (Minor spreadsheet mistake on Sheet3) B12 is shown as =B10−B11 but should be =B10+B11 (cost of goods available = beginning inventory + purchases).

3) How to fix (two options)
Option A — Proper accounting correction (recommended)
- Close the prior‑period inventory errors to retained earnings (first period presented) so equity reflects the cumulative error.
- In your sheet this is what Sheet3 does:
  - For 2002 retained earnings cell (D35) set =E17 + 17000  (add the 2001 overstatement)
  - For 2003 retained earnings cell (F35) set =G17 − 25000  (remove the 2002 understatement)
- After these adjustments Total Liabilities & Equity will equal Total Assets.

Option B — Restate prior period numbers
- Adjust the prior period balances (beginning inventory, cost of goods, retained earnings) and recalculate income and equity for each affected year so every year is consistent. This is the full restatement approach.

4) What to change in the workbook right now (practical Excel steps)

Why this works (brief)
- Ending inventory errors change net income (through COGS). The cumulative effect of prior-year errors belongs in opening retained earnings (not in current year income). Moving those amounts into retained earnings restores the accounting equation: Assets = Liabilities + Equity.
