Detailed findings, examples and recommendations

1) Obvious formula typo in "Coil slope intercepts"
- Cell: Coil slope intercepts!U5
- Formula present: =(E5+$E$15)/$E$15
- Context: All other fractional-delta columns in that block use the form =(E#-$E$15)/$E$15 (subtract the average in $E$15).
- Why it's a problem: U5 uses addition instead of subtraction, producing a different (and likely incorrect) fractional delta.
- Suggested fix: change to =(E5-$E$15)/$E$15 so it matches the pattern used elsewhere.

2) Division-by-zero results (#DIV/0!)
- Many cells intentionally compute ratios G/H (grad(Bdot)/grad(Rog)) or similar; wherever H (or the denominator) is zero you get #DIV/0!.
- Examples (not exhaustive; I found many occurrences across the workbook):
  - Lens 16: I25, I26, I27, I28 are #DIV/0! (G and/or H are zero for those rows).
  - Lens 18: several I‑cells (I7, I8, I11, I12, etc.) are #DIV/0! where H=0.
  - Lens 22, Lens 23, Lens 24 retest, Lens 27, Lens 28: multiple rows have #DIV/0! entries in the "grad(Bdot)/grad(Rog)" column (I).
- Recommendation:
  - If dividing by zero is expected (missing calibration or intentionally zero), wrap the division to avoid the error and make intent explicit, e.g.:
    - =IF($Hn=0,"", $Gn/$Hn) or =IFERROR($Gn/$Hn,"") or =IF($Hn<>0,$Gn/$Hn,"")
  - Or, check upstream data to avoid zeros in H where they are not meaningful.

3) Inconsistent date text / non-standard date strings
- Some date columns contain strings that are not standard ISO/Excel dates. These are likely typos and will prevent Excel from treating them as dates.
- Examples:

4) Inconsistent handling of measured/calculated ratio (P column)

- Why this is a problem: inconsistent UI/behavior across sheets — some rows show the ratio even when others are blank. If the IF gate is intentional (to hide low/invalid ratios), then cells without it are inconsistent.
- Recommendation:
  - Decide on a single rule for showing the measured/calculated ratio and apply consistently. If you want blanks for small ratios, change direct formulas to the same IF wrapper, e.g.:
    - =IF(N8=0,"",IF(O8/N8>0.5,O8/N8,""))
