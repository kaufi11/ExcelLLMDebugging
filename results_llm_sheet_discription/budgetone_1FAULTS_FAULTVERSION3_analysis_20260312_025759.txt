Findings (short)
- Error: D7 contains =771*D4. That’s a hard‑coded wrong multiplier and massively inflates Sales for Q3 (D7 should be price * units).
- Effect: F7 (Total Sales) and every dependent cell (F5, F9, F15, taxes, %-Sales column G, etc.) are wrong.

Fix (one-line)
- Replace D7 with =D5*D4 (or copy B7 across so each quarter uses Price * Units).

What that does / why
- =D5*D4 correctly computes Q3 sales (22 * 2,000 = 44,000). Once corrected Excel will recalc all totals and percentages based on the true sales number instead of the incorrect 771*D4 value.

Corrected key totals (after fix)
- F7 (Total Sales) = 153,000
- F8 (Total COGS) = 62,000
- F9 (Gross Margin) = 91,000
- F14 (Total Operating Costs) = 15,840
- F15 (Operating Income) = 75,160
- F16 (Taxes) = 21,044.80
- F17 (Net Income) = 54,115.20

Quick recommendations to avoid recurrence
- Don’t hard‑code numbers into formulas (like 771). Reference the proper cells.
- Use fill/right to copy formulas across quarters so they remain consistent (e.g., enter =B5*B4 in B7 then drag right).
- Add a simple check cell: =F7-SUM(B7:E7) or highlight any cell in row 7 that doesn’t follow the pattern (conditional formatting) to catch accidental edits.
