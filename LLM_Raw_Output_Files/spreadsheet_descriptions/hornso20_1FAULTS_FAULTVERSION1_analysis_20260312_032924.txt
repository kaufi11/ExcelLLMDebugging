Summary of workbook — structure, contents, key formulas and values

Sheets: E20-9 and E20-9 (2) — Job cost allocation (professionals)
- Purpose: Compute direct/indirect hourly rates, allocation rate, predict cost for a Southwest Light & Gas job, add 20% markup and produce total bid.
- Inputs (both): Direct labor hours = 16,000 (C2); Direct labor cost = 2,240,000 (C3); Office rent = 350,000 (C4); Support staff salaries = 860,000 (C5); Utilities = 246,000 (C6); Estimated job hours = 220 (C8).
- Key formulas:
  - Hourly direct labor rate (C10) = C3 / C2
  - Indirect cost hourly rate (C11) = SUM(C4:C6)  (E20-9 uses SUM(C4:C6)/C2; E20-9 (2) uses a hard-coded 5)
  - Allocation rate (C12) = C11 / C10
  - Predicted job cost (C14) = (C10 + C11) * C8
  - Markup (C16) = C14 * 20%
  - Total bid (C17) = C14 + C16
- Computed (E20-9):
  - Direct hourly rate = 2,240,000 / 16,000 = 140.00
  - Indirect hourly rate = (350,000+860,000+246,000)/16,000 = 1,456,000/16,000 = 91.00
  - Allocation rate = 91 / 140 = 0.65
  - Predicted cost = (140 + 91) * 220 = 231 * 220 = 50,820
  - 20% markup = 10,164; Total bid = 60,984
- Computed (E20-9 (2) with C11 = 5):
  - Direct hourly rate = 140.00; Indirect hourly rate = 5.00
  - Allocation rate = 5 / 140 ≈ 0.035714
  - Predicted cost = (140 + 5) * 220 = 145 * 220 = 31,900
  - 20% markup = 6,380; Total bid = 38,280

Sheets: P20-1B and P20-1B (2) — Manufacturing inventory/journal entries (Castlebury)
- Purpose/layout: Three parallel ledger columns:
  - (a) Work in Process Inventory (columns B–C)
  - (b) Finished Goods Inventory (columns D–E)
  - (c) Cost of Goods Sold (columns F–G)
  - Dates: entries for 2000-11-30 and 2000-12-31 with job numbers and costs.
- Key rows/formulas and values:
  - Work in Process totals: C6 = SUM(C4:C5) = 500 + 500 = 1,000
  - Later period WIP: C10 = SUM(C8:C9) = 1,200 + 600 = 1,800
  - Finished goods transfers: G6 = SUM(G4:G5) = 1,400 + 1,600 = 3,000; G12 = SUM(G8:G11) = 1,300 + 900 + 2,000 + 700 = 4,900
  - Inventory movements:
    - F14 (Finished Goods Inventory at one point) = E4 + G6 = 1,300 + 3,000 = 4,300; G15 = F14 (transfer)
    - F18 = G9 + G10 + G11 = 900 + 2,000 + 700 = 3,600; G19 = F18
  - Sales and receivables: Accounts Receivable = 3,800 (F22); Sales revenue G23 = F22 = 3,800
  - COGS: F26 = 2,000; G27 = F26 = 2,000
- Notes: The (2) sheet is a duplicate of the P20-1B sheet.

Sheets: P20-3B and P20-3B (2) — Job cost record (Alliance Rubber, Job 449)
- Purpose/layout: Job cost record with columns for Direct Materials (C/D), Direct Labor (E/F), Manufacturing Overhead (G–I), totals and transfers to WIP/FG/COGS and revenue.
- Key input rows:
  - Direct materials: C10=593, C11=598, C12=622
  - Direct labor hours and rate calculations (examples):
    - D10 = 20 * 15 = 300
    - D11 = 30 * 9 = 270
    - D12 = 12 * 12 = 144
  - Direct labor totals: F10 = 10 * 19 = 190; F12 = 40 * 18 = 720; F14 = SUM(F10:F12) = 190 + 720 = 910
  - Overhead rate and allocation:
    - H10 = 375 / 250 = 1.5
    - I10 = F14 * H10 (I10 references F14 × H10) → with F14 = 910 → I10 = 1,365
  - Totals:
    - D14 = SUM(D10:D13) = 300 + 270 + 144 = 714 (D13 is blank/assumed zero)
    - I14 (Total cost) = I10 + F14 + D14 = 1,365 + 910 + 714 = 2,989
  - Transfers:
    - F16 = D14 (Work in Process movement)
    - F22 = I10 (Overhead to WIP)
    - F25 = I14 (Finished goods = total job cost = 2,989)
    - F28 (Accounts receivable) = 50 * 110 = 5,500; Sales G29 = F28 = 5,500
    - Cost of goods sold: F31 = F25 = 2,989; G32 = F31
- Notes: The second P20-3B sheet is a duplicate of the first.

Overall layout and flow
- E20-9 sheets are simple costing calculators: input block at top, calculated rates and job cost below, final bid including markup.
- P20-1B sheets act as simplified ledger/journal for inventory flows across WIP, Finished Goods, and COGS with SUM formulas to aggregate rows and explicit transfer lines for completions and sales.
- P20-3B sheets are job cost records: list of material, labor, overhead entries, overhead allocation using a computed rate, totals, then transfers to WIP/FG and revenue/COGS entries.
- Most formulas are straightforward arithmetic (SUM, basic division/multiplication) and several sheets are duplicated with identical layout; a few cells are hard-coded alternatives (e.g., E20-9 (2) uses C11 = 5).
