Findings — primary error
- Sheet 1 (Consolidated Statements of Income): cell H9 (Operating profit for 1997) has the wrong formula. It is H6+H8 (adds Gross profit and S.G.&A.) but all other years use Gross profit − S.G.&A. (H6−H8). This is a sign error.

Why it's wrong
- Operating profit = Gross profit − S.G.&A. Adding S.G.&A. inflates the operating profit value and will cascade wrong results into Income before taxes, Net income and all %‑of‑sales ratios for 1997.

Fix (simple)
1. Go to Sheet1, select cell H9.
2. Replace the formula =H6+H8 with =H6-H8 (or copy the correct formula from an adjacent year, e.g. G9).

Verify
- After change H9 should equal the value in H6 minus H8.
- Check dependent cells: H10 (Operating profit %), H16 (Income before taxes), H17 (% of sales), H20, H22 (Net income) and H23 — they should update automatically. Recalculate the workbook (F9 or Save) to confirm.

Optional additional check
- Search row 9 across the sheet for any other abnormal “+” where “−” is expected (find “+” in formulas or compare the B9 formulas across years) to ensure no other sign mistakes.
