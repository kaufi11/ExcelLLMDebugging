**Error Explanation:**

- In **Sheet1**, the balance sheet does not balance because the inventory errors are not adjusted in retained earnings. The ending inventory values are direct numbers, and the retained earnings (net income) do not reflect prior inventory errors, causing imbalance.
- In **Sheet2**, the ending inventory includes inventory errors (e.g., `=179800+17000`), but retained earnings are not adjusted for these errors. Also, there's a formula error in Total Assets for 2001: `=COUNT(B20:B23)` instead of `=SUM(B20:B23)`.

---

**Solution:**

1. **Adjust Retained Earnings for Inventory Errors:**
   - Add/subtract the prior year's inventory error to retained earnings to reflect the impact of inventory errors on equity.
   - Example in Sheet3: `Retained earnings (2002) = Net Income (2002) + 17000` (prior year error added).
   
2. **Correct Total Assets Formula:**
   - Replace `=COUNT(B20:B23)` with `=SUM(B20:B23)` in Sheet2 for 2001 to correctly sum asset values.

---

**Why This Works:**

- Inventory errors affect cost of goods sold and net income, which in turn affect retained earnings.
- Without adjusting retained earnings, the balance sheet totals (Assets vs. Liabilities + Equity) won't match.
- Correcting retained earnings ensures the balance sheet balances by reflecting the cumulative effect of inventory errors.
- Using `SUM` instead of `COUNT` correctly totals numeric asset values instead of counting cells.

---

**Summary:**

- Always adjust retained earnings for prior inventory errors.
- Use correct formulas to sum totals.
- Sheet3 shows the correct approach; Sheet1 and Sheet2 miss these adjustments causing imbalance.
