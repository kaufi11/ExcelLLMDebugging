Summary of the problems I found
1. Overtime formulas (column G) reference the wrong rows and have sign errors.
   - Example: G3 uses E4 instead of E3; G4 uses E5 instead of E4, etc. G8 also uses +40 instead of -40 and references E10.
   - Cause: an off‑by‑one relative reference when formulas were entered/copied; one formula was also edited incorrectly.

2. Gross pay formulas (column C, rows 15–20) double‑count overtime.
   - Example: C15 = (F3+G3)*B15 adds total hours (F) and overtime hours (G) then multiplies by rate. That counts overtime hours twice.
   - Likely intent is to pay overtime at a higher rate (typically 1.5×). The current formula does not do that.

3. Optional: Week 4 column (E) contains AVERAGE(B:D) for each employee. That is fine if you intentionally estimate week 4 as the average of weeks 1–3, but incorrect if you should be entering actual week‑4 hours.

Correct formulas and short fixes
1. Fix overtime (G3:G8)
   - Replace each G formula with (for row 3 as example):
     =MAX(B3-40,0)+MAX(C3-40,0)+MAX(D3-40,0)+MAX(E3-40,0)
   - Then copy this corrected formula down to G4:G8.
   - This fixes the wrong row references and the +40 errors.

2. Fix gross pay (C15:C20) — assume overtime = 1.5× pay
   - In C15 use:
     =(F3-G3)*B15 + G3*B15*1.5
     (This pays regular hours at normal rate and overtime at 1.5×.)
   - A simpler algebraic equivalent you can use:
     =F3*B15 + G3*B15*0.5
     (same result; easier to copy down)
   - Copy the chosen formula down C16:C20. Make sure you use relative row references so row numbers increment as you copy.

If you do NOT pay time‑and‑a‑half and overtime is paid at the same rate, then correct gross pay is simply:
   =F3*B15
(i.e., do not add G at all).

Why these fixes work (brief)
- Overtime must be calculated from the correct cells (the four weeks for the same employee); your formulas pointed at other rows, so they were pulling the wrong week values.
- (F + G) multiplies total hours plus overtime again, which double-counts the overtime portion. Paying overtime properly requires splitting the hours into regular and overtime portions and paying the overtime portion at the overtime rate (example above uses 1.5×).
