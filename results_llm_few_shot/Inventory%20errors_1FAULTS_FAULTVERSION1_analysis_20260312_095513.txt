Summary of errors and fixes (short & simple)

1) Wrong sign in Sheet3 — Cost of goods available
- Faulty cell: Sheet3 B12
- Problem: B12 is =B10-B11 (subtracts purchases). That is incorrect.
- Fix: Change to =B10+B11
- Why: "Cost of goods available for sale" = beginning inventory + purchases. Subtracting makes COGS, gross profit and net income wrong.

2) Balance-sheet imbalance because prior-year inventory errors weren't closed to retained earnings
- Symptom: Sheet2 shows the balance sheet out by the amount of the inventory error (it does not balance).
- Cause: When an ending-inventory error occurs in year T, it affects year T income and therefore retained earnings. If you change the ending inventory but do not adjust retained earnings (or close the prior-year error), Total Assets will move but Total Equity will not match — the statements won't balance.
- Fix (concept): Adjust retained earnings by the prior-year inventory error (add if prior-year ending inventory was understated; subtract if it was overstated).
- Example (as shown correctly on Sheet3):
  - For 2002 retained earnings cell (Sheet3 D35): =E17 + 17000
  - For 2003 retained earnings cell (Sheet3 F35): =G17 - 25000
- Why: Carrying the prior-year error into retained earnings corrects equity for the past-year misstatement so Assets = Liabilities + Equity again.

Quick checklist to correct the workbook
- Fix Sheet3 B12 to =B10+B11.
- Decide which years have inventory misstatements and add/subtract those error amounts to retained earnings in the first year the error is closed (as shown in Sheet3).
- Recalculate: COGS → Gross Profit → Net Income → Retained Earnings → Total Equity. The balance sheet should then balance.
