I inspected the workbook and found the concrete formula mistakes (plus a couple of bad practices). Short, clear fixes below.

1) Wrong hard-coded number in Total row (mechanical error)
- Faulty cell: AU49
- Problem: AU49 contains =SUM(609*$E$48) — the constant 609 was entered instead of referring to the participant cell (AU48). This produces a wrong cost for that company/class.
- Fix: replace with =AU48*$E$48
- Why: the formula should multiply the participant count in AU48 by the class RISE cost in E48. Using the literal 609 breaks calculation and prevents updates when participants change.

2) A total-of-participants cell was overwritten with a value (data entry error)
- Faulty cell: BK261
- Problem: BK261 contains the value 12 instead of the formula that sums participants across the company columns for that class.
- Fix: replace with the correct formula: =SUM(F261:BG261)
- Why: BK cells are used elsewhere (student days, totals). A hard-coded BK value gives wrong derived results (BL261 etc) and won’t update when participant columns change.

3) Wrong formula pattern in BL261 (result depends on the BK error)
- Faulty cell: BL261
- Problem: currently BL261 =SUM(C261*BK261). While SUM around a single product is harmless, because BK261 is wrong BL261 is wrong too.
- Fix: after fixing BK261, set BL261 = C261 * BK261 (or keep =SUM(C261*BK261) if you prefer; simpler is =C261*BK261).
- Why: BL is "number of student days" = class length (C261) × number participants (BK261). Use direct multiplication for clarity.

4) Several instances of accidental constants or wrong cell used (search & fix)
- What to do: search the sheet for obvious numeric literals inside SUM() multiplications like SUM(609*$E$48) or any SUM(<number>*$E$row). Replace with <cell>*$E$row (example: replace 609 with the proper participant cell). The AU49 case above is one example; there may be others from manual edits.
- Why: constants in place of cell references cause broken totals and won't change when the underlying data changes.

5) Unnecessary SUM() wrappers (style / maintainability)
- Observation: many formulas use SUM() around a single arithmetic expression, e.g. =SUM(F3*$E$3) or =SUM(C3*BK3). These work but are confusing.
- Recommendation: replace with the plain arithmetic expression (e.g. =F3*$E$3, =C3*BK3). This makes formulas clearer and easier to debug.
- Why: removes confusion and reduces chance of accidental range mistakes.
