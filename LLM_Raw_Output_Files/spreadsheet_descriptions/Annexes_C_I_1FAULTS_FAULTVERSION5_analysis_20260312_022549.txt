Summary of workbook structure and key contents

General structure and conventions
- Unit: most financial sheets use £000 (stated in headings).  
- Column pattern (common across Annexes E and F): H = Original FFR forecast, I = Mid‑year revised outturn, J = Variance (I−H), K = Variance % (IF(H=0,"N/A",(J/H)*100)), L = Reason for material variance. Many J cells are simple I−H and many K cells handle H=0 with "N/A".
- Many totals computed with SUM ranges; many sequential line numbers in column B are generated by formulas like =previous+1.
- Several cross‑sheet links: Declaration - AnnexC!C3 (college name) is referenced on multiple sheets (annex e, f, g, h, h(1), i). Annex e is referenced by annex f and annex g.

Sheet-by-sheet summary
1) Declaration - AnnexC
- Title/cover: college name (C3), address/contact details, return deadline (H2) and a declaration text (A19).  
- Signature block (A26 onwards): signed, principal, date; basic text placeholders.

2) Commentary - Annex D
- Heading and instructions for financial commentary.  
- Cell A4 pulls college name: ='Declaration - AnnexC'!C3.

3) annex e — Income and Expenditure Account (Consolidated)
- Layout: rows for Income (Funding Council, Education Contracts, Tuition Fees, Research, Other income, Endowment income) then Expenditure (staff, restructuring, other operating, depreciation, interest), followed by surpluses, gains/losses on sale, tax, minority interest, historical cost adjustments, transfers and bursary/hardship lines.  
- Key formulas: totals via SUM (e.g. Total Income H15 = SUM(H9:H14); Total Expenditure H23 = SUM(H18:H22)), Surplus lines compute differences (H25 = H15−H23; later H31 = H25+H28−H29), Variance columns J = I−H, percent K as above.  
- Bursary/hardship section with over/(under)-use calculated as H56−H57 and H59−H60.  
- A4 references college name: ='Declaration - AnnexC'!C3.

4) annex f — Consolidated Balance Sheet
- Sections: Fixed assets, Endowment investments, Current assets, Creditors within one year, Net current assets, Creditors after one year, Provisions, Deferred capital grants, Endowments, Reserves, Total Funds.  
- Key calculations: Total Fixed Assets H13 = SUM(H7:H12); Total Current Assets H22 = SUM(H18:H21); Total Creditors (1yr) H32 = SUM(H25:H31); Net Current Assets H34 = H22−H32; Total Assets less Current Liabilities H36 = H13+H15+H34; Total Net Assets H48 = H36−H43−H46; Total Funds H68 = H55+H58+H66. Variance/percent columns follow same pattern.  
- Validation: H73 = H48−H68 (these cells "MUST BE ZERO").  
- Several cells are linked into annex g reconciliation formulas (e.g. annex g T26 references 'annex f'!H20+'annex f'!H21).

5) annex g — Operating Cash Flow (Consolidated)
- Two parts: ORIGINAL CASH FORECAST (columns H:S months + T total) and ACTUAL/REVISED FORECAST (same monthly layout).  
- Rows: income lines (SFEFC funding, other grants, other cash), total cash in; expenditure (payroll, other payments, interest), total cash out; cash on/off deposit; net cash inflow/outflow (monthly: =cash in − cash out + cash taken off/put on deposit).  
- Running balances: Balance b/f and Balance c/f computed to roll month‑to‑month (e.g. I18 = H19 etc; H19 = SUM(H17:H18)).  
- Reconciliation checks: per‑month H25 = H24−H19 etc (should be zero); T26 = T19 − ('annex f'!H20+'annex f'!H21) (reconciles to balance sheet).  
- Many monthly totals use SUM(Hx:Sx). Several cells are set to zero placeholders.

6) annex h and Annex H(1)
- Annex H: template to summarise impact of specific grant (FE/54/02) on I&E and Balance Sheet, with lines for income/expense and balance sheet impacts (I16, I21, I31 etc are zero placeholders). Uses college name link.  
- Annex H(1): completed example showing values (e.g. Total funding I17 = 90,000; Funding Council grants 60,000; Exceptional restructuring costs 40,000; Other operating expenses 20,000; Cash at bank 30,000; Deferred capital grants 30,000). Serves as an example for submission.

7) Annex I
- Statement of underlying operating surplus/deficit.  
- Pulls key numbers from annex e: H11 = 'annex e'!I25 (surplus after depreciation, revised outturn), H14 = 'annex e'!I19 (exceptional restructuring costs). Adds back items (SSAP 24 revaluation, other one‑offs), and totals underlying surplus H27 = SUM(H10:H26).

Cross‑sheet link map (high level)
- Declaration - AnnexC!C3 (college name) → annex e A4, annex f A4, annex g A4, annex h A6, annex h(1) A6, Annex I A2.
- annex e → Annex I (I25 used in I11), annex f/annex g referenced for reconciliations.
- annex f totals feed reconciliation checks in annex g and are used to compute Total Funds and validation rows.

Validation and checks to note
- Many "these cells must be zero" or reconciliation cells: annex f H73, annex g per‑month H25:H25 and T26, annex g T49 vs annex f I20+I21, annex g/annex f totals comparisons. Percent variances handle division by zero with "N/A".

Overall layout and purpose
- Workbook collects a mid‑year Financial Forecast Update: cover/declaration + narrative commentary, consolidated Income & Expenditure (annex e), Balance Sheet (annex f), Monthly cashflow (annex g), grant impact (annex h), and a statement of underlying operating surplus (Annex I).  
- Designed for comparison of original forecast vs revised outturn with automatic variance calculations and reconciliation checks; most numeric cells are initialized to zero placeholders and expect user input in columns H/I (or monthly H:S in annex g).
