Summary — workbook structure and contents (concise)

Sheets
- "2003-04 per credit hour", "Spring 2003 per credit hour", "Fall 2002 per credit hour".
- Each sheet is a tuition & fees table (TABLE VI-5, University of Florida) with the same layout and line items for different student categories.

Layout (common to all sheets)
- Header rows: A1–A3 titles; column headings in row 5–7.
- Two main rate presentations: Per Credit Hour (columns starting at B) and Per Term / Per Year for professional programs (column F and across F–H).
- Student categories (columns): Undergraduate (B), Graduate (C), Law (D), Medical (F), Dental (G), Veterinary (H).
- Row groups:
  - State Components: Matriculation, Building, Capital Improvement Trust Fund, Student Financial Aid (rows 10–13).
  - Subtotal State Components (row 14) — uses SUM of rows 10:13.
  - Local Fees: Activity & Service, Athletic, Health, Transportation Access (rows 16–19).
  - Subtotal Local Fees (row 20) — uses SUM of rows 16:19.
  - Total Florida Resident Rate/Credit Hour (row 21) — SUM(subtotal state, subtotal local).
  - Non-Resident lines (rows 23–24) and Total Non-Resident Rate (row 25) — SUM of resident total + non-resident lines.

Formulas
- Subtotals and totals use SUM ranges (examples shown: =SUM(B10:B13), =SUM(B16:B19), =SUM(B14,B20), =SUM(B21:B24)).
- Per-term columns mirror per-credit components but with larger absolute numbers (annual assessments for professional programs).
- Note anomaly: on "Spring 2003 per credit hour" sheet H14 uses COUNT(H10:H13) instead of SUM(H10:H13) — likely a mistake.
