   - Explanation: Ending inventory cost = units remaining * unit cost; COGS = goods available for sale cost - ending inventory cost.

   - Change `=-E47` to `=E47` (and similarly for others).
   - Explanation: These cells represent costs and should be positive values; negative sign causes incorrect COGS or inventory values.

### Explanation of solutions:

- Ending inventory should be calculated as goods available for sale minus sales (units sold). Since sales are recorded as negative units, adding them to goods available for sale gives the correct ending inventory units.
- The cost of ending inventory should be positive, reflecting the value of inventory left.
- Cost of Goods Sold is calculated as goods available for sale cost minus ending inventory cost.
- Negative signs in cost calculations cause incorrect totals and distort financial results.
