Summary of the problems I found (short):
1. Beginning‑cash row (row 6) references are inconsistent / shifted and sometimes use SUM or COUNT on a single cell. That breaks the month-to-month carry‑forward of cash.
2. Some Year‑2 formulas are mis‑placed (rows/columns shifted). Example: payroll/row formulas assigned to O22 when they belong in O20/O21 etc.

4. % of Sales / denominator inconsistencies and many formulas divide by zero risk (no IFERROR/guard).

6. Variance sheets: beginning cash and some references are wrong/shifted (same pattern as above) and variance formulas have no divide‑by‑zero protection.

Why these matter (short):
- If beginning cash for month N is not equal to month N‑1 ending cash, the cash flow rollforward is wrong and month‑end balances cascade incorrect numbers across the whole workbook.
- Misplaced row formulas mean totals (O columns) and % figures are computed on the wrong lines.
- COUNT instead of SUM (or direct reference) will return 0 or 1 and corrupt beginning cash values.
- Division by zero will produce #DIV/0! and wrong %s.
