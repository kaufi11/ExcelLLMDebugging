Findings (short)
- Error: Cell B20 (Directorate "would like to attend" total) uses =SUM(B12:B18) and omits row 19 (MPS).
- Consequence: B20 currently totals 54 instead of the correct 66. That makes the sheet conflict with the narrative in A8 ("We have 66 awardees interested...").

Fix (exact)
- Replace the formula in B20 with:
  =SUM(B12:B19)

Why this works (simple)
- The SUM range must include every row that contributes to the column total (rows 12–19). Adding B19 includes the 12 MPS responses and produces the correct total (66), matching the rest of the sheet/text.

Extra (optional, one-liner)
- To avoid this kind of off-by-one error later, convert the data area to an Excel Table and use the Table Totals row or use a dynamic formula (e.g., SUM on the whole column excluding header).
