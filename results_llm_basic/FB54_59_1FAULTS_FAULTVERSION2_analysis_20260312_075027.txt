Found one clear error:

- Cell Table 59!C15 (Alabama — Median Percent Receiving at Public Two-Year) contains the formula =-Data!L7. The leading minus sign negates the value from the Data sheet, so you get -77 instead of 77.

Why it's wrong:
- The Data sheet value (Data!L7 = 77) is already the correct percent. The minus sign turns it into a false negative value.

Fix:
- Replace the formula in Table 59!C15 with =Data!L7 (or =+Data!L7). After the change the cell will show 77.

How to do it quickly:
1. Go to Table 59, cell C15.
2. Edit the formula bar and change =-Data!L7 to =Data!L7, then press Enter.

Check for similar mistakes:
- Use Find (Ctrl+F) to search the workbook for "=-Data!" (or just "=-") to catch accidental leading minus signs in other links.

- You can remove the unnecessary leading plus signs (e.g., =+Data!L4) but they are harmless.
