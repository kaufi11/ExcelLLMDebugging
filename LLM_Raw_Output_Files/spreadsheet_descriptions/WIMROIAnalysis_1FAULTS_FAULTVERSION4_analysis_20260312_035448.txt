Summary — workbook structure and purpose
- Single main calculation sheet (Sheet1) with two unused/unspecified sheets (Sheet2, Sheet3).
- Purpose: a Return-On-Investment (ROI) / annual savings model for Auto‑ID/Warehouse improvements, broken into three application areas: Receiving, Putaway/Picking/Shipping, and Warehouse. Each area computes Min / Max / Avg / “Your Savings” scenarios.

Layout and conventions (Sheet1)
- Column A: section and line-item descriptions (benefits/activities).
- Column C: user input/data values (counts, hours, rates, ranges).
- Columns E (Min Savings), G (Max Savings), I (Avg Savings) and K (Your Savings) are the calculated results for each line.
- Typical formula pattern: Min = Cx * Cy * Cz * lower_percent; Max = same * upper_percent; Avg = (Min+Max)/2; Your Savings = same * user_selected_percent (often taken from a nearby C cell like C12, C23, etc.).
- Subtotals per application: Receiving (E42/G42/I42/K42), Putaway/Pick/Ship (E95/G95/I95/K95), Warehouse (E149/G149/I149/K149).
- Grand totals: E151/G151/I151/K151 (sums of the three application subtotals).
- Detailed summary tables later in the sheet re-map line-item results into a condensed SUMMARY OF ANNUAL SAVINGS and SUMMARY OF ALL RETURN-ON-INVESTMENT ANALYSIS.

Key inputs and constants (examples)
- Labor rates: Receiver rate = C11 = 22.49; many clerk/handler/shipper/etc. burdened rates = 17.39 (C22, C33, C40, C56, C62, C68, C76, C82, C88, C119, C125).
- Typical hours per person/year: 1920, 2080, 960, 950, 500, 450, 300, 250 (various C cells).
- Savings ranges: many percent ranges use 0.16 (16%) and 0.25 (25%); some ranges noted 2.1–4.0 days or $0.25/page.
- Capital and financials (bottom of sheet):
  - Hardware (B): C257 = 60,000
  - Software (C): C258 = 91,121
  - Depreciation in years (D): C259 = 3
- ROI / payback outputs:
  - Payback months = ((B+C)/A) * 12, where A = total annual savings (C255/E255/G255/I255 variants)
  - Return-on-Investment = (A * D) / (B + C)
  - Projected monthly cash generation = A / 12
