Summary
- Error found: On the In-Kind sheet cell F5 the formula is wrong. It is =E5*E5 instead of =D5*E5.
- Impact: F5 currently calculates 100*100 = 10,000 instead of 50*100 = 5,000, inflating the labor contribution by 5,000 and skewing totals.

What to fix (quick)
1. Select In-Kind sheet → cell F5.
2. Replace the formula =E5*E5 with =D5*E5 and press Enter.
3. Verify F5 now shows 5,000 and that Total Labor (F16), Running Total (G16/G29) and Grand Total update correctly.

Why this fixes it (simple)
- Column D is Hourly Rate and column E is Projected Hours. The contribution must be Hourly Rate * Hours (D*E). The current formula multiplies Hours by Hours (E*E), which is incorrect.

How to prevent/check similar mistakes
- Copy the correct formula down the column (fill handle) so all rows use D*E consistently.
- Use Excel’s Formula Auditing → Trace Precedents to confirm each F cell points to D and E.
- Optional quick error check formula (temporary): in a spare column enter =IF(F5<>D5*E5,"Check","OK") and copy down to spot mismatches.
