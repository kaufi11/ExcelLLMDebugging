3) Risk of #DIV/0! in every variance % formula (likely future error)
- Problem: Variance/percent change formulas like E8 = +(D8-C8)/C8 will return #DIV/0! when the base (C8) is zero or blank.
- Why it’s a problem: If any base month is zero you get errors that break summaries and charts.
- Fix: Wrap the division with an IF or IFERROR. Example:
  E8 = IF(C8=0, "", (D8-C8)/C8)
  or
  E8 = IFERROR((D8-C8)/C8,"")
- Explanation: This returns a blank (or 0) instead of an error and keeps reporting clean.

4) Unnecessary/fragile use of SUM() around single references and arithmetic
- Problem: Many formulas use SUM on single cells or simple arithmetic, e.g. SUM(B55), SUM(B15-B54), or +B23+B47.
- Why it’s a problem: Not a functional error in Excel (SUM will accept single values), but it hides mistakes and makes it harder to see intentional arithmetic vs. accidental ranges. It also makes auditing harder and increases chance of off‑by‑one mistakes when formulas are edited or filled.
- Fix / Best practice:
  - Replace SUM(B15-B54) with B15 - B54.
  - Replace SUM(B55) with B55.
  - Use consistent formulas and then fill down / right so references stay aligned.
- Explanation: Cleaner formulas are easier to audit and less likely to be accidentally mis‑rowed.
