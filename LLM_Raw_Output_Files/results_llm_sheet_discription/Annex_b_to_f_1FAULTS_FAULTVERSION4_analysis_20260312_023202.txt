I reviewed the workbook structure and formulas. I found 3 clear formula errors that will cause wrong % variances and wrong cash/financing totals. Below are short explanations and exact fixes.

1) Wrong percent-variance cell on Balance Sheet (Table 2.0 BS)
- Problem: Cell K69 (Total Funds % variance) contains =K49 (it just references the Total Net Assets % variance).
- Why it’s wrong: K69 should calculate the percent variance for Total Funds (J69/H69), not copy the percent for Net Assets.
- Fix: Replace K69 with:
  =IF(H69=0,"N/A",(J69/H69)*100)
- Result: K69 will correctly show percent variance of Total Funds (J69 vs H69) and reconciliation checks that use this value will be consistent.

2) Financing totals in ORIGINAL Cash Flow (Table 3.0 CF) use only July instead of summing all months
- Problem: T22 and T23 are set to =S22 and =S23 (July only) instead of totals across months H:S. That makes T24 (and the T column financing totals) wrong.
- Why it’s wrong: All other rows in the T (Total) column sum H:S; financing totals should do the same so the year total is correct.
- Fix (Original CASH FORECAST block):

    =SUM(H22:S22)
  - Replace T23 with:
    =SUM(H23:S23)
  - (T24 can remain =SUM(T22:T23) — it will now be correct)
- Result: T22/T23/T24 will show yearly totals (all months), giving a correct T25 reconciliation and correct total financing.
