Found one concrete error.

What’s wrong
- Cell H12 has the formula =SUM(H10:H11) but should sum H9 through H11. It omits H9 (Office imaging products) for 2001 2nd quarter.
- Because H12 (Business machines total) is wrong, every dependent calculation in column H is wrong (H15 Total net sales, H17 Net Sales reference, H19 Gross profit, H22 Operating profit, H29 Income before taxes, H33/H35 Net income, H20/H23/H30/H36 (%) etc).

Why it matters (numbers)
- Correct sum for column H (H9+H10+H11) = 247,326 + 272,577 + 49,971 = 569,874.
- Current value (H10+H11) = 322,548 — missing 247,326, so totals and margins in that quarter are understated by that amount.

Fix (easy)
1. Edit H12 and replace =SUM(H10:H11) with =SUM(H9:H11).
   - Or copy the correct formula from C12 (=SUM(C9:C11)) and paste/adjust into H12 so it points to H9:H11.
2. Recalculate (Excel does this automatically). Verify H15, H17, H19, H22, H29, H33, H35, and all % rows in column H update.

How to prevent
- Use consistent fill/copy across the row instead of typing ranges manually.
- Use the auditing tools (Trace Precedents/Dependents) or conditional checks (e.g., compare Business machines total across columns to the segment totals sheet) to catch mismatched ranges.
