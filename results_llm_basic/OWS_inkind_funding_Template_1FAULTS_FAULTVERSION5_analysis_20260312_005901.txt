Summary — the error, its effect, and the fix

1) Error found
- Sheet: "In-kind contribution template" — Cell F5 contains the wrong formula:
  - Current: =E5*E5
  - Should be: =D5*E5

2) Why it’s wrong
- F5 should calculate Hourly Rate * Projected Labor Hours (D5 × E5). =E5*E5 multiplies hours by hours (100×100 = 10,000) instead of rate×hours (50×100 = 5,000). That overstates the line item and inflates all dependent totals.

3) Effect on totals
- With the bad formula, the Grand Total (F43) in the In-kind sheet is 88,600 (inflated by 5,000).
- After fixing F5 to =D5*E5 the correct Grand Total becomes 83,600.

4) Fix (quick)
- Edit cell F5 on the "In-kind contribution template" and change the formula to:
  =D5*E5
- Or copy the correct formula from F6 and paste to F5.

5) How to verify
- Check F5 value becomes 5,000 (not 10,000).
- Check F16, G29, F41 and F43 update to the corrected totals (final grand total = 83,600).
- Use Excel’s Trace Precedents/Dependents or Error Checking to find similar inconsistencies.

6) Prevention tip
- Keep consistent formulas in matching rows across templates (copy/paste formulas rather than retyping).
- Use named ranges or structured tables so formulas are easier to audit.
