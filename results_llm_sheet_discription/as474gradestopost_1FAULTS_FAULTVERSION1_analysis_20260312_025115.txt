Findings (short)
- Error: F27 reads =AVERAGE(F7:F25) while every other column-average uses rows 7:26. That excludes the last student (row 26) from the Ex 5 average.
- Effect: Ex 5 average is wrong (slightly low) and any reports that use that cell are off.

Fix (one-line)
- Change F27 to =AVERAGE(F7:F26).

How to fix (step-by-step)
1. Select cell F27.  
2. Edit the formula and replace F7:F25 with F7:F26 (or type =AVERAGE(F7:F26)) and press Enter.

Why this fixes it (simple)
- The AVERAGE range must include all student rows. Including F26 adds the missing student's score into the calculation and gives the true average for Ex 5.

Extra checks / tips
- Scan B27:J27 to ensure all column-average ranges use the same rows (7:26).  
- Consider converting the student range to an Excel Table (Insert → Table). Then averages can use structured references and automatically expand when you add/remove students.  
- If scores >100 are unintentional, confirm whether they are extra credit; otherwise cap or correct them.
