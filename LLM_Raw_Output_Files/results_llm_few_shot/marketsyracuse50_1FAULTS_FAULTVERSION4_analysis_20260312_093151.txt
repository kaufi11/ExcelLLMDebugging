Summary — main error
- Faulty cell: F25 (Beacon Federal).
- Error: F25 uses the wrong multiplier (1.21674489270828*$E25). Beacon is a "Thrift" and should be counted at 50% of deposits (0.5), like the other thrifts. That inflated F25, so F36, every market share (G), and the HHI (H) are wrong.

Quick fix (one cell)
- Change F25 to:
  =0.5*$E25

Better, consistent solution (recommended)
- Replace the F formulas for all institutions with a single rule so thrifts and banks are handled automatically. Put this in F10 and copy down to F33:
  =IF($C10="Thrift",$E10*0.5,$E10)
  (This prevents accidental manual multipliers in future.)

Market share & HHI (clean formulas)
- Market share (G10 and down): use a decimal and format as %:
  =F10/$F$36
- HHI component (H10 and down): compute HHI directly (HHI uses squared percent shares). Use:
  =(F10/$F$36)^2*10000
  and sum H10:H33 in H36.

Why this works (short)
- F should reflect deposit ownership rules: banks = 100% of E, thrifts = 50% of E. The bad multiplier changed that for Beacon.
- G as F10/F36 gives correct market share; formatting as percent avoids multiplying by 100 inside formulas.
- HHI is sum of squared percentage shares. (Using the formula above yields the conventional HHI value, e.g., 10,000 = monopoly.)

What to check after repair
- G36 should sum to 100% (or 1.00 if decimals).
- H36 will change to the corrected HHI.
- Scan column F for any other hard-coded multipliers and replace with the IF formula to avoid repeats.
