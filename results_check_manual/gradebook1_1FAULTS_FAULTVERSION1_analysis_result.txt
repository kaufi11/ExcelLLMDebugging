I reviewed the formulas and values you supplied. There are no #VALUE or #REF errors, but there is a clear logical/formula inconsistency that will produce misleading "grades". Summary, the problem, and recommended fixes are below.

What I found
- Row 7 is labelled "Total Points Per Assignment" and contains the per-assignment maximums (D7=25, E7=25, G7=20, H7=50, J7=75). Cells F7, I7 and K7 are blank (no maxima entered for Quiz 3, Homework 3 and Final).
- L7 currently contains the formula =COUNT(D7:K7) and returns 5. COUNT is counting how many numeric cells exist in D7:K7 (there are five non-blank numeric cells).
- Each student row uses Lx = SUM(Dx:Kx) to compute the student's total points (this is fine).
- Each student's Grade cell Mx uses the formula =Lx/L$7*1. Because L7 is 5, Mx is the student's total points divided by 5 (i.e. an average points-per-assignment), not the percentage of total possible points.

Why this is a problem
- Dividing a student's total points by the COUNT of assignment columns (5) yields an average points-per-assignment number that mixes assignments with different maximum points. That is not a standard percentage or grade — it inflates the appearance of a grade and is almost certainly not what was intended.
- If the intended result is a percent of total possible points, L7 should be the SUM of the per-assignment maximums (D7:K7), not the COUNT.
- F7, I7 and K7 being blank makes the totals-per-assignment row incomplete; the SUM of D7:K7 (if used) will include zeros for those blanks unless the correct maxima are entered.
