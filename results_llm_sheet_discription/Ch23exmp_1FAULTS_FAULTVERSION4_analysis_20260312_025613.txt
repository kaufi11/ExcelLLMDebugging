Summary — primary error
- On the "Formulas" sheet the August gross margin (cell C19) has a wrong formula: it is =-C17-C18 (which computes −Sales − COGS) instead of Sales − COGS. This flips gross margin sign and cascades wrong operating income, tax and net income for August on that sheet.

Why it’s wrong
- Gross margin should be Sales less Cost of Goods Sold. =-C17-C18 equals −(C17 + C18), not C17 − C18, so it produces an incorrect (and usually large negative) value.

Fix (quick)
- Replace the formula in C19 on the "Formulas" sheet with:
  =C17 - C18
  (or =+C17 - C18 to match your sheet style).
- After fixing C19, recalculate the sheet (Excel does this automatically) — downstream rows (C25, C26, C27, etc.) will then show correct values.

Other issues / recommendations (short)
- You have two nearly identical sheets ("Example" and "Formulas"). Keep one as the master and copy formulas from the master to avoid mismatches like the sign error.
- The layout uses different columns for detail vs totals (e.g., B21:D21 while totals are in C24/E24). That’s not an error per se but causes easy copy/paste mistakes. Consider aligning each period into one contiguous column (all August items in C, all September in E) or use named ranges to reduce errors.
- Optional: you round many intermediate values to the nearest 1,000. That’s fine for presentation, but rounding intermediate results can create small rounding drift. If you want exactness, avoid rounding until final presentation rows.
