1) Wrong function in Total Liab. & Equity (cell F74)
- Error: F74 = COUNT(F67:F73)
- Why it’s wrong: COUNT counts how many numeric cells exist; it does not add values. This returns a small integer (the count) instead of the total of the F67:F73 balances.
- Fix: Change F74 to =SUM(F67:F73)
- Result: F74 will show the correct total of liabilities & equity.

2) Sign error in many Consolidation asset formulas (cells F105:F117)
- Error pattern: F105, F106, F107, F108, F109, F110, F111, F112, F113, F114, F115, F116, F117 use =(Bxxx + Cxxx + Dxxx - Exxx)
  (the worksheet shows them as = (B105+C105+D105-E105) etc.)
- Why it’s wrong: Column D is the consolidation "Dr." entry column. In all comparable consolidated-row formulas elsewhere (for liabilities & equity) the D column is subtracted: F = B + C - D + E. In the asset rows the D term is added (+D) instead of subtracted. That reverses the effect of debits and will inflate (or deflate) asset balances incorrectly and cause the consolidation proof to fail.
- Fix: For each asset row in the consolidation section, change the formula so column D is subtracted. Example corrections:
  - F105: change =(B105+C105+D105-E105)  → =(B105+C105-D105+E105)
  - F106: change =(B106+C106+D106-E106)  → =(B106+C106-D106+E106)
  - Repeat similarly for F107 through F117.
- Result: Assets will correctly reflect the effect of Dr. and Cr. consolidation entries and the totals should reconcile with liabilities & equity.

Why these fixes fix the problem (simple)
- SUM adds the numbers you intend to total; COUNT only counts numeric cells.
- Consolidation works by adding/subtracting the original balances and the adjustment debits and credits. Using the wrong sign for debit adjustments flips the adjustment and gives wrong consolidated balances and unbalanced totals.
