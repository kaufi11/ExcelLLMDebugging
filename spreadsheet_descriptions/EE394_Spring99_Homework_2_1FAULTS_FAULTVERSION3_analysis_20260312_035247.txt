Distance sweep block (rows 4..54+)
- Columns C and D:
  - Cn = =-(ROW()-4)*$B$10  (gives negative z values stepping by -B10)
  - Dn = =-Cn  (positive distance d in km)
- Columns E and F: E = COS($B$15*D*1000), F = SIN($B$15*D*1000) — compute cos/sin(β·d) where d converted to meters.
- Columns G (Real(VS)) and H (Imag(VS)):
  - G = (B6*1000*E - B14*B24*F)/1000
  - H = B14*B23*F/1000
  (these compute the sending-end voltage phasor components as functions of distance)
- I/J: VS magnitude and angle:
  - I = SQRT(G^2 + H^2)
  - J = ATAN2(G,H)*180/PI()  (angle in degrees)
- K/L: Real(IS), Imag(IS):
  - K = B23*E
  - L = B6*1000/B14*F + B24*E
- M/N: IS magnitude and angle (SQRT and ATAN2)
- The same formulas (C..N) are repeated down the rows to compute VS/IS at each distance step (rows 4 through ~54).

Notes on patterns/intent
- Column B holds global inputs and computed line parameters (L, C, Z0, β, wavelength).
- The table to the right (C:N) is a distance sweep using B10 increments (50 km by default) to compute phasors (VS and IS) along the transmission line using cosine/sine of β·d and using the characteristic impedance and load/current phasors.
- Angles are expressed in degrees via ATAN2(...)*180/PI(). Magnitudes use SQRT sums of squares.
- Reflection coefficient and VSWR are computed from the A/B terminal phasors (B28..B34, B35).
