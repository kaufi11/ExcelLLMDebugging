Summary of workbook structure and contents

Sheets
- Financial Statements (primary sheet)
  - Organized into major sections: BALANCE SHEET (top), INCOME STATEMENT, CASH FLOW STATEMENT, and NOTES TO THE FINANCIAL STATEMENTS (detailed numbered notes and schedules).
  - Currency columns: 2002 in column C, 2001 in column E. Notes references in column B, occasional flags in G/H.
  - Common layout pattern: row labels in column A, note refs in B, amounts in C/E and subtotals computed with formulas.

  Key formulas and cross‑references (typical patterns)
  - Totals use SUM over contiguous ranges (e.g. C13 = SUM(C14:C15); C17 = SUM(C18:C21); C78 = SUM(C79:C91); C92 = SUM(C93:C98)).
  - Top-level balance checks: C23 (Total assets) = C13 + C17; C42 (Total equity & liabilities) = C36 + C34 + C28.
  - Income statement: subscriptions C54 = SUM(C55:C60); other operating income C61 = SUM(C62:C69); total revenue C74 = C61 + C54 + C71.
  - Expenditure: administration C78 and other operating costs C92 summed to C100; surplus C102 = C74 - C100.
  - Cash flow: operating cash C117 = SUM(C122:C125); investing C129 (net outflow) and financing C136 combine to C144 (net change) and C147 (closing cash).
  - Several formulas reference other rows and sheets (e.g. 5 Year review pulls values from 'Financial Statements').

  Notes and schedules
  - Detailed note sections 1–20 cover accounting policies, tangible assets (movements, depreciation, leased assets), listed investments, inventory, short‑term investments, accumulated/development/National Convention funds, lease commitments, guarantees, financial instruments, etc.
  - Tangible assets schedule: cost, accumulated depreciation and movement roll‑forwards with formulas (e.g. C234 = SUM(C235:C239); C241 = SUM(C242:C246); C248 = C234 - C241).
  - Breakdown schedules: attendance fees (detailed per person), other admin expenditures, seminars income/costs, audit fee computation.

- 5 Year review
  - Compact summary with 5 columns (2002, 2001, 2000, 1999, 1998) in C/E/G/I/K.
  - Pulls many figures from 'Financial Statements' via cross‑sheet references (e.g. C15 = 'Financial Statements'!C54).
  - Shows multi‑year income, expenditure, balance sheet aggregates, cash flow summary, ratios (e.g. capital & reserves to total assets, current assets to current liabilities), membership and student statistics.

- Audit Report
  - Static text sheet containing approval signatures and the auditor's opinion (RSM Betty & Dickson), scope and audit opinion dated 17 March 2003.

Overall notes on structure and formulas
- The workbook is a standard financial statements pack: balance sheet, P&L, cash flow, detailed notes and supporting schedules; many subtotal and total lines use SUM ranges or direct arithmetic (some formulas include leading + signs).
- Cross‑sheet links are used to populate the 5‑year summary from the main Financial Statements sheet.
- The model balances (assets = equity + liabilities) and cash flow reconcilations are implemented via the formulas shown above.
