Summary of the error
- The worksheets are showing an inventory error that changes inventory (an asset) but does not always adjust retained earnings for the prior-period effect. As a result Total Assets changes but Total Equity is not adjusted by the same amount, so Assets ≠ Liabilities + Equity.
- In short: an ending-inventory error affects Cost of Goods Sold and Net Income (and so Retained Earnings). If you correct inventory for a prior year but do not do a prior‑period adjustment to retained earnings (or restate the prior year), the balance sheet will not balance by exactly the inventory error amount.

Why this happens (simple)
- Ending inventory wrong in Year 1 → Year 1 COGS wrong → Year 1 Net Income wrong → Year 1 Retained Earnings wrong.
- Changing inventory in Year 2 or later without correcting retained earnings leaves one side of the balance sheet changed (assets) but not the other (equity). The difference equals the cumulative inventory error.

Where it appears in your files
- Sheet1 shows the problem (inventory changed but no prior‑period retained earnings adjustment), so the statement is out of balance.
- Sheet2 demonstrates the error and says the balance sheet is out by the error amount.
- Sheet3 shows the correct treatment: the prior-year error is moved into retained earnings (a prior‑period adjustment) so the balance sheet balances.

How to fix (two acceptable approaches)

1) Preferred: Restate the prior year (if you can)
- Correct the prior-year ending inventory cell (e.g., 2001 ending inventory cell).
- This will automatically recalc COGS, Gross Profit, Net Income for that year, and since retained earnings is linked to that Net Income, Equity will update and the balance sheet will balance.
- Practical Excel action: edit the Year‑1 ending inventory cell (B11 in your layout) to the corrected value. Verify C12, C13, C15 update and B33 (retained earnings) flows from C15.

2) If you cannot restate: make a prior‑period adjustment to retained earnings
- Compute error = corrected prior-year ending inventory − reported prior-year ending inventory.
- Add that error to beginning retained earnings of the first year you discover it (increase or decrease retained earnings depending on sign).
- Excel example (based on your Sheet3 approach): if Year 1 ending inventory was understated by 17,000, change Year 2 retained earnings formula to:
  D35 = E17 + 17000
  (or wherever Year‑2 retained earnings is stored; add the error amount).
- Alternatively make a separate line in Equity called "Prior period inventory adjustment" and set that cell to the error amount so Total Equity increases/decreases by the correction.

Short concrete checklist to fix your workbook now
1. Decide: Restate prior year (best) or do a prior-period adjustment.
2. If restating: update the prior-year ending inventory cell (e.g., B11) to the corrected number. Confirm the income statement and retained earnings update and the sheets balance.
3. If adjusting: compute error = corrected − reported. Insert that error into the next-year retained earnings (or a clearly labeled prior-period adjustment line) so Total Assets = Total Liabilities + Equity.
4. Recalc and confirm the difference disappears.
