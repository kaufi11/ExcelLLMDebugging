Findings — the error
- Cell H12 (Business machines total for 2001 2nd quarter) contains COUNT(H9:H11) instead of SUM(H9:H11).
- Consequence: H12 equals 3 (count of rows) not 569,874. That wrong value propagates to H15 (Total net sales), H17 (Net Sales), H19 (Gross profit), H22 (Operating profit), H29 (Income before taxes), H33/H35 (Income), and any %‑of‑sales in column H — so the entire 2001 Q2 column is incorrect.

Fix (very short)
1. Edit H12 and replace =COUNT(H9:H11) with =SUM(H9:H11).
2. Copy H12 and paste/formula-fill into any other Business machines total cells if needed (C12:R12) to ensure all use SUM.
3. Recalculate (F9) or press F9 to refresh; verify H15, H17, H19, H22, H29, H33, H35 and % rows update to expected values.

How to verify there are no more similar mistakes
- Use Find (Ctrl+F) for "COUNT(" in the workbook to catch accidental COUNTs used where SUM is expected.
- Or on the Business machines total row (row 12) visually inspect formulas C12:R12 — every cell should be =SUM(<three product rows>).
