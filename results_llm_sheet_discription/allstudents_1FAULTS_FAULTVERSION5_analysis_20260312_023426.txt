Findings
- Error in 1997 "All Grades" tested total (cell D74).
  - D74 currently =SUM(D68:D73) — it omits Grade 3 (D67).
  - E74 correctly =SUM(E67:E73) (includes Grade 3), so the tested total and the passing total are inconsistent.
- Consequence: F74 (All Grades percent for 1997) is wrong because it divides the passing sum by a too-small tested sum.

Numbers (to illustrate)
- Correct tested sum (D67:D73) = 1,770,615
- Current (wrong) tested sum (D68:D73) = 1,531,995
- Passing sum (E67:E73) = 1,217,643
- Wrong percent F74 = 1,217,643 / 1,531,995 ≈ 79.51%
- Correct percent F74 = 1,217,643 / 1,770,615 ≈ 68.79%

Solution (how to fix)
1. Edit cell D74 and change the formula to:
   =SUM(D67:D73)
2. Verify F74 is =E74/D74 (it already is) and will update automatically.
3. Quick check: scan other "All Grades" rows to ensure their SUM ranges include all grade rows (3,4,5,6,7,8,10).

Why this fixes it
- Including D67 makes the tested total and passing total cover the same set of grades. The percent must divide the sum of passing by the sum of tested for the same grade set; fixing the SUM range corrects the denominator and thus the percent.
