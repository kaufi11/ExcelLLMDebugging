I reviewed both worksheets and found a few clear formula errors that will cause wrong totals and % allocations. I’ll list each error, why it’s wrong, and the exact fix.

1) MAIN / root cause — total earnings summed from the wrong column
- Error: On Sheet "STEP 1 OF 2" cell E22 contains =SUM(E11:E20)
- Problem: the actual dollar amounts for jobs appear to be in column D, not column E. Summing E11:E20 returns zero or garbage and breaks every formula that uses that total.

2) Dependent cell on STEP 2 uses the wrong reference for total earnings

- Problem: F10 is supposed to compute saving% = H10 / H6, but it points to H8 (giving) so saving percent will equal giving percent.
- Fix: Change F10 to =H10/H6

- Error: On "STEP 1 OF 2" cell E33 =SUM(E29:E30)
- Problem: That sums only rows 29–30 but your giving area has three lines (29:31). Row 31 is omitted.
- Fix: Change E33 to =SUM(E29:E31)

5) Use of SUM with a plus sign (poor form)
- Examples: I33 =SUM(G32+I32) and M33 =SUM(K32+M32)
