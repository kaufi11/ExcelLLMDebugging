Overview: the workbook contains six main sheets for retail store operations — sales tracking, department balance, cashflow planning, maintained margin calculations, a maintained margin report, and a formulas/KPI cheat sheet. Most sheets combine labeled input cells (often highlighted blue) with summary rows and simple arithmetic formulas (SUM, division, percentage of total).

Sheet-by-sheet summary

1) Store Mgr Sales Worksheet
- Purpose: daily/monthly store sales log for manager review.
- Layout: Title in A1; month entry in A2; column headers in row 3:
  - B: Actual, C: Plan, D: Sales LY, E: Sales Prior, F–H: AvgTr (this year/plan/LY), I–K: IPT (this year/plan/LY), L–M: Conversion, N–O: Weather, P: Holidays, Q: Comments.
- Rows A4:A41 list days (repeated days for a full month). Intended inputs are daily values; no explicit formulas provided in the excerpt.

2) Balance of Business
- Purpose: departmental sales/margin/space analysis as % of totals.
- Layout: Departments/classes in A4 down; numeric columns B (Sales $), D (Margin $), F (Markdown $), H (GMROII), J (Space) and corresponding % to total columns C, E, G, I, K.
- Key formulas:
  - Totals in row 15: B15 = SUM(B5:B14), D15 = SUM(D5:D14), F15 = SUM(F5:F14), H15 = SUM(H5:H14), J15 = SUM(J5:J14).
  - Percent columns: C5 = B5/B$15, E5 = D5/D$15, G5 = F5/F$15, I5 = H5/H$15, K5 = J5/J$15 (same pattern for rows 6–14).
  - Variance column L: L5 = C5 - E5 (and same down the column).
- Note: Row 10 uses a hard-coded numerator (C10 = 189/B$15).

3) CashFlow (6-month cash flow budget planner)
- Purpose: 6-month cash-in / cash-out budgeting and month-by-month cash position.
- Layout: Six month pairs of Budget / Actual across columns C–N; sections: Cash In (sales/other), Cash Out (many expense categories B18:B34), Monthly Summary lines.
- Key formulas:
  - Line A totals (Line A = total cash received): C13 = SUM(C9:C12) … N13 similar.
  - Line B totals (Line B = total cash paid out): C35 = SUM(C18:C34) … N35 similar.
  - Cash flow surplus (Line C): C38 = C13 - C35 … N38 similar.
  - Cash available (Line E): C40 = C39 + C38 … N40 similar (uses prior month cash-on-hand C39).
  - End-of-month copies: C41 = C40 … N41 similar.
- Instructions in B42:B46 explain process (fill budgets, subtract, carry forward).

4) Maintained Margin Worksheet
- Purpose: compute maintained margin from net sales and inventory items.
- Layout & key values:
  - Example inputs in column C (C4 Net Sales 15000, C5 Mark Downs 1000, C6 Allowances 200, C7 Employee Discounts 300, C8 Shrink Reserve 150). Inventory to be accounted = C9 = 16650.
  - Cost % in C10 = 0.551; C11 (Cost of Goods Sold) = 9174 (derived).
- Key formulas:
  - D9 = SUM(D4:D8) (user column)
  - D11 = D9 * D10
  - D14 = D12 - D13 (maintained margin dollars)
  - D15 = D14 / D12 (maintained margin %)
- Note: instructions to fill blue areas.

5) Maintained Margin Report
- Purpose: expanded monthly maintained margin / inventory reporting in cost and selling columns.
- Layout: Beginning inventory, receipts, mark-ups, inventory to be accounted for (cost and selling columns), net sales, inventory reductions, COGS and maintained margin.
- Key formulas/examples:
  - D8 = SUM(D5:D7) and F8 = SUM(F5:F7) (inventory to be accounted for).
  - Net sales E16 = SUM(E14:E15). Inventory reductions total F25 = SUM(F19:F24).
  - Cost of goods sold calculated by applying (100% - markup%) to selling totals (example yields E30 = 9174).
  - Closing inventory: D39 = D36 - D37 ; F39 = F36 - F38.

6) Formulas (KPI cheat sheet)
- Purpose: common retail KPIs with example inputs and formulas.
- Items included (examples and formulas):
  - Average Transaction = Sales / Number of Transactions (D5 = C5 / C6).
  - Items per Ticket = Total Items Sold / Total Transactions (D8 = C8 / C9).
  - Conversion Rate = Total Transactions / Total Traffic (D11 = C11 / C12).
  - GMROII = Maintained Margin Dollars / Average Inventory at Cost (D14 = C14 / C15).
  - Sales per Sq Ft = Sales / Selling Area (D17 = C17 / C18).
  - Turnover = Sales / Average Inventory (D20 = C20 / C21).
  - Selling Cost ratio, Return on Net Assets, Quick Ratio (all shown as value/divisor formulas).

General notes
- Recurrent patterns: %-to-total columns reference the totals row using absolute row anchoring (e.g., B$15); many rows use simple SUM ranges and basic arithmetic.
- User inputs: several sheets explicitly instruct to "Fill in the blue areas" — those are the intended input cells (sales, transactions, inventory, expenses).
- Key totals to watch: Balance of Business totals in row 15 drive % calculations; CashFlow lines 13 (cash received), 35 (cash paid), 38 (surplus), and 40 (cash available) form the monthly cash-position flow; Maintained Margin sheets compute COGS and MM% used in profitability metrics.
