Summary of the error
- Cell F72 contains a hard-coded number instead of a cell reference. Its formula is:
  =(264-D72+E72)
  It should reference the opening retained earnings in column C (C72), not the literal number 264.

Why this is wrong
- F72 should calculate the "New" retained earnings as: Old retained earnings (C72) + Dr (D72) - Cr (E72). Using 264 fixes the opening balance to 264 regardless of C72, so retained earnings, Total Liabilities & Equity, and the proof of debits = credits will be wrong.

Fix (quick)
1. Select F72.
2. Replace the formula with:
   =C72-D72+E72
3. Press Enter and recalc.

What to check after fixing
- Verify totals: C118, D118, E118, F118 and C132..F132 update correctly.
- Verify the proof lines: D134 and E134 (and D76 / E76) should match.
- If you used absolute references elsewhere, you can use =($C$72)-D72+E72 instead — same result.

Advice to avoid similar mistakes
- Search formulas for hard-coded numbers (use Find in Formulas mode or look for numbers in formulas).
- Compare a few rows where the pattern is regular (assets vs. liabilities/equity) to confirm consistent references.
