Simple, safe fix (recommended)
1. Replace the whole Column A sequence with a non-cumulative formula that ignores blank rows. In A3 enter:
   =IF($B3="","",COUNTA($B$3:$B3))
2. Copy/fill that formula down the column for all used rows (A3:A40+).

Why this works (short)
- COUNTA($B$3:$B3) counts how many non-empty first-name cells are from the first data row to the current row, producing a stable sequential ID (1,2,3...) only when a row has data. 
- IF($B3="","",...) leaves A blank for rows with no first name (keeps header/subheader rows blank).
- This avoids cumulative errors like +821 and broken chains when rows are inserted/deleted. A40 will become a formula result (not a hard-coded 35) and will update automatically.

Quick alternative
- If you prefer simple row-based numbering and there will be no blank rows between records, use:
  =IF($B3="","",ROW()-2)
  (enter in A3 and fill down).
