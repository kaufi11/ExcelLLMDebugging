High-level summary (concise)

Workbook has 3 sheets: Annex1, Annex2, Annex3. Each is a structured statistical annex on Morocco’s financial system with year-by-year amounts and derived ratios. Common formula patterns are SUMs, simple arithmetic, and percentage calculations (amount/total) using absolute references.

Annex1 — "Morocco: Overview of the financial system, 1992/1998"
- Purpose / layout: Snapshot tables for the banking system, other financial institutions, contractual savings, capital market, money market, and government domestic debt. Columns present amounts for two benchmark years (typically 1998 and 1992 or 1996/1993 depending on block).
- Key blocks and cells:
  - BANKING SYSTEM: Totals in billions of dirhams (A6–G6): Total assets B6=301, (C6 185.6 for 1992), Total credits D6=167.6, Total deposits F6=183.3. Group shares calculated for Commercial banks (numerator 234.2) and Specialized banks (numerator 66.8) with formulas dividing by totals (e.g., B7 =234.2/B6).
  - Other financial institutions: Treasury network, National Savings deposits and consumer loan/leasing companies with some cells built as small arithmetic formulas (e.g., B13 =6.07+0.2).
  - CONTRACTUAL SAVINGS: Insurance technical reserves B21=36; Pension funds B22 computed as 84.73−B21; Total/GDP uses (B21+B22)/341.
  - CAPITAL MARKET: Market capitalization rows (B30=145.1 etc.), trading volume and ratios. Some formulas use combined denominators and absolute refs (e.g., trading-volume/market-cap uses ($D$38+$D$41)). A fixed divisor 23.2 is used for some market ratios.
  - MONEY MARKET and GOVT DOMESTIC DEBT: Domestic debt B52=131, percent held by banks B53 =60.9/B52, and domestic debt/GDP uses B52/341.
- Notable: Uses explicit GDP constants (350, 341, 242.9, 320.9, 249.2) in several ratio formulas. A few cells use odd constructions (e.g., C7 =73.3%+3.7% or G7 =0.903+0.048) rather than a single computed percentage.

Annex2 — "Financial assets of non-financial agents (1992–1998)"
- Purpose / layout: Year columns (1992,1993,…,1998) across B, D, F, H, J, L, N. Rows break down financial assets: Liquid assets (currency & demand deposits), Short-term investments (passbook savings, term deposits, short-term BTs, marketable securities, OPCVMs), Medium-term investments (BTs, 3–5 year national bonds, medium-term marketable securities), Institutional savings (pension funds, insurance), and Total.
- Key formulas / mechanics:
  - Each year has an Amount column and an adjacent (%) column that divides the item by that year’s Total (e.g., C11 = B11 / $B$32). Totals per year are computed in row 32 (B32 = B28 + B23 + B14 + B10).
  - Short- and medium-term subtotals frequently use SUM ranges (e.g., B14 = SUM(B17:B19)+B15).
  - OPCVMs, some entries are blank for early years; other years filled (e.g., J21=1915, L21=5765, N21=15744).
  - Memo rows provide total/GDP percentages (B34, D34, …). L34 and N34 use explicit denominators (318,346 and 341,386) to compute percent.
- Key values: Currency (B11=35,745), Demand deposits (B12=65,921), Passbook savings (B15=14,912), Total (row B32 is a computed sum).

Annex3 — "Assets, deposits, and credits by bank: 1992 to 1997"
- Purpose / layout: Three main sections laid out across pages: ASSETS (page 1), DEPOSITS (page 2), CREDITS (page 3). For each section the sheet lists banks grouped into Moroccan commercial banks (I), Foreign commercial banks (II), and Specialized banks (III). For each bank/year there is an Amount column and an adjacent (%) column (share of that year’s total).
- Structure / formulas:
  - Year columns: B (1992), D (1993), F (1994), H (1995), J (1996), L (1997). Amounts in these columns; percentage columns immediately to the right compute amount / yearly total (e.g., C13 = B13 / $B$38 or similar).
  - Group totals use SUM of member rows (e.g., B12 = SUM(B13:B24)). Overall totals combine the three groups (e.g., B38 = B33 + B26 + B12).
  - Similar pattern repeated for Deposits and Credits sections with their own totals (e.g., B77 for Deposits total, B116 for Credits total).
- Key values (examples):

  - CREDITS: Major lenders have large credits (e.g., B94 BMCE=9,637; B97 CPM=11,213; B113 CIH=15,202; B114 CNCA=12,655).
