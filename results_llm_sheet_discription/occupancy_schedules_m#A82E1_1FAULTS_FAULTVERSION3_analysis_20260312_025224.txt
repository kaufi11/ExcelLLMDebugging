2) Latent “per person” formula uses wrong cells (systemic) — Occ L0, Occ L1-*, Occ L2-* (cells labelled F10 / F11 etc)
- Problem: the latent per-person formula multiplies the latent gains by Occupancy!F19 and F20 and divides by F19+F20 (those are sensible-load numbers), e.g.
  =(Occupancy!F22*Occupancy!F19 + Occupancy!F23*Occupancy!F20) / (Occupancy!F19+Occupancy!F20)
  This weights latent gains by sensible-load values (wrong) instead of by the schedule sums (what the sensible-per-person formula does).
- Impact: latent BTU/person/hr is calculated incorrectly (biased by the sensible values), so total latent gains will be wrong wherever the per-person latent value is used.
- Fix: use the same weighting method as sensible gains — weight by the living/bedroom schedule sums (Occupancy!F38 and F39). Replace with:
  =(Occupancy!$F$22*Occupancy!$F$38 + Occupancy!$F$23*Occupancy!$F$39) / (Occupancy!$F$38 + Occupancy!$F$39)
- Where to change: every sheet that has the current incorrect latent formula (Occ L0 F10, Occ L1-WD F10, Occ L1-DU F10, Occ L2-* F11, etc.). Use absolute refs ($) if you copy.

3) Inconsistent/hard-coded day-fraction denominator (Occupancy!G28:G31)
- Problem: G28..G31 are =F28/365 etc but F28..F31 sum is used elsewhere (and year 2000 is a leap year). Other derived fractions (F33..F36) use SUM(F28:F31).
- Impact: inconsistent fractions and potential error if day counts change (and if you really simulate year 2000 you should use 366 or the sum).
- Fix: change G28..G31 to divide by the actual total (robust):
  =F28/SUM($F$28:$F$31)  (and same pattern for F29..F31)
  OR if you want to force year 2000 explicitly, divide by 366.
- Why: using SUM keeps the fractions consistent with the other formulas and resilient to edits.
